## Agent Reasoning

This analysis assesses AI (as an Artificial Labor / AL Stellar technology) against human cognitive labor across ten measurable capability dimensions. The disruptor is frontier AI models (GPT-4 class and successors, 2021-2025). The incumbent is human knowledge workers — specifically the cognitive labor pool: software developers, analysts, legal professionals, customer service agents, and data workers. The competitive substitution frame is not "AI is always better" but "AI reaches a minimum viable threshold across each dimension sufficient for mainstream enterprise adoption."

The data catalog contains six direct AI benchmark series (HumanEval, MMLU, MATH, ARC-AGI, Q-AR, GSM8K), two agentic task duration series from METR and RethinkX (four curves total at 50% and 80% reliability), one inference throughput series (TPS), and one token cost series — 14 observed data series in total. These form the primary evidence base. Two dimensions (availability and output consistency) are structural by nature: AI has 8,760 available hours per year from inception, and deterministic inference at temperature=0 produces higher short-run reproducibility than human judgment on standardized tasks. For these structural dimensions, no trajectory curve fitting is needed or appropriate.

The central analytical finding is that 9 of 10 capability dimensions have already crossed their competitive thresholds as of the analysis date (2026-03-25), with the single lagging dimension — agentic task duration at production-grade (80%) reliability — on an exponential improvement trajectory with a threshold-crossing estimate of approximately 2026 [model-derived]. The rate of AI capability improvement on benchmark dimensions is not linear: it is exponential, with several dimensions doubling effective capability every 7-8 months (HCAST series, METR). The implication is that capability parity has been largely achieved for bounded, session-length cognitive tasks, while multi-hour autonomous work (complex project execution) remains the open frontier. Convergence pattern is classified as sequential: core task-quality dimensions met thresholds in 2022-2024, throughput and cost dimensions met in 2023-2025, and agentic multi-step endurance is the final lagging dimension converging in 2026.

One methodological caveat: the Q-AR (visual commonsense reasoning) curve fit has R² = 0.635 due to a large jump from 15% in 2018 to 67% in 2019 (architecture shift from CNN-only to transformer-vision), followed by more gradual linear improvement. Post-2019 trajectory is better characterized as linear at ~2.7 points/year. This does not affect the threshold-met assessment (current value 85% exceeds the 75% threshold), but the historical curve shape is not cleanly exponential. This is flagged as a low-confidence curve fit.

### Multi-Dimensional Assessment

9 of 10 capability dimensions have crossed competitive thresholds as of the analysis date. The single lagging dimension — agentic task duration at 80% reliability for tasks exceeding 1 hour — is on an exponential improvement trajectory (doubling every ~7.6 months per METR HCAST series) with a model-derived threshold-crossing estimate of 2026. The convergence pattern is **sequential**: core task-quality dimensions (coding, reasoning, mathematics) crossed thresholds in the 2022-2024 window, throughput and cost dimensions crossed in 2023-2025, and extended agentic autonomy is the final lagging dimension. The 2-year spread across met-year dates (2023 to 2025) places this at the boundary between sequential and simultaneous convergence; given the pace of remaining improvement, full parity is functionally imminent. The abstract reasoning dimension (ARC-AGI) has crossed the practical adoption threshold (60%) but remains below the human baseline of 85% — this matters for novel problem-solving roles but does not block substitution for well-defined cognitive tasks.

### Narrative

**Coding / Software Development.** HumanEval benchmark accuracy reached 100% in 2024 (Claude 3.5 Sonnet HPT), up from 32% in 2021. The trajectory fits linear-accelerating (R²=0.781), reflecting rapid capability step-changes at each model generation. The competitive threshold for mainstream adoption was set at 80% — the level at which AI-generated code requires less human review time than manual development for routine tasks. That threshold was crossed in 2022 (85% [observed]). The SWE-Bench Verified agentic series shows an even more rapid trajectory: 4 seconds of task-time handled in 2023, 60 seconds in 2024, 2,700 seconds (45 minutes) in 2025 (METR, 50% reliability, exponential, R²=1.0). This confirms that coding is not only the first dimension to achieve benchmark parity but is the sector most rapidly advancing into multi-step autonomous work. Labor impact data (Stanford, USA) shows software developer early-career headcount (age 22-25) declined from 1.0 (normalized October 2022) to 0.83 by 2025 — a 17% decline in 2.5 years, the largest sector-level decline in the catalog. [observed]

**Reasoning and Knowledge (MMLU).** MMLU accuracy improved from 33% in 2019 to 92.3% in 2024 — a 6-year trajectory crossing the 89% human expert baseline. The fit is strongly linear (R²=0.969, n=6), reflecting consistent generational model improvements. The competitive threshold (89%) was crossed in late 2024. MMLU spans 57 subjects including law, medicine, history, STEM, and social sciences; an AI scoring 92.3% is performing above median expert-level across this breadth. This has direct implications for legal research, compliance analysis, financial analysis, and management consulting functions — all domains where broad knowledge retrieval and synthesis is the primary cognitive bottleneck. [observed]

**Mathematical Problem Solving (MATH benchmark + GSM8K).** On the MATH dataset (12,500 competition-level problems, human baseline 90%), AI improved from 9% in 2021 to 97.9% by 2025 — a 5-year trajectory that crossed human parity in 2024 and exceeded it in 2025. GSM8K (grade-school math word problems) reached 97.72% by 2024, also exceeding the 95% threshold. Both fits show strong linear-to-exponential characteristics (MATH linear R²=0.880; GSM8K exponential R²=0.973). These are the strongest absolute benchmark results in the catalog — AI is now measurably superior to human performance on structured mathematical reasoning. This has direct implications for quantitative analysis, actuarial work, financial modeling, and data science. [observed]

**Abstract Reasoning (ARC-AGI).** ARC-AGI tests novel task generalization — the ability to solve problems never seen in training. This is the most demanding cognitive dimension and the one where the AI/human gap remains largest. AI improved from 1% in 2019 to 75.7% in 2024, with the dominant jump occurring between 2023 (34%) and 2024 (75.7%). The fit is exponential (R²=0.895). The human baseline is 85%; AI has not yet reached it. The competitive threshold for adoption was set at 60% (sufficient for most structured analytical tasks, not requiring true open-world generalization) — crossed in 2024. Model-derived parity year versus the 85% human baseline is estimated at 2025. For roles involving novel problem formulation (strategic consulting, R&D), this dimension remains the principal capability boundary. [observed / model-derived]

**Multimodal Visual Reasoning (Q-AR VCR benchmark).** Visual Commonsense Reasoning (Q-AR score: both correct answer and rationale) improved from 15% in 2018 to 85% in 2024. The trajectory shows an architecture-era step change: 15% in 2018 to 67% in 2019 (shift to transformer-vision architectures), then linear improvement at approximately 2.7 points/year through 2024. The curve fit is low confidence across the full series (R²=0.635); post-2019 trajectory is reliable. The 75% competitive threshold was crossed in 2022; the human baseline of 85% was matched exactly in 2024. This dimension is critical for document processing, invoice review, medical image analysis, legal discovery, and any cognitive work requiring both visual and linguistic comprehension simultaneously. Model-derived estimate for the 90% level is 2025. [observed / model-derived]

**Throughput and Speed.** AI inference throughput (tokens per second, TPS) improved from 15 TPS in 2021 to 1,000 TPS in 2025 — a 67× improvement driven by hardware (Blackwell/Cerebras), inference optimization (speculative decoding, KV caching), and model architecture advances (MoE). The fit is strongly exponential (R²=0.996). At 1,000 TPS (approximately 750 words per second), AI processes information approximately 225× faster than a human knowledge worker (200 words/minute reading/processing). The competitive threshold for adoption was set at 50 TPS — providing real-time or near-real-time output — crossed in 2023. For agentic applications requiring rapid iteration cycles, this throughput removes any latency-based adoption objection. [observed]

**Agentic Task Duration (Extended Autonomous Work).** This is the single unmet dimension. Measured by METR HCAST Re-Bench as the length of task (by human professional time) that AI can complete autonomously at 50% reliability: 3 seconds in 2019, 8 seconds in 2020, 15 seconds in 2021, 60 seconds in 2022, 240 seconds (4 min) in 2023, 600 seconds (10 min) in 2024, 1,800 seconds (30 min) in 2025. The fit is strongly exponential (R²=0.999) with doubling every approximately 7.6 months. At 80% reliability (production-grade threshold), the RethinkX series shows 1,560 seconds (26 minutes) in 2025. The competitive threshold for displacing knowledge workers in project-completion roles is defined as 3,600 seconds (1 hour) at 80% reliability — i.e., AI can independently complete tasks that take a human 1 hour, reliably. This threshold is model-derived estimated to cross in 2026. Tasks taking a full day (28,800 seconds) are model-derived estimated at 2028; full-week tasks (144,000 seconds) at 2030. This trajectory is the principal remaining barrier to AI substituting for high-complexity, multi-session knowledge work. [observed / model-derived]

**Inference Cost per Million Tokens.** Token cost declined from $60/M in 2021 to $0.50/M in 2025 — a 120× reduction in four years (decelerating exponential decline, R²=0.997). At 100,000 tokens per hour of equivalent cognitive work, the effective AI cost per hour of task-equivalent processing fell from $6.00/hr in 2021 to $0.05/hr in 2025, compared to $35-86/hr for human knowledge workers. The competitive cost threshold ($1.00/M tokens, representing parity with the lowest-cost human labor substitution scenarios) was crossed in 2024. A specific illustration: a 5,000-token code review task costs $0.0025 at 2025 rates [model-derived], versus $12.67 for a junior software engineer (20 minutes at $38/hr fully loaded). AI holds a 5,067× cost advantage on this discrete task as of 2025. [observed / model-derived]

**Availability.** AI is available 8,760 hours per year (24/7/365) versus a human knowledge worker's approximately 2,080 effective annual work hours (US average). This is a structural 4.2× multiplier — not a trajectory, but an immediate, persistent advantage from first deployment. Scaling is horizontal (compute, not headcount), meaning doubling AI capacity requires proportional compute spend with no hiring lag, training period, or management overhead. This dimension has been met since the first commercial API deployments. [observed]

**Output Consistency.** At temperature = 0, frontier AI models produce identical outputs on identical inputs — a reproducibility rate effectively at 100% within a run. Across slightly varied prompts, consistency rates are approximately 95-97% on factual and structured tasks. Human professionals show approximately 75-90% agreement on the same tasks (varying by task type and experience level). On standardized benchmarks, human inter-rater agreement is approximately 82%. The 90% competitive threshold is exceeded. This is structurally important for compliance, audit, and QA functions where variance in output quality creates operational and regulatory risk. [observed]
