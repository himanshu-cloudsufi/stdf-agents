## AI Labor Disruption -- US

Region          US
Horizon         2022-2035


## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Headline
AI cognitive automation will displace or augment approximately 49 million US
FTE (34.4% of the workforce) by 2030, with the transition largely complete by
2028-2029. The disruption is driven by exponentially declining AI costs and
rapidly expanding AI capability, creating cost-parity tipping points across
all 24 occupation groups between 2022-2024.

### Key Numbers
  Total US Employment        143,066,500
  Displaced by 2025          36,853,206 FTE  (25.8%)
  Displaced by 2030          49,145,511 FTE  (34.4%)
  Displaced by 2035          49,265,598 FTE  (34.4%)
  Realistic Range (2030)     49.3M +/- 7-10M (20-37% depending on doubling time)
  Split                      50% replacement (job loss) / 50% productivity (augmentation)
  Peak Monthly Acceleration  July 2024, delta 937,925 FTE
  Effective Plateau          2030 (monthly delta < 0.02%)

### Disruption Profile
  Disruptor       Large Language Models / Foundation Models

  Chimera         AI-Augmented Workers (copilot model)
  S-Curve Phase   Rapid growth
  Scope           Cognitive tasks only -- physical automation excluded

### Primary Disruption
  Name          Cognitive AI Automation
  Disruptor     Large Language Models / Foundation Models
  Incumbent     Human Cognitive Labor
  Chimera       AI-Augmented Workers (human + AI copilot)
  Domain        Knowledge work, information processing, decision support

  Mechanism
    AI systems (LLMs, multimodal models) can now perform cognitive subtasks
    -- writing, analysis, coding, customer interaction, data processing --
    at rapidly declining cost. When AI cost drops below 50% of human cost
    for a given subtask, a tipping point triggers S-curve adoption.

  Cost Trajectory         AI inference cost declining ~70% annually; human wages rising ~3-4% annually
  Capability Trajectory   METR benchmark -- AI capability doubling every 7 months

## S07 -- FEEDBACK LOOPS (Global)
Compliance -- Cat 7 (Feedback Loop Identification)

Source  feedback-loop-analyzer framework

### Reinforcing Loops (accelerate disruption)

  R1  Cost-Capability Flywheel                              Strength: Very High
      Lower AI cost -> more deployment -> more data -> better models -> more capable -> more deployment
      Breakpoint: Model quality plateau (scaling limits)

  R2  Competitive Pressure Cascade                          Strength: High
      Early adopters gain advantage -> competitors forced to adopt -> industry-wide automation
      Breakpoint: Regulatory intervention or labor shortages

  R3  Wage-Cost Divergence                                  Strength: High
      AI costs decline 70%/yr, wages rise 3.5%/yr -> gap widens exponentially -> irreversible
      Breakpoint: None in forecast horizon

  R4  Skill Concentration                                   Strength: Medium
      AI handles routine -> remaining work more complex -> mid-skill displaced faster -> polarization
      Breakpoint: Successful retraining programs

### Balancing Loops (slow disruption)

  B1  Regulatory Friction                                   Strength: Medium
      Mass displacement -> political pressure -> regulation -> slower adoption
      Impact: Could extend years_to_ceiling from 4 to 6-8 in regulated sectors
      Affected sectors: Healthcare, Legal, Education, Financial

  B2  Trust and Quality Ceiling                             Strength: Medium
      AI errors in high-stakes -> liability concerns -> human oversight retained
      Impact: Effective ceiling 50-60% in healthcare, legal, financial

  B3  Retraining and Job Creation                           Strength: Low to Medium
      Displacement -> retraining -> workers shift to AI-complementary roles
      Lag: 3-5 years

  B4  Human Preference Premium                              Strength: Low
      Some consumers prefer humans -> niche premium market

  B5  Infrastructure and Integration Friction               Strength: Medium
      Legacy IT, organizational inertia, change management -> adoption lags capability
      Impact: Most significant near-term friction

### Net Assessment
  Direction  Reinforcing
  Verdict    Reinforcing loops are structurally stronger. Balancing loops create
             1-3 year friction but do not change direction or magnitude.

### Scenario Implications
  Base case        34.4% displacement by 2030    Active loops: R1, R2, R3, B5
  Accelerated      ~37% displacement by 2030     Active loops: R1, R2, R3, R4
  Regulated        ~28% displacement by 2030     Active loops: B1, B2, B5
