Replacement guidance (what to say and why)
- "Transition" -> "transformation" or "disruption"
  - Reason: "transition" implies slow, managed change within incumbents; "transformation" signals rapid system replacement.
- "Evolution" -> "disruption" or "phase change"
  - Reason: "evolution" implies gradual improvement; Seba framing is rupture after tipping points.
- "Incremental improvement/optimization" -> "systemic change" or "cost/capability advantage"
  - Reason: emphasizes whole-system economics and resets rather than marginal tweaks.
- "Renewable/alternative/green/sustainable" -> "stellar energy", "superabundance", "zero marginal cost energy"
  - Reason: scarcity-anchored framing underplays cost-collapse and abundance.
- "Net zero/carbon neutral" -> "emissions collapse via system replacement"
  - Reason: offsets suggest compromise and delay; Seba framing is replacement-driven elimination.
- "Grid parity" -> "cost advantage" or "cost dominance"
  - Reason: parity assumes incumbents remain baseline; Seba framing is disruptor dominance.
- "Peak oil" -> "oil demand collapse"
  - Reason: demand collapse is driven by technology economics, not resource depletion.
- "Hydrogen economy" -> "SWB + electrification convergence"
  - Reason: hydrogen framing implies a parallel system rather than the dominant electrified stack.
- "Baseload power" -> "always-available distributed capacity"
  - Reason: baseload reflects centralized legacy thinking; distributed systems replace it.
- "Smart grid" -> "distributed energy architecture"
  - Reason: smart grid modernizes incumbents; Seba framing is decentralized architecture.
- "Intermittency" -> "variability managed by overbuild + storage + orchestration"
  - Reason: avoids overstating constraints; emphasizes system-level design.
- "Policy-driven transition/subsidized renewables" -> "market-driven disruption"
  - Reason: economics and learning curves drive adoption.
- "Energy storage" (standalone) -> "SWB system" or "storage as part of SWB stack"
