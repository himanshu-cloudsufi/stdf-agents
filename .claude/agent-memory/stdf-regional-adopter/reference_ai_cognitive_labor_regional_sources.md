## Source Quality by Region (for AI cognitive labor substitution)

### Tier 1 (Highest reliability)
- **Eurostat enterprise AI adoption**: ec.europa.eu/eurostat -- 20.0% EU enterprises 2025, 13.5% 2024, 8.1% 2023, 7.7% 2021. Best EU time series available. Annual cadence. Covers enterprises 10+ employees. Limitation: measures any AI use, not cognitive task substitution specifically.

### Tier 3 (Web, historical, observed -- but usable)
- **Microsoft Work Trend Index** (annual, ~May): Best source for per-country knowledge worker AI usage rates. Covers 31 markets, ~31,000 respondents. India (92%), USA (56%), global (75%) are the key 2024 numbers. Surveys: augmentation, not substitution.
- **IBM Institute for Business Value** (annual): Country-level enterprise AI deployment. China 58%, India 57% are the 2024 anchors. Measures enterprise deployment, not knowledge-worker substitution.
- **Stanford AI Index** (annual): Best source for national AI investment ($), model production counts. USA $109B, China $9.3B, UK $4.5B for 2024.
- **McKinsey State of AI** (annual, ~May or Nov): Global enterprise adoption (78%-88%) but minimal country breakdown. Best for enterprise depth (% achieving EBIT impact: 17%-39%).
- **allaboutai.com/visualcapitalist AI adoption by country**: Aggregated from multiple surveys. UAE 64%, Singapore 60.9%, South Korea strong growth. Use for directional RoW signals only.

## Data Gaps (persistent)
1. No primary-source per-region COGNITIVE TASK SUBSTITUTION time series exists for any region. All data is enterprise tool adoption or knowledge-worker usage surveys -- measuring augmentation/co-pilot, not substitution.
2. China has no Eurostat equivalent. IBM enterprise data is the best available but is survey-based.
3. India: Microsoft WTI 92% measures tool usage (very high), not substitution depth. Ghost Research BPO automation data (40-50% RPA) is qualitative.
4. RoW is too heterogeneous. UAE/Singapore lead; Sub-Saharan Africa/South Asia ex-India are sparse.
5. The multiplier methodology (enterprise adoption × regional multiplier = substitution share) is an inference bridge, not a direct measure. The multiplier is calibrated to the global 9.0% anchor but regionally unvalidated.

## Note on EU AI Act timeline
- Feb 2025: AI literacy requirements effective
- Aug 2025: GPAI model rules (documentation, training transparency)
- Aug 2026: Full applicability incl. transparency duties (HIGH IMPACT on EU adoption)
- Aug 2027: High-risk systems in regulated products
The full EU employment-related provisions do not apply until Aug 2026. Post-analysis-date headwind for EU adoption not yet captured in 2025 data.
