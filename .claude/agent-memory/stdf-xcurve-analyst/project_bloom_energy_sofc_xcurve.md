Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE) is a structurally unusual incumbent: the AI data center demand shock of 2024-2025 created a counter-disruption growth phase that extends revenue growth 2-3 years past the nominal SWB tipping threshold (2025.6). Revenue grew $1.47B (2024) to $2.02B (2025, +37.3%).

**Why:** AI data center power demand (grid interconnection queues 3-7 years) created a new BTM on-site power TAM that expanded faster than SWB took share within the original enterprise C&I TAM. Bloom pivoted to AI infrastructure, locking in AEP (1 GW), Oracle, Equinix (100 MW), Brookfield ($5B).

**Revenue peak:** ~2027-2028 at $3.0-3.2B [model-derived]. Revenue decelerates below 10% YoY in 2028-2029 when: (1) 2 GW capacity fills; (2) AI buildout plateaus; (3) long-duration BESS (~8hr, ~$150/kWh) commercializes, enabling SWB to approach 24/7 data center reliability.

**Short entry signal:** Revenue growth deceleration below 10% YoY, observable in quarterly reports. Target entry 2028-2029. SWB at 14-17% share, LCOE gap closing on 2-3yr procurement horizon.

**Structural thesis:** Multiple compression trade -- 125x forward P/E (growth) to 15-20x P/E (declining services). Bloom becomes a $700-900M/yr service annuity business from ~2035 on stack replacement revenue. At 15-20x P/E, equity value is $11-15B vs. ~$41B current market cap (March 2026) = 63-73% downside.

**Volume breakeven:** ~303 MW/yr (model-derived). Bloom crosses below this ~2032. Fixed cost/MW inflation at breakeven vs. peak: +141% (from $0.82M/MW at ~730 MW peak to $1.98M/MW at 303 MW).

**Stack replacement tail:** ~5,500 MW peak installed base (~2028-2030). ~$8.2B total replacement pipeline. Annual replacement revenue peaks ~$750M/yr in 2038-2042. This is the "slow death spiral" -- masks product revenue collapse until ~2038.

**$2.2B convertible notes due 2030** create balance-sheet risk: notes mature precisely at the onset of the order collapse, creating potential liquidity crunch or forced equity issuance.
