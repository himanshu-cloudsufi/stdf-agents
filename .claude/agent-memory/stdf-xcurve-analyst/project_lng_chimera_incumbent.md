LNG trucks in China rose to ~29% new truck sales share by 2024 then entered market-driven disruption by BEV trucks in 2025. Key pattern: "chimera incumbent" — LNG displaced diesel before being displaced itself, compressing the entire LNG lifecycle into ~10 years.

**Why:** The BEV TCO advantage of −27% vs. LNG in 2024 (widening to −41.8% by 2030 per LFP learning rate 16.70%/yr) is the structural driver. LNG's advantage over diesel collapsed (2/3 spread erosion from 2024 peak to May 2025) as BEV simultaneously undercut LNG.

**How to apply:** When analyzing chimera incumbents (technologies that rose as disruptors but are now themselves being displaced), frame the stranded asset analysis as doubly compressed: both infrastructure built for the chimera AND the fleet residual values are at risk on a shorter-than-normal timeline. The incumbent decline spiral is faster than for traditional long-tenured incumbents.

Key data points (observed, 2025):
- LNG fleet: ~1 million trucks operating in China
- LNG refueling stations: ~6,000 (CNY 30–60B sunk capital)
- LNG domestic price Dec 2025: CNY 3,500/tonne (5-yr low, down ~26% from normal CNY 4,500–5,000)
- LNG imports: down 13 consecutive months through Nov 2025; 2025 total ~64.6 Mt vs. 78.27 Mt in 2024
- LNG market share: peaked ~29–30% new sales 2024; fell to ~26% H1 2025 despite March 2025 subsidy support
- Westport/Weichai LNG engine orders: zero in Q1 2024 — key investment drought signal
- Top 5 LNG OEMs = Top 7 BEV producers (>50% electric truck sales) — OEM defection confirmed
