## AI Cognitive Labor X-Curve -- March 2026 Run

**The incumbent is human cognitive labor itself, not a company or industry.** This requires reframing all five market trauma mechanisms into labor-market analogs.

### S-curve parameters (from upstream)
- L=88%, k=0.5903, x0=2028.6
- Current stage: Early volume loss (incumbent ~82.4% at March 2026 [model-derived])
- Peak decline rate: 13.0pp/yr at inflection 2028.6 [model-derived]

### Observed evidence (highest quality)
- Stanford/ADP "Canaries in the Coal Mine" (Brynjolfsson et al., August 2025 [T1]): -20% software devs aged 22-25, -13% customer service young workers from 2022 peak
- Dallas Fed (February 2026 [T1]): posted knowledge work salary plateau since mid-2024
- NBER WP33509 (2025 [T1]): college wage premium flat since 2010
- FRBNY (2024 [T1]): 40%+ recent college grads underemployed
- Eurofound (2024 [T1]): EU collective bargaining AI clauses, Denmark/Spain
- EU AI Act Regulation (EU) 2024/1689: in force August 2024

### India IT headcount (T3 observed)
- TCS + Infosys + Wipro + HCL: collective -42,000 headcount 2023-2025 while revenues grew
- Infosys: 300+ enterprise AI agents, 5-15% productivity gains per agent (Q3 FY2026)

### Five trauma mechanisms -- adapted for labor market
1. **Wage deflation** (Fixed-cost spread analog) -- beginning all regions; no aggregate deflation yet, but college premium flat and posted salaries plateauing
2. **Entry-level job collapse** (Investment drought analog) -- active USA and China; beginning Europe
3. **Education/credential erosion** (Talent flight analog) -- active USA; beginning China and Europe
4. **BPO geographic disruption** (Panic pricing analog) -- active USA gig economy; not yet China; Philippines/India at risk but not yet advanced
5. **Social/political disruption** (Policy lobbying analog) -- active Europe (EU AI Act); beginning USA and China

### Key structural observations
- The death spiral stage enters in 2029.1 (AI=50%) [model-derived] -- this is only 3 years from analysis date
- The deflationary transmission: productivity gains flow to capital owners (AI infrastructure), not to displaced workers -- aggregate consumer demand compressed
- Rough deflationary shock magnitude: $3-6T/yr by mid-2030s if wage bill tracks S-curve [directional only]
- Bifurcation is already measurable: AI-skills workers +56% wage premium vs non-AI cognitive workers (PwC 2024)

### Jevons classification
- Stellar: confirmed. Cheaper AI does NOT rebound into demand for human cognitive workers.

**Why:** These patterns are novel for X-curve analysis -- the incumbent is a labor market, not an industry, so all trauma mechanisms require reframing. Future runs on similar topics (AI vs. white-collar work, digital disruption of professional services) should reuse these mechanism mappings.

**How to apply:** When the incumbent is a labor market rather than a discrete industry/technology, reframe the 5 mechanisms as: (1) wage deflation, (2) entry-level/junior role collapse, (3) credential/education value erosion, (4) geographic labor arbitrage disruption, (5) political/policy lobbying for employment protection.
