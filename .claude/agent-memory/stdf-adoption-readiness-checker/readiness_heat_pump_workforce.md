---
name: Heat pump adoption readiness — HVAC installer workforce as binding constraint
description: Heat pump V3 adoption readiness is NOT_MET in all major regions (USA, China, Europe) due to HVAC installer workforce shortage, not manufacturing capacity
type: project
---

Key observed metrics (2023–2024):

**USA:**
- 423,000 HVAC workers in market; 80,000-person shortage (16% gap) [ACCA 2024]
- 40% of technicians are over 45 years old (succession risk)
- 81% of contractors report need for better training on next-gen heat pumps [Midea survey 2024]
- Break-even electricity price for HP operating parity: $0.088/kWh vs current US national avg $0.176/kWh — cost parity NOT reached

**UK:**
- 60,000 heat pumps installed in 2024 vs government trajectory of 600,000/year by 2028 (10% attainment)
- Only 50% of UK HVAC installers worked on heat pumps in Q1 2025 (down from 60% a year prior)

**Europe:**
- Severe shortage of air-conditioning/refrigeration mechanics in 11 of 27 EU countries [European Labour Authority 2023]
- Only one-third of HVAC training programs in Southern Europe include heat pump modules [Cedefop 2024]
- Germany: 80%+ of HVAC installers are heat pump qualified (outlier)

**Manufacturing:**
- China: 40% of global HP manufacturing; US: 20%; EU: 15% — manufacturing is NOT the constraint
- ~10% of global building heating served by heat pumps [IEA 2024]

**Why:** The HVAC installer workforce gap is the binding supply chain constraint for V3. Manufacturing capacity is adequate; installation requires specialized skills (refrigerant, electrical, hydronic) not covered in traditional boiler training. Retraining pipelines require 5–10 years.

**How to apply:** Rate V3 supply chain as PARTIAL for all regions due to installer gap. Workforce readiness: curve-trajectory-implied 2029–2031. In the US, V3 has the additional barrier of cost parity not being reached (HP cost = 1.985× gas furnace, 2024). European markets with higher gas prices are structurally closer to cost parity.
