Key observed metrics (end-2024):

**USA:**
- 51,000 public DC fast charging ports (end-2024), up 56% from 33,000 in June 2023 [AFDC]
- 59.1% of most-trafficked US highway corridors have DCFC at ≤50-mile spacing [AFDC/Joint Office]
- NEVI program has opened only 57 stations across 15 states as of Feb 2025 (federal funding paused Jan 2025)
- 8.1 DCFC ports per 1,000 BEVs on the road
- Status: PARTIAL (highway corridor gap is binding; urban/home charging is adequate)

**China:**
- 12.82 million total charging points (end-2024), 49% YoY increase
- 98% of highway service area coverage [China Daily, Jan 2025]
- 0.41 charging points per EV on road (31.4M EVs in fleet)
- Status: READY

**Europe:**
- 1 million+ public charging points (end-2024), 35% YoY increase
- AFIR mandates 150 kW stations every 60 km on TEN-T core network by end-2025
- Exact corridor compliance rate pre-2025 not published; trajectory suggests near-compliance
- Status: PARTIAL (AFIR deadline in progress)

**Why:** Highway corridor coverage is the binding V1 infrastructure metric because it enables long-distance BEV travel and accelerates fleet conversion; urban charging is well-served.

**How to apply:** For BEV disruption analyses, use 59.1% US corridor coverage (end-2024) as baseline; curve-trajectory-implied 70% by 2026, 90%+ by 2028 at observed build-out rate.
