Key observed metrics (end-2024):

**USA:**
- 43,500 public DCFC ports + 133,750 L2 public ports = ~177,000 publicly accessible (Jul 2024, AFDC)
- 59.1% of most-trafficked US highway corridors have DCFC at ≤50-mile spacing [AFDC/Joint Office, Q4 2024]
- NEVI program federal funding paused Jan 2025; non-NEVI networks deploying faster than Tesla for first time in 2024
- Build-out rate: ~5.3 pp/yr -> model-derived 80% threshold: ~2028
- Status: PARTIAL (highway corridor gap is binding; urban/home charging is adequate)

**China:**
- 12.82 million total charging points (end-2024), 25% YoY increase [Argus Media Dec 2024]
- 98% of highway service area coverage [China Daily, Jan 2025]
- 2.45 EVs per public charging point (31.4M fleet / charging points) — tracking to 2:1 government target (2025)
- ~46% of public charging points are DC fast chargers
- Status: READY

**Europe:**
- ~1M+ public charging points (end-2024), ~58% YoY growth from 632K end-2023 [EAFO + Transport & Environment]
- AFIR in force April 13, 2024: 150 kW DCFC every 60 km on TEN-T core network from 2025
- Exact TEN-T corridor compliance rate pre-2025 not published; AFIR deadline drives compliance
- Uneven deployment: Germany, Belgium, Italy, Poland have significant gaps vs AFIR targets
- Status: PARTIAL (AFIR deadline in progress; READY by ~2026)

**Why:** Highway corridor coverage is the binding V1 infrastructure metric because it enables long-distance BEV travel and accelerates fleet conversion; urban charging is well-served.

**How to apply:** For BEV disruption analyses, use 59.1% US corridor coverage (end-2024) as baseline; model-derived 80% by ~2028 at 5.3 pp/yr build-out. EU READY ~2026. China already READY.
