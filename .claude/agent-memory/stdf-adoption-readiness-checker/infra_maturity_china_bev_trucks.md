China BEV-HDT infrastructure readiness as of end-2025:

- **Highway service area coverage:** 98% of national expressway service areas have EV charging (Jan 2025, China Daily)
- **Dedicated HD truck charging stations:** 9,000+ nationwide (Nov 2025)
- **Heavy-truck freight corridor coverage:** 70%+ of major city-pair freight routes have charging/swap access
- **Qiji Energy (CATL) swap stations:** 305 heavy-truck stations by end-2025, covering "two horizontal, two vertical" backbone
  - G2 Beijing–Shanghai, G4 Beijing–HK, G42 Shanghai–Chengdu, G60 Shanghai–Kunming
  - 78,000 km network length = 44.1% of ~177,000 km expressway system
- **Swap throughput capacity gap [model-derived]:** 305 stations × 60 swaps/day = 18,300 vs 70,000+ swap-truck fleet demand (0.26x ratio)
- **Readiness by region:**
  - Eastern (YRD), Northern (BTH), Southern (PRD): READY
  - Central (Wuhan, Henan, Chongqing): PARTIAL
  - Western (Xinjiang, Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu): NOT_MET

**Why:** Swap infrastructure is the binding variable for long-haul BEV disruption of LNG trucks beyond ~400 km range. Depot/captive/regional is already well-served.

**How to apply:** When evaluating BEV-HDT adoption readiness for China in future runs, differentiate by use-case: captive fleet = MET, long-haul = PARTIAL until swap network reaches ~900 stations (trajectory-implied 2026).
