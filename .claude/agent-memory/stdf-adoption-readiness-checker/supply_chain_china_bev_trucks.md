China BEV-HDT supply chain as of 2024–2025:

- **Battery capacity:** ~1,097 GWh estimated (548 GWh installed at ~50% utilization, CRU 2025)
- **HDT battery demand [model-derived]:** 233,200 trucks × 350 kWh = 81.6 GWh = 7.4% of capacity
- **LFP price:** USD 53/kWh by late 2024 (deflationary — actively improving BEV TCO)
- **Top suppliers:** CATL 45%, BYD 25%, CALB 7% = 77% domestic; CATL supplies 60% of HDT batteries specifically
- **Critical minerals:** China controls 67% lithium processing, 73% cobalt — no upstream bottleneck for domestic supply
- **OEM count:** 10+ ZE truck OEMs (XCMG, SANY, FAW, Sinotruk, Shacman, Dongfeng, Foton, BYD, Hunan Truck...)
- **Workforce gap:** 1.03 million NEV technician shortfall by 2025 (MITI); 80% shortage in NEV repair sector
  - Real friction, NOT a blocking constraint — OEM dealer networks scaling reactively with fleet

**Why:** Supply chain is READY in China for the BEV HDT disruption. Battery overcapacity (~50% utilization) is a structural feature, not a weakness — it means price competition and continued deflationary cost-curve dynamics.

**How to apply:** In future China BEV readiness assessments, supply chain is unlikely to be the binding constraint. Workforce gap warrants tracking but has not yet constrained the 233K/year production rate.
