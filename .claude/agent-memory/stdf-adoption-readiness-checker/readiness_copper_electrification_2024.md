## Adoption Readiness: Electrification Disruptions as Copper Demand Drivers

**Analysis date:** 2026-03-25 | **Status:** NOT_MET globally; MET China

### 7 Disruption Vectors
1. BEV passenger/commercial (70 kg/vehicle Cu, 3x ICE)
2. Solar PV (1.2–5.5 t/MW Cu depending on BOS scope)
3. Onshore/offshore wind (2.7–9.5 t/MW Cu)
4. LFP BESS displacing gas peakers
5. Grid T&D expansion (largest volume Cu demand vector)
6. Aluminum substitution (negative Cu vector — internal to OEMs, no readiness assessment needed)
7. Air-source heat pumps (3–5 kg/unit Cu) — user override addition

### Sub-Condition Ratings

| Sub-Condition | Status | Binding Factor |
|---|---|---|
| Infrastructure | PARTIAL | US grid interconnection queue 2,300 GW, 5-yr wait; EU 500+ GW grid backlog |
| Supply chain | PARTIAL | Copper mine production 22.9 Mt vs 27 Mt refined demand — structural deficit; Li refining 70% China |
| Regulatory | PARTIAL | USA IRA credits removed 2025, 100% tariff on Chinese BEVs; EU permitting 7-9yr for wind |

### Regional Summary
- China: MET — 3.2M charging points (8.7:1), 356 GW solar/wind added 2024, NEV 44% new sales
- USA: NOT_MET — grid queue binding, IRA credits removed, no federal EV mandate
- Europe: NOT_MET — 81% wind stuck in permitting, 500+ GW grid backlog, EU countervailing duties Oct 2024

### Key Numbers (all [observed] 2024)
- Solar manufacturing: 1,405 GW capacity vs 728 GW production (52% utilization, non-binding)
- Battery: ~2 TWh capacity vs ~1 TWh demand (50% utilization, non-binding)
- Wind installs: 127 GW in 2024, China 72% share
- Copper mine: 22.9 Mt vs 27 Mt refined demand — deficit 167,000 tonnes (structural, widening)
- Heat pump US sales: 4.2M units 2024 (outsold gas furnaces 3.3M)
- Heat pump Europe: 2.2M units 2024 (down from 2.8M — policy reversal)

### Critical Insight
Copper is the only supply chain constraint that spans ALL seven disruption vectors simultaneously. Solar, battery, and wind manufacturing are all oversupplied. The copper mining deficit is the binding cross-vector bottleneck, not component manufacturing.

**Why:** Mine production growing at ~2-3%/yr; new project approvals <300 kt/yr vs 600-700 kt/yr needed. Codelco structural projects reach capacity in 2030.

**How to apply:** In multi-vector electrification analyses, always flag copper mining capacity as the cross-cutting supply chain risk, even when individual technology components (solar panels, batteries, turbines) are in surplus.
