**Four disruption vectors evaluated, all MET:**

| Vector | Status | Parity Year | Confidence | Key Evidence |
|--------|--------|-------------|------------|--------------|

**Aggregate confidence:** 0.89

**Key structural pattern:** Solar and Wind are stellar energy vectors with the earliest and most decisive parity crossings (10–13 years before analysis date). Both are now operating far below inflection zone (solar at 26% of incumbent, wind at 45%). BESS is in inflection zone (52% of incumbent). EV is the most recent crossover (0.8 years before analysis date), entry-level only — full-market EV parity not yet registered.

**EV binding constraint:** Entry-level BEV parity MET but market-mix average transaction price (~$55K) remains well above ICE (~$30K). Copper demand growth from EVs driven by volume adoption at moderate cost advantage, not dominant cost superiority. EV inflection zone (50–70% of ICE cost) not until 2034–2044.

**Why:** Multi-vector commodity disruption study. Copper demand growth from electrification (CAGR 15.1%/yr vs 1.9%/yr for overall copper demand) is the swing factor in copper markets. Cost parity confirmation across all vectors means electrification-driven copper demand growth is structurally locked in by cost superiority.

**How to apply:** When this slug recurs, all four vectors are MET with no further evaluation needed. Focus of downstream analysis shifts to S-curve adoption pace and copper intensity coefficients, not cost parity uncertainty.
