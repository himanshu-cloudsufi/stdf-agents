## EV vs ICE Passenger Car — Capability Dimension Reference (validated 2026-03-16)

### Range (range_km)
- Threshold: 350 km (mainstream buyer objection floor)
- Threshold crossed: 2021 (median fleet)
- 2024 value: 455 km median fleet (WLTP-equiv)
- Trajectory: exponential; 130 (2012) → 165 (2015) → 250 (2019) → 455 (2024)
- ICE incumbent: ~800 km full tank

### DC Fast Charging Time (charge_time_min, 0-80%)
- Threshold: 45 min (mainstream convenience)
- Threshold crossed: 2019
- 2024 value: 18–30 min mainstream; 9 min BYD Flash Charging (2025)
- Trajectory: linear → asymptotic; 90 (2012) → 45 (2019) → 20 (2024)
- ICE: 3–5 min refuel (structural gap remains for non-home chargers)

### Acceleration (0-60 mph, sec)
- Threshold: 7.0 sec (match avg midsize ICE)
- Threshold crossed: 2017 (mainstream performance EVs)
- 2024 values: 2.8 sec (performance tier); 5.5 sec (mainstream)
- ICE avg midsize 2024: 6.5 sec
- Structural advantage: instant torque at 0 RPM, single-speed drivetrain

### Energy Efficiency (kWh/100km primary energy)
- Threshold: <30 kWh/100km
- Threshold crossed: well before 2015
- 2024 values: USA 17.9; Europe 16.4; China 12.2
- ICE equivalent: ~63 kWh/100km primary energy (7.5 L/100km × 9 kWh/L × ~94% thermal loss)
- BEV is 3.5x more efficient at primary energy level
- Sources: FuelEconomy.gov (USA), ICCT (Europe), DieselNet (China)

### Maintenance Cost ($/mile)
- Threshold: at or below ICE ($0.101/mile)
- Threshold crossed: ~2015
- 2024 values: EV $0.078/mile vs ICE $0.101/mile (AAA)
- EV saves 23% on maintenance (DoE: 6.1 vs 10.1 cents/mile = 40% savings)
- Sources: AAA annual study, U.S. DoE, Consumer Reports

### Total Cost of Ownership ($/mile, 7yr/15k mi/yr)
- Threshold: at or below ICE overall (~$0.633/mile)
- Status: PARTIAL (fleet avg EV $0.761/mile; segment-level varies)
- Vincentric 2024: SUVs/sedans already 8–20% below ICE; trucks 4% above
- Full fleet parity projected: 2026–2028
- TCO blockers: higher purchase price (55% more), faster depreciation (52% vs 39% 3yr), higher insurance (30% more)

### NVH Cabin Noise (dBA at 65mph)
- Threshold: ≤65 dBA (premium car standard)
- Threshold crossed: 2021
- 2024 values: BEV 59 dBA vs ICE 67 dBA
- EV challenge: high-freq motor whine 500–10,000 Hz (addressable with acoustic barriers)
- Sources: PMC/NCBI NVH study, SAE 2025 review, EV Engineering Online

### Software/Connectivity (SAE level index, 0–5)
- Threshold: ≥1.0 (L1 ADAS baseline)
- Threshold crossed: 2016
- 2024 values: BEV L2+ = 2.7/5.0; ICE traditional = 0.5/5.0
- China L2-capable BEV penetration: 55.7% of new sales (H1 2024)
- Structural advantage: native OTA architecture, high-compute platforms
- LiDAR costs: high-end spinning USA fell $60k (2018) → $9k (2024)

### Charging Infrastructure (global public chargers)
- Threshold: >500k global public chargers (viable network)
- Threshold crossed: 2019
- 2024 values: 5.44M global; 200k USA; 1M+ Europe
- CAGR 2015–2024: 45.7% (30x growth from 184k)
- Sources: STDF catalog (Database), ICCT, Joint Office of Energy and Transportation

### Overall Convergence Pattern
- 7 of 9 dimensions: threshold met
- Convergence type: SIMULTANEOUS (all 7 cleared within 2016–2021 window)
- Remaining blockers: TCO overall (resolves 2026–2027), DC fast charging convenience for non-home-chargers (ultra-fast mainstream ~2027–2028)
- Full capability parity year: 2026
