## EV-ICE Tipping Point (March 2026 Analysis)

**Key finding:** For the BEV disruption of ICE, adoption readiness is the binding constraint — cost parity ($/mile) was already crossed in 2023 and capability parity across 8 of 9 dimensions by 2021. The lag is infrastructure and supply chain.

**Why:** Cost-curve dynamics for battery packs are fast (16.8%/yr learning rate). Infrastructure deployment (charging networks) follows vehicle sales but with a 2-5 year lag. Supply chain (cell manufacturing, rare earth sourcing) constrains volume ramp.

**How to apply:** In transport disruption analyses, always check if cost and capability have already crossed before assuming they are the binding constraint. Infrastructure/supply chain readiness often lags 2-5 years behind technical and economic parity.

## Regional Tipping Sequence — Transport (BEV/ICE)

China tips 3-5 years ahead of the USA. Sequence observed:
1. China: 2024-2025 (all 3 conditions met; 35% BEV share, 3.58M chargers, entry-segment 40% below ICE)
2. Europe: 2026-2027 (cost parity and capability met; infrastructure 2 years behind China)
3. USA: 2027-2029 (same cost/capability; geographic infrastructure gap, no domestic entry-level BEV below $25,000 at volume, tariff barriers blocking cheapest Chinese models)
4. Global composite: 2026-2027

**Why China leads by 3-5 years:** Manufacturing scale (BYD/CATL localized supply chain), coordinated infrastructure deployment (3.58M chargers vs 204,000 USA), largest single EV market enabling faster learning-curve progression.

## Convergence Acceleration Pattern (EV)

- SDV-EV already embedded in current BEV advantage (OTA, software revenue $1k-12k/vehicle) — accelerates capability gap widening, not a future event
- SWB-EV (solar+battery home charging) adds $0.04-0.06/mile cost advantage for ~30% of BEV owners
- A-EV (autonomous + BEV) contributes 1-2 year compression in S-curve TAIL (not tipping point itself), primarily via TaaS fleet demand post-2027
- Combined convergence estimated to accelerate global 80% completion by 1-2 years vs BEV-only scenario

## Post-Tipping Dynamics Key Numbers (EV/ICE)

**Incumbent vicious cycle anchors:**
- ICE platform breakeven: ~250,000 units/year; below this, per-unit cost rises $800-1,500
- Dealer service revenue: 40-60% of gross profit from maintenance; drops 25-35% at 30-40% BEV penetration
- VW already announced 35,000 job cuts and 40% MEB cost reduction (2024) — early vicious cycle signal

**Disruptor virtuous cycle anchors:**
- Learning rate: 16.8%/yr (empirically derived, not assumed)
- Target China LFP pack: ~$55/kWh by 2028 (from $85/kWh in 2025)
- At $55/kWh, 60 kWh mid-size BEV manufactured at <$22,000 globally
- Supply chain investment: $200B+ committed through 2030

## Data Gap Pattern: TCO Measurement

TCO fleet-average EV vs ICE lacks a multi-year time series. Upstream capability agent had segment-level snapshots (2024 Vincentric) but no trend line. For future transport disruption analyses, note that:
- Segment-level TCO parity typically arrives 2-3 years before fleet-average TCO parity
- Fleet averages are skewed by higher-priced EV segments before volume ramps in entry-level
- Best indicator: SUV and sedan segment TCO (the highest-volume segments) rather than fleet average
