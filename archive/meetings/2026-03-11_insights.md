## 4. ANALYSIS FEATURES

### AI Employment Loss (AEL) Model
- Core model tracking AI-driven job displacement. Deep dive presented by Richard and Jitin. (Vasul, line 59)

### Task-Level Clustering for AEL
- Two approaches being explored:
  1. **Rule-based clustering**: Filter tasks by LLM exposure categories (E0 = no LLM capability, E1 = LLM-doable, E2 = LLM + tools), then further subdivide by metrics (anthropic penetration %, task frequency, median age, etc.) to create 5-10 task clusters (e.g., physical, cognitive, low-wage, high-wage, active). (Jitin, lines 141-153)
  2. **Unsupervised algorithmic clustering**: K-means, hierarchical clustering on task features, then manually associate business logic to each cluster. Drawback: clusters are mathematical, need manual interpretation. (Jitin, lines 154-163)
- Plan to experiment with both and potentially reconcile them. (Jitin, line 163-164)

### Tunable Parameters per Cluster
- Parameters: **time to 80% adoption, adoption lag, replacement ratio, and ceiling** (maximum adoption cap -- e.g., no fewer than 20% humans). (Jitin, lines 152, 168-173)
- Example: a "fast mover" cluster (anthropic penetration > 10%) might get: adoption speed = 24 months, adoption lag = 0, replacement ratio = 65/35, ceiling = 80%. (Jitin, lines 166-167)
- Users will still be able to adjust parameters in real time during chat sessions. (Jitin, line 185)

### Calibration Against Target Signals
- Plan to use youth unemployment as a target signal to calibrate model parameters: run correlation between predicted adoption curves and actual unemployment data, iterate to minimize loss / maximize correlation. (Jitin, lines 180-184)

### Backtesting Framework
- Model validation against last 3 years of real data. Tony's goal: make the model "less wrong" iteratively. (Tony, lines 189-199, 213)

### Sanity Metrics
- Proposed tracking: predicted sector displacement vs. unemployment change; percentage of sectors where model correctly predicts employment direction. (Jitin, line 185)

### SIBA Framework (Second-order Implications, Bottlenecks, Accelerators)
- Tony considers this non-optional for every analysis run. One session failed to include it. (Tony, lines 339-343)

## 5. PHILOSOPHY & THINKING

### "We're Not Quite Horses -- Not Yet"
- Tony references his essay: current AI disruption is not yet the full "horses moment" (where an entire class of labor becomes obsolete), but it is heading there. The dynamics are multi-dimensional, not simple. (Tony, lines 186-188)

### Simultaneous GDP Growth + Unemployment Growth
- Tony believes both GDP growth and unemployment growth will happen concurrently: "we're gonna have both GDP growth and unemployment growth... folks think that job creation will kick in... I don't think that's gonna happen." (Tony, line 253)
- Unemployment rate matters more than GDP growth rate: "whether GDP growth is gonna be three and a half percent or seven percent next year is not as important as whether unemployment is gonna be six percent or five percent." (Tony, line 253)

### Edge is Biggest Now
- The gap between Stellar's analytical capability and mainstream market understanding is at its maximum. Mainstream economists still believe new jobs will replace lost ones; this narrative has "probably another year" of life. (Tony, line 253)

### Signal vs. Noise
- Tony is skeptical of anecdotal corporate layoff stories (Block, RI Advisory) as evidence: "a lot of this is noise... whether Block fired 4,000 people because of AI or because they were not doing great, who knows." Insists on statistical data over anecdotes. (Tony, lines 216-227)

### War/Conflict Accelerates Disruption Adoption
- Tony suggests geopolitical crises (Iran conflict, energy disruption) may accelerate adoption of alternative energy: "do you need more conflicts and war... to realize what's in front of your eyes." (Tony, line 27)

### Discovery Phase
- Tony acknowledges the team is still in a "discovery phase" and expects many hidden dynamics and patterns to be found: "I'm pretty sure that there's a lot to be discovered." (Tony, lines 192-198)

### Data Integrity as Core Principle
- Dr. Waleed describes the data factory as "a highly guarded embassy" -- "the internet data is poison. We don't want any poison coming in... only curated data will be allowed." (Dr. Waleed, lines 311-314)
