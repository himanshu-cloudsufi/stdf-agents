## 4. ANALYSIS FEATURES

### SIBA Framework Integration
- Core analytical framework used across the product.
- Stellar Sense should track signals related to the SIBA framework hypotheses.
- Tony wants: signals as evidence for/against hypotheses; bottleneck detection; adoption decelerator detection.

### Scaling Properties Analysis
- Track scaling rates of different technologies (solar PV, batteries, logic chips, memory).
- Identify when scaling rates diverge -- "correlation breaks."
- **Tony:** "What are the different scaling properties of different technologies, and when do we think they might break?"

### Supply-Demand Bottleneck Identification
- Demand may scale quickly but supply may not (copper) or supply may outpace demand (lead).
- Track daily supply-demand dynamics to identify bottleneck emergence.
- **Tony:** "If we do the work every day on how demand for copper is increasing... but the supply is not scaling as quickly, then we can identify when bottlenecks may emerge before they emerge."

### Rupture Point Detection
- Rupture points occur at ~5-10% market penetration of a new technology.
- Key trigger: when new technology becomes cheaper than incumbent in even one market.
- Software should flag when any market is approaching a rupture point.

### S-Curve Adoption vs. Capacity Factor
- Tony draws a distinction between technology **adoption** (S-curve) and technology **utilization** (capacity factor).
- **Tony (car analogy):** "We use cars 5% of the time. We park it 95% of the time. The reason that transport as a service is gonna be so disruptive is that it's gonna take that 5% capacity factor to 50 or 80%."
- **Tony:** "Adoption in terms of S-curve is not the same thing as using the technology to its fullest extent."
- Within their own organization: "100% adoption of 1% of the capability."

### Back-Testing Framework for AL Model
- Back-test unemployment predictions against actual data for different time windows.
- Next step: back-test job-specific unemployment (not just aggregate) to check model robustness.
- Currently under-predicting -- parameter calibration needed.

### Cumulative Surplus / Inventory Modeling
- For commodities like lead: inventory accumulates because supply cannot be shut off (byproduct of zinc mining + recycling).
- Carrying costs eventually force fire-sale prices -- "the whole thing blows up."
- **Tony:** "Lead is a byproduct of mining, so mining itself doesn't get shut off when lead demand drops. And because of the recycling, you can also not shut off the supply."

## 5. PHILOSOPHY & THINKING

### AI Should Drive, Humans Advise
- Tony envisions a model where AI runs continuously and humans provide oversight/input when prompted.
- **Tony:** "We still have humans in the loop, but it's driven primarily by the AI."
- The current paradigm (human prompts AI) is backwards; it should be AI prompts human.

### Data and Intelligence Never Sleep
- **Tony:** "We know data doesn't sleep, we should update it every day. Now we need to realize that the intelligence also does not sleep."
- Data tracking should be at minimum daily; ideally real-time.

### Correlation Breaks = Investment Opportunities
- When established correlations between technologies or markets break, that signals a major opportunity.
- **Tony:** "Correlations breaking is a huge investment opportunity for us."

### Capacity Factor as a General Concept
- Tony applies the concept of "capacity factor" broadly: cars used 5% of the time, AI used a tiny percent of capability.
- The disruption opportunity is in technologies/services that dramatically increase capacity utilization.
- **Tony:** "100% adoption of 1% of the capability" -- this is the current state, and it needs to change.

### Disruption Is About Scaling Properties
- Different technologies have fundamentally different scaling properties.
- Distributed technologies (solar, batteries) can scale exponentially while centralized infrastructure (grid) can only scale incrementally.
- **Tony:** "Solar PV distributed and batteries installed can scale at a way faster pace than the existing grid. The existing grid is a kludge, it's a disaster, it can only increase incrementally."

### Supply Dynamics in Byproduct Commodities
- When a commodity is a byproduct (lead from zinc mining), supply cannot respond to demand drops.
- This creates a unique dynamic: supply increases as demand drops, accelerating price collapse.
- **Tony:** "Supply will increase as demand drops, and that's what makes this trade work."
