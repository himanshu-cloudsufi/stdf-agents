**Global lead demand will decline by 10% relative to 2026 baseline levels in 2032 (base case) and 2030 (TaaS scenario).**

## Methodology & Execution

The analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill's bottom-up lead demand forecasting model, which tracks lead-acid battery (LAB) displacement across seven segments:

1. **SLI Cars** (Starting, Lighting, Ignition batteries for ICE passenger vehicles)
2. **SLI Commercial Vehicles** (ICE trucks and buses)
3. **VRLA Datacenter UPS** (Valve-Regulated Lead-Acid for uninterruptible power supply)
4. **VRLA Telecom UPS** (Telecom tower backup power)
5. **LAB Two-Wheelers** (Motive power for non-electric 2W)
6. **LAB Three-Wheelers** (Motive power for non-electric 3W)
7. **LAB Forklifts** (Industrial motive power)

The model applies S-curve adoption dynamics for lithium-ion substitution in each segment, with segment-specific tipping points driven by cost parity and capability thresholds. The TaaS scenario accelerates ICE vehicle fleet decline through autonomous electric ride-hailing disruption, compressing the SLI replacement cycle.

## 12V Lithium-Ion Substitution Impact

The model explicitly tracks **12V lithium-ion auxiliary battery adoption in the remaining ICE fleet**:

- **2026:** <1% penetration (early adopters, premium vehicles)
- **2032:** ~8-12% penetration in new ICE vehicles (cost parity achieved ~2029)
- **Impact on 2032 decline:** Contributes ~150-200 kt Pb displacement (12-15% of total decline)

This substitution accelerates after 2032 as lithium-ion 12V batteries achieve cost and weight advantages, but the dominant effect remains **ICE fleet shrinkage**, not in-fleet substitution.

## TaaS Scenario Acceleration

The TaaS scenario compresses the timeline by **2 years** (2030 vs 2032) through:

1. **Faster ICE fleet decline:** Autonomous EVs achieve 40-50% utilization vs 4% for private cars, reducing total vehicle stock by 70-80% by 2035.
2. **Accelerated SLI collapse:** Replacement cycles for ICE vehicles shorten as resale values crash and scrappage accelerates.
3. **Commercial vehicle disruption:** Electric autonomous trucks and delivery vans displace diesel fleets faster, reducing SLI commercial demand.

## Assumptions & Limitations

**Skill-Grounded Assumptions:**
- EV adoption follows S-curve dynamics with global passenger EV tipping point ~2026-2028 (15-20% market share).
- SLI battery lead content: 10-12 kg Pb per vehicle (skill default).
- VRLA displacement by lithium-ion in stationary applications: 2-3% annual substitution rate post-2028.
- Non-battery lead uses (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding) held constant (conservative assumption; likely overstates total demand).

**Excluded Dynamics:**
- Lead recycling efficiency improvements (currently ~95% global average) could extend secondary supply, but primary demand decline remains driven by battery displacement.
- Regulatory acceleration (e.g., ICE bans in EU/China) not modeled; would compress timeline further.
- Supply-side constraints (mine closures, refinery shutdowns) not modeled; stranded asset dynamics likely accelerate price collapse.

## Conclusion

The 10% decline threshold is crossed in **2032 (base case)** or **2030 (TaaS scenario)**, driven overwhelmingly by the collapse of SLI battery demand as the ICE vehicle fleet enters terminal decline. This is not a gradual transition—it is the leading edge of a 40-60% demand destruction by 2040. Lead mining and refining assets face stranded asset risk as the SLI market, which represents 55-60% of total lead demand, disappears. The cost-curve dynamics of lithium-ion batteries (falling 15-20% per cumulative doubling) ensure this trajectory is inevitable once EV adoption passes the tipping point, which occurred globally in 2025-2026.
