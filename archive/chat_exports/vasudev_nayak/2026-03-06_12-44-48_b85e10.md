**2. The cost spread across tiers is ~5,600× [C1][C3].** GPT-5.2 Pro output at $168/MTok vs. Qwen 3 4B at $0.03/MTok represents a 5,600× price differential for output tokens — the widest spread in the history of commercial AI APIs.

### Cost-Curve Trajectory: The Structural Conclusion

AI token pricing is on a double-exponential cost-curve collapse driven by hardware improvement (GPU generations), software efficiency (model architecture, quantization, speculative decoding), and competitive pressure from open-weight Chinese models. The trajectory:

- **2021:** GPT-3 API input ~$60/MTok [C5]
- **2023:** GPT-3.5 Turbo ~$2/MTok input [C5]
- **2024:** GPT-4o mini launched at $0.15/MTok input — a 60% reduction from GPT-3.5 Turbo [C4]
- **2026:** Qwen 3 4B at $0.03/MTok input; DeepSeek cache-hit at $0.028/MTok [C3]

This is a ~2,000× cost reduction in five years for capable AI inference. The cost-curve does not stop here. Frontier model pricing (GPT-5.2 at $1.75 input) will follow the same trajectory as GPT-4o did: within 18–24 months, frontier-class capability will be available at sub-$0.10/MTok input. The market is bifurcating into commodity inference (near-zero marginal cost) and premium reasoning (high-value, low-volume tasks).
