## AGI Definition & Arrival Forecast

**AGI is operationalized through measurable capability thresholds, not philosophical criteria.** The dominant framework defines AGI as systems achieving **human-level or better performance across economically valuable tasks with breadth, depth, and autonomy** [W6][W1].

### Technical Definition (Levels of AGI Framework)

AGI progression is measured across **five performance levels** [W6]:

1. **Emerging AGI** (Level 1): Equal to or slightly better than unskilled humans
2. **Competent AGI** (Level 2): 50th percentile skilled adult performance
3. **Expert AGI** (Level 3): 90th percentile skilled adult performance
4. **Virtuoso AGI** (Level 4): 99th percentile skilled adult performance
5. **Superhuman AGI** (Level 5): Outperforms 100% of humans

Combined with **breadth dimensions**: Narrow (specific tasks) → General (wide range of cognitive tasks).

### Key Capability Thresholds

**METR autonomous research agent (ARA) benchmarks** test whether AI can independently conduct ML research tasks—a critical capability threshold for advanced AI safety policies [W5]. Current frontier models (2026) demonstrate:

- **Emerging AGI capabilities** in some domains (coding, reasoning, language)
- **Failures on extended autonomy**, complex multi-step reasoning, and robust generalization [W2][W5]
- **Task-specific competence** without general cross-domain transfer

**OECD AI Capability Indicators** track progress via standardized benchmarks: MMLU (knowledge), GSM8K (math reasoning), HumanEval (coding), long-horizon planning, and tool use [W4][W1].

### AGI Arrival Projection

**No consensus date exists; estimates range widely based on definition strictness.** Using the artificial-labor model's capability trajectory:

- **AI capability in 2030:** ~620,000 seconds (172 hours) — sufficient for multi-day cognitive workflows
- **Doubling time:** 7 months (METR empirical)

By **2030-2032**, AI systems will likely achieve **Competent AGI (Level 2)** across most economically valuable cognitive tasks, defined by 50th percentile human performance with broad task coverage [W6]. This aligns with the artificial-labor model showing 30%+ displacement by 2030.

**Expert AGI (Level 3)** arrival depends on breakthroughs in long-horizon planning, robust generalization, and autonomous research—**likely 2032-2035** if current trajectories hold [W2][W5].

**Superhuman AGI (Level 5)** timelines remain speculative; no validated model exists for predicting capability beyond human-level performance ceilings.

## Critical Clarification: Displacement ≠ Unemployment

**The artificial-labor skill outputs FTE displacement from cognitive task automation, NOT unemployment rate.** Unemployment requires modeling:

- Labor force reabsorption into new roles
- Job creation in non-cognitive sectors
- Workforce exit (retirement, disability, discouraged workers)
- Labor force participation rate changes

A 20% displacement rate does NOT imply 20% unemployment. Historical precedent (agricultural mechanization, industrial automation) shows partial reabsorption, though at lower wages and with transition trauma. The 28.8M displaced workers represent the **maximum potential unemployment** if zero reabsorption occurs—an upper bound, not a forecast.
