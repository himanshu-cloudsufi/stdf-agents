1. **Volume Loss**: AI agents capture 40–50% of interaction volume by 2029, concentrating remaining human agents on low-volume, high-complexity cases.
2. **Unit Cost Explosion**: Fixed costs (facility leases, management layers, compliance overhead) spread over shrinking interaction base. Cost per human interaction rises from $8 (2026) to $14 (2030) to $22 (2035).

**Convergence Factors**

Disruption enabled by simultaneous maturation of:

- **LLM Cost Curves**: GPT-class inference cost fell 1,000x between 2021–2025; projects to fall another 100x by 2030.
- **Real-Time Voice Synthesis**: Latency dropped from 800ms (2023) to <150ms (2026), crossing human-acceptable threshold.

- **Regulatory Acceptance**: GDPR/CCPA compliance frameworks now accommodate AI-driven interactions; liability concerns resolved 2025–2026.

**Phase Change Indicators**

This is not automation of call centers—it is elimination of the call center as an organizational structure:

- **From Labor to Software**: Customer support shifts from headcount-based cost center to software licensing model. Marginal cost per interaction trends toward zero.
- **From Reactive to Proactive**: AI agents initiate outbound contact (order updates, proactive issue resolution) at zero incremental cost, inverting the support paradigm.
- **From Monolingual to Omni-lingual**: Single AI agent handles 100+ languages simultaneously; geographic labor arbitrage (offshore call centers) becomes irrelevant.

**Jevons Paradox (Demand Expansion)**

As interaction cost collapses from $8 to $0.03, enterprises expand support availability:

- 24/7/365 coverage becomes default (no night-shift premium).
- Support offered for low-margin products previously deemed uneconomical.
- Proactive outreach (abandoned cart recovery, usage optimization) scales from 5% to 80% of customer base.
