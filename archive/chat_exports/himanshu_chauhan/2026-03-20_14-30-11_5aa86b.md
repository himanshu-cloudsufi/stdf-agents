### Why 2026

**SLI (Starter-Lighting-Ignition)** — the largest global lead demand segment — is structurally eroded by BEV adoption. Each BEV sold permanently removes one SLI battery from the replacement pool, compounding year-over-year [C1].

**VRLA (UPS, telecom, industrial)** — lithium-ion has already achieved cost dominance in datacenter UPS and telecom backup. Substitution accelerates through 2025–2026 as pack prices continue their descent [C1].

**E2W/E3W traction (China)** — historically the largest traction lead-acid segment globally, systematically displaced by lithium-ion since the early 2020s [C1].

On the supply side, primary lead is a co-product of zinc and silver mining — it does not contract in response to demand signals. Secondary (recycled) lead supply continues to grow on its own trajectory. The result is supply growth colliding with multi-segment demand collapse, crossing the 10% structural oversupply threshold in 2026 [C1].

**Bottom line:** 2026 is the year. Both scenarios confirm it, which means the outcome is demand-driven and not scenario-dependent. The X-curve for lead demand is past its rupture point.
