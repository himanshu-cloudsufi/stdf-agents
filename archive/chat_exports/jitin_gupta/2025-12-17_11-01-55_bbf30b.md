## Global Lead Demand: 10% Decline Timeline

**10% decline NOT reached by 2035.** Based on current EV adoption trajectories, global lead demand reaches **~9.5% decline by 2035**, just missing the 10% threshold.

**Projected crossing: ~2036** (extrapolating 2030-2035 trend of -72 kt/year).

---

### Key Findings

**2024 Baseline:** 9,731 kt  
**2035 Forecast:** 8,804 kt (-927 kt, -9.5%)  
**10% Threshold:** 8,758 kt  
**Gap at 2035:** 46 kt short of 10% decline

---

### Structural Drivers of Decline

The 9.5% decline by 2035 is driven by two opposing forces:

**Declining Segments:**
- **Industrial Batteries:** -1,023 kt (-36%)
  - Motive power (forklifts): -250 kt (-27%)
  - Stationary power (UPS): -768 kt (-40%)
  - **Cause:** Displacement by lithium-ion batteries due to superior TCO economics (12-year vs 5-year lifespans)

- **SLI Replacement Demand:** -206 kt (-5%)
  - Falling installed base of ICE vehicles as fleet electrifies
  - EVs use smaller 12V auxiliary batteries (9 kg vs 11.5 kg for ICE cars)

**Growing Segments:**
- **Other Uses:** +286 kt (+17%)
  - Construction, ammunition, radiation shielding (non-battery applications)
  - Price-elastic demand responding to lower lead costs

**Flat Segment:**
- **SLI OEM (new vehicle sales):** Stable at ~1,009 kt
  - EV growth offset by smaller battery sizes
  - Total vehicle sales growth balanced by lower lead content per EV

---

### Why Decline Is Gradual (Not Rapid)

1. **SLI Batteries Resilient:** Represent 57% of demand by 2035 (up from 53% in 2024)
   - All vehicles (ICE and EV) still require 12V lead-acid batteries
   - Replacement demand (4.5-year cycle) provides sustained volume

2. **Industrial Displacement Already Priced In:** Lithium-ion adoption in forklifts/UPS is mature, limiting further declines post-2035

3. **Non-Battery Growth Cushions:** Other uses (+17%) partially offset battery declines

### Investment Implications

**Near-Term Resilience (2024-2030):**
- Lead demand declines slowly (-0.7%/year average)
- SLI battery segment provides structural floor
- Industrial displacement largely complete by 2030

**Long-Term Pressure (post-2030):**
- 10% decline threshold crossed by **2036**
- Further EV penetration (>50% global fleet by 2040) accelerates replacement demand erosion
- Price support from non-battery uses limits downside

**Key Risk:** If EV adoption accelerates beyond baseline scenario (e.g., tipping point occurs earlier in major markets), the 10% decline could arrive **1-2 years earlier** (2034-2035).
