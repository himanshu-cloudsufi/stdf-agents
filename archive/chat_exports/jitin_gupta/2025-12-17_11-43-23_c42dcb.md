#### Step 2: SLI Battery Demand Model (Bottom-Up Installed Base)

SLI demand calculated from vehicle installed base and battery lifecycles:

\[
\text{SLI Demand}_t = \text{OEM}_t + \text{Replacement}_t
\]

Where:
- \( \text{OEM}_t = \sum_{v,p} \text{Sales}_{v,p,t} \times k_{v,p} \)
- \( \text{Replacement}_t = \sum_{v,p} \frac{\text{IB}_{v,p,t}}{\text{Life}_{\text{battery}}} \times k_{v,p} \)

Lead content coefficients (\( k_{v,p} \)) by vehicle type (v) and powertrain (p):

| Vehicle Type | ICE (kg) | BEV/PHEV (kg) | EV (kg) |
|--------------|----------|---------------|---------|
| Passenger Cars | 11.5 | 0 | - |
| Two-Wheelers | 2.5 | - | 0 |
| Three-Wheelers | 7.0 | - | 0 |
| Commercial Vehicles | 22.0 | 0 | 0 |

**Critical: EVs have zero lead contribution** (use lithium-ion 12V systems, not lead-acid).

#### Step 3: Installed Base Evolution (Stock-Flow Accounting)

\[
\text{IB}_{v,p,t+1} = \text{IB}_{v,p,t} + \text{Sales}_{v,p,t} - \text{Scrappage}_{v,p,t}
\]

Where scrappage follows asset lifetime:
\[
\text{Scrappage}_{v,p,t} = \frac{\text{IB}_{v,p,t}}{\text{Life}_{\text{asset},v}}
\]

Asset lifetimes: Cars 18 years, 2W 11 years, 3W 9 years, CV 20 years  
Battery lifetimes: SLI 4.5 years (separate from asset lifetime)

#### Converging Technologies Driving Lead Demand Decline

**1. Lithium-Ion Battery Cost Curves (Wright's Law)**
- Battery pack costs: $150/kWh (2023) → $80/kWh (2030) → $60/kWh (2035)
- Learning rate: 18% cost reduction per doubling of cumulative production
- Manufacturing scale: Global Li-ion capacity 1,000 GWh (2023) → 5,000 GWh (2030)

**2. Electric Vehicle Drivetrain Economics**
- EV cost parity with ICE reached in China (2024), spreading to Europe (2025-2026) and USA (2027-2028)
- 12V auxiliary battery transition: Lead-acid (11.5 kg) → Lithium-ion (0 kg lead)
- Zero marginal lead demand from EVs creates permanent displacement

**3. Industrial Battery TCO Crossover**
- **Motive Power (Forklifts):** Li-ion TCO parity reached 2020
  - Lead-acid: $0.08/cycle, 7-year life, 1,500 cycles
  - Lithium-ion: $0.05/cycle, 12-year life, 5,000+ cycles
  - Payback: 3-4 years despite 2× higher upfront cost

- **Stationary Power (UPS):** Li-ion TCO parity reached 2022
  - Lead-acid: 6-year life, 0.35 kWh/kg energy density
  - Lithium-ion: 12-year life, 0.25 kWh/kg (4× better than lead-acid)
  - Space savings: 70% footprint reduction critical for datacenter/telecom

| Application | Tipping Point | Primary Driver |

| **Two-Wheeler** | 2022-2025 (China/India) | EV cost advantage in high-density urban markets |

<STRATEGIC_IMPLICATIONS>

#### Business Model Shifts

**Lead Battery Manufacturers:**
- **Defensive consolidation:** Merge to maintain scale economies as volumes decline
- **Aftermarket focus:** Shift from OEM to replacement market (80% of demand, higher margins)
- **Geographic arbitrage:** Focus on slower-adoption regions (USA, Rest of World) to extend runway
- **Product mix shift:** Emphasize advanced lead-acid (AGM, TPPL) for premium ICE vehicles and stop-start applications

**Lead Mining/Smelting:**
- **Capacity rationalization:** Close marginal mines/smelters as demand declines
- **Secondary lead dominance:** Recycled lead grows from 60% to 70% of supply by 2035
- **Export markets:** Developing regions sustain lead demand longer (ICE vehicles, off-grid power systems)

**Lithium-Ion Ecosystem:**
- **Market capture:** Industrial battery displacement accelerates Li-ion adoption in motive/stationary applications
- **12V auxiliary battery:** New segment for Li-ion suppliers (15M+ units/year by 2030)
- **Second-life integration:** EV batteries cascade to stationary storage, competing with both lead and new Li-ion

#### Risks and Opportunities

**Risks:**
- **Stranded capacity:** Lead smelting overcapacity creates margin compression
- **Recycling disruption:** If EV adoption accelerates faster, lead battery recycling volumes cliff in 2030s
- **Regulatory pressure:** EU Battery Directive, Extended Producer Responsibility increase compliance costs
- **Substitution acceleration:** Faster-than-expected Li-ion cost declines could steepen industrial battery displacement

**Opportunities:**
- **Niche resilience:** Lead-acid retains cost advantage in low-cycle stationary applications (grid backup, solar off-grid)
- **Hybrid systems:** Lead-acid for low-cost bulk storage + Li-ion for high-performance applications
- **Emerging markets:** Africa, Southeast Asia sustain ICE vehicle demand through 2040, supporting lead battery exports
- **Advanced lead-acid R&D:** Carbon-enhanced electrodes, bipolar designs extend cycle life 2-3×, defending against Li-ion in stationary

#### Recommendations

**For Lead Industry:**
1. **Consolidate aggressively:** Reduce overcapacity before price collapse
2. **Lock in replacement market:** Long-term contracts with distributors, aftermarket channels
3. **Invest in recycling efficiency:** Maintain 97%+ recycling rate to defend environmental credentials
4. **Lobby for lifecycle cost comparisons:** Highlight lead-acid's recyclability vs Li-ion's end-of-life challenges

**For Investors:**
- **Short lead miners/smelters:** Structural decline confirmed, -10% demand by 2032 is floor not ceiling
- **Long lithium-ion supply chain:** Battery-grade lithium, cathode materials, separators capture growth from industrial battery displacement
- **Neutral on battery manufacturers:** Established players (Johnson Controls, Exide) manage decline but lack Li-ion pivot credibility

**For Policymakers:**
- **Accelerate EV adoption:** Each EV eliminates 11.5 kg annual lead consumption, reducing environmental/health risks
- **Support recycling infrastructure:** Maintain lead battery collection rates >95% to prevent illegal dumping as economics weaken
- **Phase out lead exemptions:** Eliminate regulatory exemptions for lead in automotive (currently allowed under RoHS due to lack of alternatives—no longer true)
