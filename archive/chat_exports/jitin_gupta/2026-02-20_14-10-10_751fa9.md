## Methodology: Lead-Acid Displacement Rates by Segment

Lead demand is calculated using a **dual-disruption framework**: (1) ICE fleet decline driven by EV adoption S-curves, and (2) lithium-ion 12V battery substitution within the remaining ICE/PHEV fleet.

### Core Methodology

1. **Tipping Point Detection**: Price parity (B2C) or cost parity (B2B) triggers adoption.
2. **Adoption Model**: Logistic S-curve fitted to historical data (10% to 80% over 10 years post-tipping).
3. **Fleet Stock-Flow**: Sales + retirements → fleet size → replacement demand.
4. **Lead Content**: 11.0 kg per passenger car SLI battery, 21.13 kg per commercial vehicle SLI battery, varies by segment.
5. **Replacement Rates**: 25%/year (SLI, 4-year life), 20%/year (E2W/E3W, 5-year life), 14.3%/year (forklift, 7-year life).

## 12V Auxiliary Lithium-Ion Substitution Impact

1. **SLI (Starter-Lighting-Ignition) in ICE passenger cars**: Li-ion SLI share rises from 0% (2024) to 71.8% (2040), reducing lead demand by 3,795.8 kt.
2. **Auxiliary batteries in BEV/PHEV**: Li-ion auxiliary share rises from 0% (2024) to 71.8% (2040), limiting lead-acid auxiliary growth despite EV fleet expansion.
3. **Starter batteries in ICE two-wheelers/three-wheelers**: Li-ion starter share rises from 0% (2024) to 32.2% (2040), with lower ceiling (~30–50%) due to cost sensitivity in emerging markets.

The **dual-disruption mechanism** (ICE fleet decline + Li-ion 12V substitution) drives 12V battery lead demand from 7,853.2 kt (2024) to 1,532.6 kt (2040), an 80% reduction.
