## Economic Collapse Driven by Lithium-Ion Disruption

Lead demand faces systemic collapse as lithium-ion batteries disrupt every major application segment. Global lead demand reached 12,259 kt in 2024, with 62.5% (7,665 kt) tied to vehicle applications—starter batteries for ICE vehicles and replacement batteries for the existing fleet. This concentration creates a single point of failure: as BEV adoption accelerates, lead-acid battery demand collapses.

**2024 Demand Breakdown:**
- Vehicle replacement batteries: 5,854 kt (47.8%)
- Vehicle sales batteries: 1,811 kt (14.8%)
- Industrial stationary (UPS, telecom): 1,987 kt (16.2%)
- Industrial motive (forklifts): 913 kt (7.4%)
- Non-battery uses: 1,691 kt (13.8%)

## Disruption Timeline

BEV market share follows an S-curve trajectory, reaching 20% of new sales in 2025, 55% by 2028, and 95% by 2034. Fleet turnover lags sales by approximately 8 years, but the impact on lead demand is immediate and accelerating.

## Segment-Specific Collapse Dynamics

**Vehicle Applications (87% decline by 2036):**
BEVs eliminate lead-acid starter batteries entirely. The 12V auxiliary battery market transitions to lithium-ion for weight, energy density, and integration advantages. Replacement demand collapses as the ICE fleet shrinks from ~1.5 billion vehicles globally to under 200 million by 2035.

**Industrial Motive Power (85% decline by 2036):**
Forklifts and material handling equipment transition to lithium-ion, driven by total cost of ownership advantages: faster charging, longer cycle life, and higher energy efficiency. Lithium-ion adoption in forklifts reaches 80%+ by 2030.

**Industrial Stationary (55% decline by 2036):**
UPS, telecom backup, and grid storage applications shift to lithium-ion. Lead-acid retains limited cost advantage in short-duration backup, but this erodes as lithium-ion costs fall below $100/kWh and continue declining.

**Non-Battery Uses (stable):**
Construction, radiation shielding, and other non-battery applications remain stable at ~1,700-1,800 kt/year, growing modestly at 0.5% annually.

## Conclusion

Global lead supply exceeds demand by more than 10% in **2026**, driven by the rapid collapse of vehicle-related demand as BEV adoption accelerates.
