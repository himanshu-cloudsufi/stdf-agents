## Methodology

This analysis uses the canonical lithium-ion-demand skill to model lead-acid battery demand across all segments: SLI automotive (passenger cars and commercial vehicles), VRLA datacenter/telecom UPS, LAB two-wheelers/three-wheelers/forklifts, and EV auxiliary batteries. ## Answer: 2031

Global lead demand reaches 6,722.1 kt in 2031 [C2], representing a 19.2% reduction [C3] from the 2026 baseline of 8,319.6 kt [C1]. This exceeds the 10% reduction threshold (7,487.7 kt target) by 765.6 kt.

## Primary Driver: ICE Passenger Car Displacement

The decline is driven overwhelmingly by the collapse of ICE passenger car SLI batteries:

- **2026 SLI car lead demand:** 2,937.9 kt [C4]
- **2031 SLI car lead demand:** 1,665.6 kt [C5]
- **Decline:** 1,272.3 kt (43.3% reduction) [C6]

This segment accounts for 73% of total battery lead decline (2026→2031). The mechanism is dual:

1. **ICE fleet reduction:** Global ICE passenger fleet declines 17.5% [C7] as BEV adoption accelerates (China 78% BEV share by 2031, USA 49%, Europe 63%, Rest of World 61%).
2. **Li-ion SLI substitution:** Li-ion SLI battery penetration reaches 21-39% by 2031 (USA/Europe lead at 39%, China/Rest of World at 21%), further reducing lead-acid demand within the remaining ICE fleet.

## Secondary Contributors

- **SLI Commercial (ICE+NGV):** 532.2 kt decline (31% of battery decline)
- **Forklifts:** 102 kt decline (6% of battery decline)
- **EV Auxiliary (LAB):** +198 kt increase (offsets decline; BEV/PHEV fleet growth drives LAB auxiliary demand before Li-ion auxiliary adoption accelerates post-2031)

## Non-Battery Lead Demand

Non-battery uses (industrial, construction, ammunition) grow modestly at ~1.5% annually, rising from 1,749 kt (2026) to 1,885 kt (2031). This growth partially offsets battery decline but does not alter the 2031 crossover.
