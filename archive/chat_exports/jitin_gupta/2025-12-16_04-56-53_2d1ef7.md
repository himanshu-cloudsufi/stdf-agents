- Heavy regulatory friction, organizational inertia
- ~3 million job-equivalents at risk in covered sectors

**Base Scenario:** +2.9 points (3.7% → 6.6% unemployment)  
- 8% actual adoption of automatable cognitive hours
- Typical enterprise lag (12-18 months post cost parity)
- ~6 million job-equivalents at risk in covered sectors

**Fast Scenario:** +5.5 points (3.7% → 9.2% unemployment)
- 15% actual adoption of automatable cognitive hours
- Aggressive tech/finance sector adoption, AI exceeds capability expectations
- ~11 million job-equivalents at risk in covered sectors

### Key Drivers:

**Cost Parity Already Achieved:** AI hit cost parity with human labor in 2024 at $0.50/M tokens. By 2026, costs project to $0.15-0.20/M tokens—making AI 60-70% cheaper than already-competitive levels.

**High-Risk Jobs (2.8M+ each):**
- Customer Service Representatives (95% cognitive, very high risk)
- Secretaries/Administrative Assistants (87% cognitive, high risk)
- Bookkeeping/Accounting Clerks (87% cognitive, high risk)

**Cognitive Task Exposure:** 79.3% of hours in covered occupations are cognitive tasks—directly automatable by current AI without requiring robotics.

### Critical Limitations:

This analysis covers 186 occupations (93M workers, ~60% of US workforce). The extrapolation to national unemployment rates requires assumptions about uncovered sectors, reabsorption rates (assumed 50%), and net job creation—all highly uncertain. **Displacement ≠ unemployment**: workers may shift roles, reduce hours, or exit the labor force rather than become unemployed.

2026 is very near-term (14 months out). Enterprise adoption typically lags technical capability by 12-24 months, meaning we're still in early majority phase. The wide scenario range (1.5-5.5 points) reflects genuine uncertainty in adoption speed, not data quality issues.
