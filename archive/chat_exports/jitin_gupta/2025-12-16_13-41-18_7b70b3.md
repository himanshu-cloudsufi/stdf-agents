## 🔍 Marginal Cost Sources & Methodology

### Primary Sources:

1. **Carbon Tracker LRMC** (Long-Run Marginal Cost)
   - Most comprehensive for USA and Germany
   - Includes full cost stack: fuel + variable O&M + fixed O&M + carbon
   - Updated annually 2020-2025

2. **EIA Henry Hub** (USA Natural Gas Prices)
   - Benchmark for USA natural gas spot prices
   - Used in variable cost calculations
   - Real-time trading hub in Louisiana

3. **TTF Index** (Title Transfer Facility - European Gas Hub)
   - Benchmark for European natural gas prices
   - Netherlands-based virtual trading point
   - Primary price reference for EU gas markets

4. **EU ETS** (European Union Emissions Trading System)
   - Carbon pricing for Germany calculations
   - Historical prices: €25/tCO2 (2020) → €80/tCO2 (2022) → €65/tCO2 (2024)
   - Adds ~$30-90/MWh to gas generation costs in Germany

5. **NREL ATB** (National Renewable Energy Laboratory - Annual Technology Baseline)
   - O&M cost assumptions
   - Heat rate assumptions for efficiency calculations

6. **Enerdata**
   - LNG trade balance data
   - Cross-regional commodity flows

7. **Energy Institute Statistical Review of World Energy**
   - Historical LNG import/export volumes
   - Formerly BP Statistical Review

## ⚠️ Critical Methodological Notes

### For Disruption Analysis (Per Tony Seba Framework):

1. **Use MARGINAL COST, not LCOE** for incumbent fossil fuels
   - LCOE includes sunk capital costs (irrelevant for displacement timing)
   - Marginal cost = decision point for plant operations
   - When SWB LCOE < Gas Marginal Cost → Displacement begins

2. **Germany 2022 Gas Crisis Visible in Data**
   - Gas LRMC spiked to $323/MWh (2022) due to Russian pipeline cutoff
   - LNG imports jumped from 0 (2015-2022) to 6.5 BCM (2023)
   - Shows vulnerability of pipeline-dependent systems

3. **LNG Displacement Priority** (Europe)
   - Cost-curve logic dictates order:
     1. **US LNG** displaced first (highest cost: liquefaction $5-7/MMBtu + shipping $2-3/MMBtu)
     2. **Qatar LNG** displaced second (lower extraction cost, but long shipping)
     3. **Norwegian Pipeline** displaced last (no liquefaction, short distance, lowest delivered cost)

4. **USA Transition from LNG Importer to Exporter**
   - 2013: 2.74 BCM imports
   - 2016: Net exporter transition
   - 2024: 123.63 BCM exports (world's largest LNG exporter)

## Variable Cost Methodology: How We Calculate Natural Gas & Coal Operating Costs

### Core Formula

Variable cost represents the **short-run marginal cost** of operating existing power plants:

**Variable_Cost = Fuel_Cost + Variable_O&M + Carbon_Cost**

This excludes capital costs (which are sunk) and focuses on the incremental cost of producing each MWh.

---

## Component-by-Component Breakdown

### 1. Fuel Cost Calculation

**Natural Gas (CCGT):**
```
Fuel_Cost = Gas_Price ($/MMBtu) × Heat_Rate (MMBtu/MWh)
```

**Coal:**
```
Fuel_Cost = Coal_Price ($/ton) / Heat_Content (MMBtu/ton) × Heat_Rate (MMBtu/MWh)
```

### 2. Variable O&M
- **Natural Gas:** $3/MWh (low maintenance, automated)
- **Coal:** $5/MWh (ash removal, scrubbers, fuel handling)

### 3. Carbon Cost (where applicable)
```
Carbon_Cost = Emission_Factor (tCO2/MWh) × Carbon_Price ($/tCO2)
```

## Variable Cost vs. LRMC: Critical Distinction

The database contains **two separate cost datasets** for different use cases:

### **Variable_Cost.json** (This question's focus)
```
Variable Cost = Fuel + Variable O&M + Carbon
```
- **Use case:** Dispatch modeling, short-term operations, merit order
- **Question answered:** "Should this plant run today?"
- **Excludes:** Fixed O&M, capital costs

### **Marginal_Cost.json** (LRMC - Used for tipping analysis)
```
LRMC = Fuel + Variable O&M + Fixed O&M + Carbon
```
- **Use case:** Tipping point analysis, investment decisions
- **Question answered:** "Should we keep this plant operating long-term?"
- **Includes:** Fixed O&M (~$10-20/MWh additional)
- **Source:** Carbon Tracker LRMC database

**Why the distinction matters:** In tipping point analysis, we use LRMC because it represents the cost threshold where fossil plants become uneconomic to *keep running*, which requires covering fixed costs like maintenance contracts, staffing, and property taxes.

Variable cost represents the **floor price** at which incumbents can operate:

**Key Disruption Threshold:** When SWB stack cost crosses below variable cost of gas/coal, those plants become economically irrational to dispatch even 1 hour/year. This is the "death spiral" moment.
