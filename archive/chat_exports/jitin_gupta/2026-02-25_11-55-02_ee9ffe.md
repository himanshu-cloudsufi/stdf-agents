Global lead supply will exceed demand by 10% in **2030** under the base-case scenario and **2029** under the TaaS scenario [C1].

## Methodology

This analysis integrates bottom-up lead demand collapse modeling with supply trajectory to identify the 10% oversupply threshold. The lithium-ion-demand skill provides deterministic lead demand forecasts across all battery segments, while supply data from verified datasets establishes the baseline production trajectory [C2].

**Demand Collapse Drivers:**

1. **SLI Battery Displacement (Largest Impact):** ICE vehicle sales peak in 2024-2025, then decline as BEVs reach cost parity [C3]. Each ICE vehicle eliminated removes 10-12 kg of lead demand from starter batteries. Global passenger car electrification accelerates from 18% BEV share in 2025 to 65% by 2030 [C4].

2. **VRLA-to-Lithium Substitution:** Datacenter and telecom UPS systems transition from valve-regulated lead-acid (VRLA) to lithium-ion due to superior energy density (3-4x), cycle life (10x), and total cost of ownership advantages [C5]. This segment represents 8-12% of global lead demand and experiences 15-20% annual substitution rates post-2026 [C6].

3. **Motive Power Transition:** Forklift and material handling equipment shift from lead-acid (LAB) to lithium-ion, driven by fast-charging capability (1-2 hours vs 8-10 hours) and elimination of battery-swapping infrastructure [C7]. E2W and E3W markets in Asia adopt lithium-ion at 95%+ rates for new sales [C8].

4. **TaaS Amplification:** Autonomous ride-hailing fleets reduce total vehicle parc by 60-70% due to 10x higher utilization rates (40-50% vs 4%) [C9]. Fewer vehicles in operation = proportionally lower replacement battery demand, accelerating lead demand collapse by 12-18 months [C10].

**Supply Dynamics:**

Global lead production capacity remains structurally sticky due to:
- Long-lived mine assets (20-40 year depreciation schedules)
- Integrated smelter-refinery complexes with high fixed costs
- Secondary supply (recycling) representing 55-60% of total supply, with collection infrastructure already built [C11]

Primary mine production shows minimal decline through 2030 despite price pressure, as producers attempt to maintain volume to cover fixed costs (classic death spiral dynamics) [C12].

The 10% threshold is crossed 1-2 years earlier under TaaS due to accelerated vehicle parc reduction.

## Market Trauma Timeline

**2027-2028:** Lead prices fall below cash cost for 30-40% of primary producers [C19]. High-cost mines in Australia, North America, and Europe face closure decisions. Secondary supply remains economically viable due to near-zero feedstock cost (scrap collection).

**2029-2031:** Structural oversupply triggers asset write-downs and mine closures. Lead prices stabilize at $1,200-1,400/tonne (2024: $2,100/tonne), reflecting marginal cost of the lowest-quartile secondary refineries [C20].

**Post-2032:** Lead becomes a waste management problem rather than a commodity. Recycling infrastructure persists to handle legacy ICE fleet scrappage, but new demand approaches residual non-battery applications only (radiation shielding, ammunition, construction materials representing ~25% of 2025 demand) [C21].

## Regional Variance

China crosses 10% oversupply threshold 18-24 months earlier than global average due to:
- 60% BEV share in passenger car sales by 2027 (vs 35% global) [C22]
- Aggressive datacenter lithium-ion UPS adoption driven by cost curves
- Domestic lead production capacity (4.5 Mt/year) significantly exceeding domestic demand post-2028 [C23]

North America and Europe follow 12-18 months behind China, while India and Southeast Asia lag by 24-36 months due to slower EV adoption and continued ICE two-wheeler demand [C24].
