## Wright's Law Cost Dynamics

Lithium-ion battery pack costs follow Wright's Law with ~20% cost reduction per cumulative doubling of production. Lead-acid costs are commodity-driven (lead metal price) with no learning curve. Lead metal prices fluctuated $450–$2,600/t (1998–2024) with no downward trend. Lead-acid battery pack costs declined modestly from $250/kWh (2010) to $130/kWh (2025) in China, driven by manufacturing efficiency, not material cost reduction.

Lithium-ion pack costs reached $84/kWh in China (2024), crossing TCO parity with lead-acid in B2B applications (forklifts, UPS) where cycle life and energy density dominate economics. B2C applications (automotive SLI) reach price parity 2029–2031 as lithium-ion costs drop below $60/kWh.

## Scope and Methodology

**Geographic scope:** Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World aggregated).

**Included applications:** Automotive SLI (ICE/PHEV passenger cars and commercial vehicles), 12V auxiliary batteries (BEV/PHEV), starter batteries (2W/3W ICE), traction batteries (E2W/E3W with lead-acid chemistry), forklifts, datacenter UPS (VRLA), telecom UPS (VRLA).
