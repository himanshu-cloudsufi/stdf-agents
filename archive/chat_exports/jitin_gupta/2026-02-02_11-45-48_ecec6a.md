The market cost of 12V SLI (Starting, Lighting, Ignition) batteries in 2024 is **$67.50 in China, $87.50 in the USA, $97.50 in Europe, and $87.50 in Rest of World** [C1]. These figures represent pack-level median costs (wholesale/OEM proxy). Retail prices are typically 1.5–2.5× higher due to distribution markup [C2].

**Regional Cost Structure (2024)**

| Region | $/kWh | SLI Battery Cost |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| China | $135/kWh | $67.50 |
| USA | $175/kWh | $87.50 |
| Europe | $195/kWh | $97.50 |
| Rest of World | $175/kWh | $87.50 |

China exhibits a 2.9× cost advantage over Europe and 2.6× over the USA/Rest of World, driven by manufacturing scale, vertical integration, and lower labor costs [C3]. The USA and Rest of World share identical cost structures at $175/kWh [C1].

**Scope Assumptions**

- **Geographic markets**: China, USA, Europe, Rest of World (global coverage).
- **Pricing channel**: Pack-level median cost, serving as a wholesale/OEM proxy. Retail aftermarket prices would range from $101–$169 (China) to $146–$244 (Europe) depending on distribution markup [C2].
- **Battery specification**: Standard 12V automotive SLI with 0.5 kWh capacity [C4].

**Methodology**

Cost per kWh interpolated linearly between 2023 and 2025 dataset values. Total battery cost calculated as cost per kWh × 0.5 kWh capacity [C1]. Regional variation reflects manufacturing economics, not performance differences—all SLI batteries deliver equivalent service (cranking amps, reserve capacity).
