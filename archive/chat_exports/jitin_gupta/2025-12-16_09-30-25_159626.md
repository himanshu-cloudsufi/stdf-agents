Analysis of converging technologies driving lead demand disruption.

#### Key Converging Technologies
**1. Lithium-ion Battery Technology**
- Cost trajectory: $1,100/kWh (2010) → $150/kWh (2020) → $85/kWh (2024) → ~$50/kWh (2030)
- Energy density improvements enable smaller, lighter auxiliary batteries in EVs
- Manufacturing scale economies from EV traction battery production benefit 12V auxiliary batteries

**2. Electric Powertrain Architecture**
- BEVs eliminate large SLI battery requirements (no engine cranking needed)
- 12V auxiliary systems support electronics, HVAC, safety (only 9 kg lead vs 11.5 kg ICE)
- Integrated battery management systems enable longer replacement cycles

**3. Industrial Battery Displacement**
- Datacenter UPS: TCO parity reached ~2022-2023 (Li-ion 12-year life vs VRLA 5-year)
- Forklift motive power: Li-ion opportunity charging eliminates battery swap infrastructure
- Telecom backup: Li-ion thermal performance advantages in distributed sites

#### Disruption Tipping Points
**Passenger Vehicle SLI:** Peak 2024 (ICE fleet peaks → replacement demand plateaus)
**Industrial Stationary:** Declining 2023+ (datacenter TCO parity drives adoption)
**Industrial Motive:** Declining 2023+ (forklift electrification + Li-ion displacement)

The convergence of EV adoption (reducing lead content per vehicle) and Li-ion cost parity (displacing industrial lead-acid) created a **dual headwind** that drove the 2023 global peak. This is structural, not cyclical—the installed base of lead-intensive ICE vehicles is now in managed decline.
