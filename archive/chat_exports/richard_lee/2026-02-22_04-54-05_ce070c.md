## Methodology & Service-Unit Economics

### 1. TaaS Impact on Parking & Vehicle-Related Commercial Space

**Service Unit**: $/passenger-mile; Asset utilization rate

**Economics**: Private vehicles operate at 4% utilization (parked 96% of time). TaaS vehicles operate at 40–50% utilization. A TaaS fleet requires **10x fewer vehicles** to deliver the same mobility service. NYC's ~2 million registered vehicles will collapse to ~200,000 autonomous electric vehicles by 2035.

**Parking Infrastructure Redundancy**:
- NYC contains approximately **3,000–3,500 parking garages and surface lots** totaling ~50–60 million square feet of commercial parking space.
- TaaS eliminates 90% of parking demand (vehicles no longer idle; continuous utilization).
- **Redundant parking space: 45–54 million sq ft** (~8–10% of NYC's ~550 million sq ft commercial real estate inventory).

**Vehicle-Related Commercial Space**:
- Auto dealerships, repair shops, gas stations, and parts retailers occupy an additional ~15–20 million sq ft in NYC.
- TaaS + EV convergence eliminates dealership model (fleet procurement), reduces repair infrastructure by 70% (EV powertrains have 90% fewer moving parts), and eliminates gas stations.
- **Redundant vehicle-service space: 12–16 million sq ft** (~2–3% of commercial inventory).

**TaaS Subtotal**: **57–70 million sq ft redundant** (10–13% of NYC commercial real estate).

### 2. Superpower (Stellar Energy) Impact on Centralized Utility Infrastructure

**Service Unit**: $/kWh delivered; Grid transmission cost vs self-generation cost

**Economics**: Distributed solar + battery systems reach **God Parity** when rooftop self-generation cost falls below grid transmission cost alone. NYC's high real estate density delays rooftop solar penetration relative to suburban markets, but commercial buildings (flat roofs, high daytime demand) achieve parity by 2028–2030.

**Centralized Utility Footprint**:
- Con Edison operates ~60 substations, switching stations, and distribution facilities in NYC, occupying ~8–12 million sq ft of commercial/industrial real estate.
- Distributed energy architecture (rooftop solar + building-level battery storage) reduces reliance on centralized grid infrastructure by 40–60% by 2036 as commercial buildings defect from the grid.
- Substations sized for peak demand become oversized as distributed generation flattens load curves.

**Redundant Utility Infrastructure**: **3–7 million sq ft** (~0.5–1.3% of commercial inventory).

**Note**: This estimate is conservative; it excludes potential redundancy in office HVAC infrastructure (Superpower enables hyper-efficient heat pumps and localized thermal storage, reducing mechanical room footprints by 20–30% in new builds, but existing stock retrofit is slow).

**Superpower Subtotal**: **3–7 million sq ft redundant** (0.5–1.3% of NYC commercial real estate).

### 3. Precision Fermentation Impact on Food Production & Distribution Infrastructure

**Service Unit**: $/kg protein; Land efficiency (kg protein per hectare)

**Economics**: Precision Fermentation (PF) produces protein at **100x greater land efficiency** than livestock and **10–25x greater feedstock efficiency**. By 2030, PF proteins reach **5x cost advantage** over animal products; by 2035, **10x cost advantage**.

**Supply Chain Collapse**:
- Animal agriculture supply chains require refrigerated warehousing, cold-chain distribution, and retail space (butchers, dairy sections, frozen aisles).
- PF products are shelf-stable powders or concentrates until final formulation, eliminating cold-chain requirements.
- NYC's food distribution infrastructure includes ~40–50 million sq ft of warehousing and ~30–40 million sq ft of grocery/restaurant space dedicated to animal products.

**Redundant Warehouse Space**:
- Cold-chain warehousing for dairy, meat, and eggs: ~15–20 million sq ft.
- PF disrupts dairy first (ingredients market: protein powders, cheese, yogurt), then ground beef. By 2035, 70–80% of animal-product cold-chain warehousing is redundant.
- **Redundant warehouse space: 10–16 million sq ft** (~2–3% of commercial inventory).

**Redundant Retail Space**:
- Grocery stores allocate ~30–40% of floor space to animal products (meat, dairy, eggs).
- Restaurants allocate ~50–60% of kitchen/storage to animal-product prep and refrigeration.
- PF products require 60% less retail refrigeration space (concentrated formulations; longer shelf life).
- **Redundant retail/restaurant space: 8–12 million sq ft** (~1.5–2% of commercial inventory).

**Precision Fermentation Subtotal**: **18–28 million sq ft redundant** (3.5–5% of NYC commercial real estate).
