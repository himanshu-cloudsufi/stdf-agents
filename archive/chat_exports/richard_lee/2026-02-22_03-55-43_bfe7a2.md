**Methodology for Curtailment Identification**

Curtailment occurs when:
1. **Instantaneous solar generation exceeds instantaneous demand + transmission export capacity + storage absorption capacity**
2. **Grid operators lack sufficient flexible load or dispatchable generation to balance the system**
3. **Transmission bottlenecks prevent solar-rich regions from exporting surplus to demand centers**

Regions experiencing curtailment share these characteristics:
- High solar penetration (>15-20% of annual generation)
- Limited battery storage deployment
- Insufficient transmission capacity between generation and load centers
- Inflexible baseload generation (coal, nuclear) that cannot ramp down during solar peaks
- Weak interconnection with neighboring grids
