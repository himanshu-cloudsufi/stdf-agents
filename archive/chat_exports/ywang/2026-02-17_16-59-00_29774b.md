**Part 2: Cost-Curve Methodology for AI Connectivity Infrastructure**

**Applicable Framework: Modified Experience Curve (Hybrid Model)**

AI connectivity infrastructure exhibits **three simultaneous cost-reduction mechanisms** that require a hybrid approach:

**1. Manufacturing Learning (Classical Experience Curve)**

Networking ASICs (switches, NICs, transceivers) follow semiconductor manufacturing learning:

• **Observed trajectory:** NVIDIA's Mellanox InfiniBand cost per Gbps dropped 60% from 2020-2024 as cumulative shipments doubled.

• **Mechanism:** Process node shrinks (7nm → 5nm → 3nm), yield improvements, and packaging optimization (CoWoS, chiplet architectures).

• **Learning rate:** 18-22% cost reduction per doubling of cumulative production volume, consistent with TSMC/Samsung fab learning curves.

**2. Architectural Innovation (Step-Function Improvements)**

AI-specific interconnects achieve cost/performance gains via architectural breakthroughs:

• **NVLink evolution:** NVLink 2.0 (300 GB/s, 2018) → NVLink 4.0 (1.8 TB/s, 2024) delivered 6x bandwidth at 2.5x cost, not via learning but via SerDes redesign and switch topology changes.

• **Optical interconnects:** Co-packaged optics (CPO) will reduce transceiver costs 40-50% by 2027 by eliminating discrete packaging, a one-time architectural shift independent of volume.

• **Implication:** Cost curves exhibit **discontinuous jumps** at architectural transitions, not smooth exponential decline.

**3. Ecosystem Scale Effects (Network Externalities)**

AI connectivity costs decline as the **installed base of compatible infrastructure** grows:

• **Standardization:** Ethernet's 800G adoption (IEEE 802.3ck, 2024) triggered 30% cost reduction as multiple vendors (Broadcom, Marvell, Cisco) competed on interoperable silicon.

• **Software leverage:** NVIDIA's NCCL (collective communication library) optimization reduced effective cost-per-operation by 40% from 2022-2024 with zero hardware changes, purely via algorithmic improvements exploiting existing bandwidth.

• **Mechanism:** Larger ecosystems amortize R&D across more units and enable software-driven efficiency gains.

**Recommended Model: Segmented Hybrid Curve**

**Cost per Gbps = f(Cumulative Volume, Architecture Generation, Ecosystem Maturity)**

**Segment 1 — Proprietary Interconnects (NVLink, Infinity Fabric):**

• **Curve type:** Steep initial learning (25-30% per doubling) due to single-vendor optimization and tight hardware-software co-design.

• **Constraint:** Volume ceiling at ~5M units/year (GPU attach rate limits), preventing commodity-scale learning.

• **Projection:** NVLink cost per TB/s drops 70% by 2030 but remains 3-5x more expensive than Ethernet per raw Gbps due to volume asymmetry.

**Segment 2 — AI-Optimized Ethernet (Spectrum-X, Ultra Ethernet Consortium):**

• **Curve type:** Moderate learning (18-20% per doubling) with step-functions at 800G (2024), 1.6T (2026), 3.2T (2028) transitions.

• **Advantage:** Leverages existing Ethernet ecosystem (100M+ ports/year) for faster cost decline than proprietary fabrics.

• **Projection:** 800G Ethernet cost per Gbps reaches parity with 400G InfiniBand by 2027, triggering substitution in all but latency-critical workloads.

**Segment 3 — Optical Interconnects (Silicon Photonics, CPO):**

• **Curve type:** Nascent (2024-2027) with 40-50% annual cost reduction as production scales from <1M to >10M units/year, then converging to 15-18% learning rate post-2028.

• **Catalyst:** Co-packaged optics eliminates transceiver discrete packaging, reducing $/Gbps by 50% in single architectural step (2026-2027).

• **Projection:** Optical interconnects become cost-competitive with copper at >10m distances by 2028, capturing 60% of AI cluster fabric by 2032.

**Why NOT Pure Wright's Law:**

Wright's Law (constant % reduction per cumulative doubling) **underestimates** AI connectivity cost decline because:

1. **Architectural discontinuities** (CPO, chiplet disaggregation) create step-function improvements independent of volume.

2. **Software leverage** (NCCL, RDMA optimization) reduces effective cost without hardware changes, violating Wright's hardware-production assumption.

3. **Ecosystem convergence** (Ethernet standardization) accelerates cost decline beyond single-vendor learning by enabling multi-vendor competition and interoperability-driven volume growth.

**Conclusion:**

AI connectivity infrastructure costs decline via **hybrid dynamics**: 18-22% manufacturing learning per doubling + 40-50% architectural step-functions every 2-3 years + 20-30% ecosystem-driven efficiency gains. Model as **segmented experience curves with discrete innovation shocks**, not smooth exponentials. Proprietary fabrics (NVLink) exhibit steeper initial learning but volume-constrained ceilings; standardized Ethernet captures long-term cost leadership via ecosystem scale. Optical interconnects represent the next architectural rupture (2026-2028), delivering 50% cost reduction independent of cumulative volume.
