- **Grid stability**: Variable solar + wind + batteries provide faster frequency response than synchronous coal/gas generators

- China coal capacity additions near zero (2024-2035)
- Existing coal utilization: 50-60% (2024) → 20-30% (2030) → <10% (2035)

<STRATEGIC_IMPLICATIONS>
#### Business Model Innovations
- **Overbuild strategies**: Utilities deploying 3-5× solar capacity to maximize daytime generation, using batteries for evening peak
- **Virtual power plants**: Aggregated rooftop solar + distributed batteries competing with utility-scale generation
- **Green hydrogen**: Sub-$1.50/kg hydrogen from dedicated solar+electrolyzer plants using curtailed daytime solar

#### Risks and Opportunities

**Risks**:
- **Trade barriers**: EU/US tariffs on Chinese modules create regional price differentials but don't slow China domestic deployment
- **Technology disruption**: Perovskite tandem cells (30%+ efficiency) could accelerate cost decline beyond baseline
- **Grid integration constraints**: Transmission bottlenecks in Northwest China limiting solar deployment in high-resource regions
- **Material supply**: Polysilicon capacity needs to grow 15-20%/year to sustain module production growth

**Opportunities**:
- **Manufacturing equipment**: Chinese solar equipment manufacturers capturing 60-70% global market (screen printers, laminators, testing)
- **Recycling infrastructure**: 1-2 million tonnes/year of end-of-life modules by 2035 creating circular economy opportunity
- **Technology licensing**: TOPCon, HJT, perovskite IP licensing revenue streams for technology leaders
- **System integration**: BOS (balance of system) optimization, AI-driven O&M, blockchain-enabled peer-to-peer trading

#### Recommendations

**For Governments**:
- Accelerate transmission buildout to absorb 250+ GW annual solar additions without curtailment
- Phase out coal capacity payments and transition to capacity markets valuing flexibility (batteries, demand response)
- Establish recycling standards and EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) for end-of-life modules

**For Utilities**:
- Transition from baseload generation model to flexible portfolio (solar + wind + batteries + demand management)
- Strand coal assets early (2025-2028) rather than late (2030-2035) to minimize losses
- Invest in grid-edge technologies (smart inverters, V2G, distributed storage) to manage two-way power flows

**For Manufacturers**:
- Non-Chinese manufacturers: Focus on premium segments (building-integrated PV, specialty applications) where 20-30% cost premium acceptable
- Chinese manufacturers: Vertical integration + automation to sustain cost leadership as learning curve flattens
- Technology developers: Commercialize perovskite tandem (30%+ efficiency) or other breakthrough technologies to extend cost curve

**For Investors**:
- **Long solar manufacturing**: Chinese tier-1 manufacturers with integrated supply chains (LONGi, JinkoSolar, Trina)
- **Long enablers**: Polysilicon (Tongwei, Daqo), solar glass (Xinyi Solar), inverters (Sungrow, Huawei)
- **Short coal**: Thermal coal miners, coal power generators without diversification strategies
- **Short gas peakers**: Gas turbine manufacturers, gas-fired peaker plants facing battery competition
