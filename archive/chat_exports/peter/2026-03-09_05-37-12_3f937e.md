## 4. Float and Liquidity Risk — ADDITIVE

The IPO float is 23.575 million shares [C1] at $25/share = approximately **$589M free float** against a ~$6B market cap. This implies a free float of roughly **9–10%** of total market capitalization.

**Incremental risk mechanism:** A 9–10% free float produces:
- Wider bid-ask spreads relative to seasoned comparables (Quanta's float is ~99% of market cap)
- Higher intraday volatility per unit of news flow
- Institutional position-building constraints (large funds cannot accumulate meaningful positions without moving the price)
- Amplified downside on negative catalysts (interconnection delay disclosure, margin miss) because the float cannot absorb institutional selling

**Magnitude:** This is structurally additive. The fundamental business risks (interconnection delays, margin compression) produce the same operational outcome whether SOLV is newly listed or seasoned — but the *market impact* of those outcomes is amplified by the constrained float. A 25% revenue recognition miss in Q1 2026 would produce a larger price decline for SOLV than for an equivalent seasoned EPC contractor, purely due to float mechanics.
