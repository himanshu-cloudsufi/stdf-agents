#### Key Converging Technologies Driving Natural Gas Disruption

**Solar-Wind-Battery (SWB) Convergence:**
- **Solar PV**: Exponential cost decline (8%/year baseline) driven by module efficiency gains (25%+ cells), manufacturing scale (China 500+ GW/year capacity), and balance-of-system cost reduction
- **Wind Power**: Turbine size scaling (onshore 5-7 MW, offshore 12-15 MW), capacity factors rising (onshore 35% → 45%, offshore 50%+), repowering existing sites
- **Battery Storage**: Li-ion cost decline (10%/year) driven by EV scale (global 20+ million EVs/year by 2024), chemistry improvements (LFP, sodium-ion), and 4-8 hour duration standardization

- **Complementary Generation Profiles**: Solar peaks midday, wind peaks evening/night → combined capacity factor ~40-50% vs 15-30% individually
- **Battery Arbitrage**: Store cheap solar/wind (marginal cost ~$0/MWh) → dispatch at peak demand → effective LCOE $60-70/MWh vs gas $65-70/MWh
- **Grid Integration**: Distributed solar + centralized wind + grid-scale batteries = resilient, dispatchable system rivaling gas reliability

**Risks:**
- **Stranded Asset Exposure**: US gas power fleet ~543 GW, avg age 15 years → $200-300B book value at risk (assumes $400-600/kW valuation)

- **Decommissioning Services**: Gas plant retirements 50-100 GW by 2035 → remediation, site reuse
