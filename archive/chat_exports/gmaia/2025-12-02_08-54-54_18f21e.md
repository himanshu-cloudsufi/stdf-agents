In financial market terms, **CFD** stands for **Contract for Difference**.

## What is a CFD?

A CFD is a derivative financial instrument that allows traders to speculate on price movements of underlying assets (stocks, commodities, currencies, indices) **without actually owning the asset**.

## How it Works

- **Contract between trader and broker**: You agree to exchange the difference in the asset's price from when the contract opens to when it closes
- **Long or short**: Profit from rising prices (buy/long) or falling prices (sell/short)
- **Cash settlement**: No physical delivery of the asset—only the price difference is exchanged

## Key Characteristics

**Leverage**: Trade with a fraction of the position's total value (e.g., 10:1 leverage means controlling $10,000 worth of assets with $1,000)

**Two-way trading**: Profit potential in both rising and falling markets

**No ownership**: You don't own the underlying asset, so no voting rights or dividends (though dividend adjustments are typically made)

**Margin requirements**: Must maintain minimum account balance; losses can exceed initial deposit

## Risks

- **High leverage amplifies losses**: Can lose more than initial capital
- **Counterparty risk**: Contract is with broker, not exchange-traded
- **Overnight financing costs**: Holding positions beyond one day incurs interest charges
- **Volatile instruments**: Rapid price movements can trigger margin calls

CFDs are popular for short-term trading and hedging but are **banned in some jurisdictions** (e.g., United States for retail traders) due to high risk.

## Key Differences: CFD vs. Actual Stock Trading

**1. Capital Requirements**
- CFD: Trade with 5-20% of position value (leverage)
- Stock: Pay 100% of position value (or 50% margin in some markets)

**2. Leverage**
- CFD: High leverage (5:1 to 30:1 typical, sometimes higher)
- Stock: Limited or no leverage (max 2:1 in US margin accounts)

**3. Counterparty**
- CFD: Contract with broker (counterparty risk exists)
- Stock: Exchange-traded, centrally cleared (broker is intermediary, not counterparty)

**4. Margin**
- CFD: Initial margin + maintenance margin required; can lose more than deposit
- Stock: Margin optional; losses limited to invested capital (unless using margin)

**5. Fees/Costs**
- CFD: Spread (bid-ask markup), overnight financing charges, guaranteed stop fees
- Stock: Brokerage commission, exchange fees (typically lower ongoing costs)

**6. Ownership**
- CFD: No ownership, no voting rights, no direct dividends (adjustments made)
- Stock: Actual ownership, voting rights, receive dividends

**7. Short Selling**
- CFD: Easy to short (same process as going long)
- Stock: Harder to short (must borrow shares, not always available)

**8. Tax Treatment**
- CFD: Often taxed differently (e.g., capital gains vs. gambling in some jurisdictions)
- Stock: Standard capital gains treatment
