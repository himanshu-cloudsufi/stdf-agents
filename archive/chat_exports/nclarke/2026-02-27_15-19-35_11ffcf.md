**Why these work:** They invoke S-curve modeling with explicit thresholds, regional scope, and time-bound outputs. The system applies exponential adoption dynamics and X-curve incumbent decline without artificial constraints.

## 2. Cost Curve Analysis and Learning Rate Applications

**Optimal examples:**
- "Calculate the cost trajectory for grid-scale battery storage ($/kWh) from 2025-2035. Show cumulative GWh deployed and cost-curve dynamics."
- "When does TaaS cost per mile drop below $0.10? Compare to private ICE vehicle TCO."

- "Forecast lithium-ion pack cost for BEVs in China vs USA. When does China achieve $50/kWh at pack level?"

**Why these work:** They demand explicit service-unit economics, cumulative scaling, and cost-parity calculations. The system applies validated learning rates and avoids linear extrapolation.

## 3. Market Impact Assessments for Emerging Technologies

Quantify displacement, stranded assets, and second-order cascades. Specify incumbent sector, disruption driver, and impact metric (jobs lost, GW retired, Mt demand destroyed).

**Optimal examples:**
- "How many FTE jobs are displaced by AI automation in the US by June 2030? Break down by occupation group and show replacement vs productivity split."

- "Model the collapse timeline for the US dairy industry driven by precision fermentation cost parity. When do revenues fall below break-even?"

**Why these work:** They specify the disruption mechanism (cost/capability advantage), the incumbent being displaced, and the quantitative impact metric. The system models vicious cycles and does not protect incumbents from near-zero outcomes.

**Why these work:** They invoke validated skills (lithium-ion-demand, copper-forecast, energy-sector) with explicit segmentation. The system applies stock-flow accounting, replacement cycles, and convergence cascades without gap-filling.

**Optimal examples:**
- "Build a value chain analysis for the global EV battery supply chain. Compare incumbent OEMs (GM, Ford) vs disruptors (Tesla, BYD) vs enablers (CATL, Panasonic). Focus on cell manufacturing and pack integration stages."

- "Create a precision fermentation value chain for dairy protein disruption. Show incumbent dairy producers vs PF startups vs enabling biotech platforms."

**Why these work:** They specify the market focus, chain types (incumbent/disruptor/enabler), and analytical lens. The system resolves tickers, fetches financial metrics, and generates investment narratives grounded in disruption dynamics.

## 6. Methodology-Driven Scenario Modeling

**Optimal examples:**
- "Run two scenarios for US AI job displacement by 2030: (A) METR capability growth 0.5 OOMs/year, (B) 0.8 OOMs/year. Show FTE impact delta."
- "Model China grid decarbonization under two battery duration assumptions: (A) 4-hour BESS, (B) 8-hour BESS. Show coal displacement timeline and total storage GWh required."
- "Forecast global lithium-ion demand 2030 under base-case vs TaaS disruption scenario. Show passenger car segment delta and total GWh impact."
- "Sensitivity analysis: EU copper demand 2035 under (A) 50% EV penetration, (B) 80% EV penetration. Isolate transportation segment contribution."

**Why these work:** They specify the driver, the range, and the output metric. The system runs skill-based scenarios without inventing constraints or hedging conclusions.

## Query Structure Best Practices

**DO:**
- Specify region, time horizon, and output unit (GWh, kt, TWh, FTE, $/unit)
- Request segmentation (by technology, end-use, occupation, sector)
- Ask for tipping points, inflection years, and displacement timelines
- Invoke skills explicitly when known ("Use lithium-ion-demand skill for...")
- Request methodology transparency ("Show S-curve parameters and fitting")

**DO NOT:**
- Ask for "realistic" or "conservative" estimates (the system applies cost-curve dynamics, not plausibility filters)
- Request regulatory/policy lag analysis unless explicitly stated (the system excludes lags by default)
- Assume fixed demand or linear growth (the system models elastic demand and exponential dynamics)
- Ask for "balanced" scenarios or incumbent protection (the system allows near-zero outcomes when economics dictate)
