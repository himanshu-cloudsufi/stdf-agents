### Step 1 — Lead Demand Destruction by Segment

**SKILL OUTPUT (DETERMINISTIC):** Global lead-acid battery demand peaked at 9,909 kt in 2024 [C2] and enters irreversible structural decline driven by three simultaneous disruption vectors.

**The double-compression mechanism in SLI passenger cars** is the dominant driver. Two S-curves operate simultaneously: (1) ICE fleet shrinkage as BEV price parity triggers adoption (China: 2022 [C3], Europe: 2024 [C4], USA: 2025 [C5]), and (2) Li-ion 12V substitution within the residual ICE fleet (tipping: USA/Europe 2031, China 2033 [C6]). The global ICE fleet shrinks from ~897 million vehicles in 2026 to ~87 million by 2040 — a 90% reduction [C7]. SLI passenger car lead demand collapses from 3,788 kt (2026) to 406 kt (2040), a 89.3% decline [C8].
