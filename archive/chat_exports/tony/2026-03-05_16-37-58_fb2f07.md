**Clarification:**

Jevons Paradox describes a phenomenon in extractive economies: when coal efficiency improved in 19th-century Britain, total coal consumption increased because the efficiency gains made coal economically viable for new applications. The paradox holds when the resource itself remains scarce and must be continuously extracted.

Stellar and digital technologies operate under fundamentally different physics:

- **X-Flow (Extractive):** Efficiency gains → increased total extraction. The resource flow never ends. Jevons Paradox applies here.
