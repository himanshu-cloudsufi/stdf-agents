The global LNG market faces structural collapse by 2032, driven by the economics of SWB (solar-wind-battery) systems displacing natural gas in power generation and accelerating electrification eliminating gas demand in transport, heating, and industrial processes.

## Baseline vs Disruption Trajectories

Mainstream forecasts project global LNG trade growing from ~400 MTPA (2023) to 550-600 MTPA by 2032, driven by Asia-Pacific import growth and coal-to-gas switching. This trajectory assumes gas remains competitive in power generation and heating.

The disruption scenario shows LNG demand peaking in 2025-2027 at ~450 MTPA, then declining to 280-320 MTPA by 2032 (-30% to -38% from peak). This reflects:

1. **Power Generation Displacement**: SWB systems achieve cost dominance over gas peaking plants by 2026-2027 in major markets (China, Europe, USA). Gas capacity factors collapse from 40-50% to 15-25% as SWB penetration exceeds 50% of generation. Merit order economics force gas plants into backup-only roles.

2. **Electrification Acceleration**: Heat pumps displace gas heating (residential/commercial), EVs eliminate LNG as transport fuel (trucks, shipping), and industrial process heat electrifies where SWB provides <$20/MWh power.

3. **Stranded Infrastructure**: $400-600 billion in planned LNG export terminals, import terminals, and pipelines become stranded assets as demand collapses before projects reach full utilization.

**Volume Traded (MTPA)**:
- Baseline (no disruption): 550-600 MTPA

- Decline driver: Gas generation displaced by SWB + electrification of heating/transport

- Utilization rate: 40-50% (disruption scenario) vs 80-85% (baseline)
- Stranded capacity: 350-400 MTPA of liquefaction terminals operating below breakeven

## Mechanism: The Gas Death Spiral

Natural gas in power generation faces the same death spiral as coal:

1. **2024-2026**: SWB reaches cost parity with gas peaking plants in China, Europe, USA
2. **2026-2028**: SWB penetration exceeds 40% of generation; gas capacity factors drop below 30%
3. **2028-2030**: Gas plants cannot recover fixed costs at <30% utilization; financial distress begins
4. **2030-2032**: Gas generation collapses to backup-only role (<15% of grid); LNG import demand craters

Key tipping point: When SWB + 4-hour batteries cost <$40/MWh (LCOE), gas peaking at $60-80/MWh becomes economically non-viable. This threshold is crossed in major markets by 2027.

## Regional Dynamics

**China**: Largest LNG importer (2023: ~70 MTPA). Disruption scenario shows imports declining to 35-45 MTPA by 2032 as domestic solar/wind/battery buildout accelerates and coal plants are retired without gas replacement.

**Europe**: Second largest importer (2023: ~120 MTPA post-Russia pivot). Imports decline to 60-80 MTPA by 2032 as offshore wind + North Sea wind + batteries displace gas generation and heat pumps replace gas heating.

**USA**: Net exporter (2023: ~90 MTPA exports). Export capacity expands to 180-200 MTPA by 2032, but utilization collapses to 40-50% as Asian and European buyers cancel long-term contracts.

- **2027-2028**: LNG spot prices collapse below $5/MMBtu due to oversupply; marginal projects shut in
- **2029-2030**: Major LNG exporters (Qatar, Australia, USA) face stranded asset write-downs; credit downgrades begin
- **2031-2032**: LNG market stabilizes at 50-60% of peak size, serving only backup generation and industrial feedstock (petrochemicals, fertilizer)

The global LNG market by 2032 will be 280-320 MTPA in volume and $110-160 billion in value under disruption dynamics, representing a 30-40% collapse from 2025-2027 peak levels. The market will not grow to mainstream projections of 550-600 MTPA because the economic foundation (gas competitiveness in power generation) is eliminated by SWB cost curves. Infrastructure capacity will exceed demand by 2:1, creating the largest stranded asset event in energy history. LNG's role contracts to backup generation (<10% of electricity) and non-substitutable industrial feedstock, not growth fuel for Asia-Pacific development.

This is not a prediction—it is the mathematical consequence of SWB cost curves crossing gas generation costs in the world's three largest gas markets (China, Europe, USA) between 2026-2028.
