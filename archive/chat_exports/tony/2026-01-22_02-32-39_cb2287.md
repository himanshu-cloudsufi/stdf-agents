God Parity is a critical concept in energy disruption that marks the moment when **rooftop solar electricity becomes cheaper than just the transmission and distribution costs alone** — meaning it's less expensive than the cost of simply moving electricity through wires, even if the generation itself were free.

## The Concept

At God Parity, centralized utilities face an insurmountable economic challenge: even if they could generate electricity at **zero cost** (like an act of divine intervention), they still couldn't compete with rooftop solar because the T&D infrastructure costs exceed what distributed solar provides.

## Why It Matters

This represents a structural tipping point for the electricity industry:

- **Centralized generation becomes obsolete** regardless of fuel type (coal, gas, nuclear, or even renewables)
- **Grid architecture fundamentally shifts** from centralized to distributed
- **Utility business models collapse** — the "poles and wires" infrastructure that justified monopoly status becomes economically uncompetitive
- **No path to competitiveness** — utilities can't innovate their way out since T&D costs are largely fixed

## Current Status

God Parity has already been reached in several high-electricity-cost markets:
- **California**: Residential electricity rates ~$0.25-0.35/kWh, while rooftop solar delivers ~$0.05-0.08/kWh
- **Germany**: High retail rates make rooftop solar economically superior to grid electricity
- **Australia**: Strong solar resources + high retail rates = widespread God Parity

## Implications

Once God Parity hits:
- **Death spiral accelerates** — as customers defect to rooftop solar, remaining customers face higher T&D costs per kWh, driving more defection
- **Policy battles intensify** — utilities lobby for grid connection fees, solar taxes, or mandatory grid purchases to preserve their business model
- **Decentralization inevitable** — the economic logic favors distributed generation + storage over centralized systems

This is distinct from simple **grid parity** (solar cheaper than retail electricity) — God Parity represents a deeper structural disruption where even free centralized generation can't compete with distributed solar economics.
