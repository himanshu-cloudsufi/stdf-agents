### Key Price Drivers [C1][C2]

1. **Strait of Hormuz disruption**: ~20% of global oil supply transits this chokepoint. Active disruption has pushed global shipping costs up ~30% [C1].
2. **OPEC+ production cuts**: Maintained at ~2.2 million barrels/day reduction, tightening global supply [C1].
3. **Iran conflict escalation**: US military strikes on Iran's Kharg Island (a major oil export terminal) reported by 14 March 2026 [C2].
4. **Rupee depreciation**: The INR has fallen to ₹92.42/USD [C1], amplifying import cost pressure on India's crude bill (India imports ~85% of its crude).

### Government Stance on Domestic Prices [C1]

As of 9 March 2026, the Indian government confirmed **no immediate retail price hike** despite crude crossing $100/barrel. Officials stated fuel prices will remain stable "for the time being" — absorbing the crude surge through oil marketing company margins rather than passing costs to consumers [C1].

**Implication**: If Brent sustains above $110–120/barrel, the fiscal buffer erodes rapidly. A retail price revision becomes mathematically inevitable at that level given India's ~85% crude import dependency and a weakened rupee at ₹92.42/USD.
