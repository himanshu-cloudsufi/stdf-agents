## Analysis Approach
This convergence analysis examines two parallel disruptions: (1) passenger vehicle electrification driven by battery cost curves and total cost of ownership advantages, and (2) solar-wind-battery displacement of coal-fired generation driven by marginal cost competition. The key insight is that EVs create NEW electricity demand, but this demand is increasingly met by near-zero marginal cost SWB generation rather than coal, leading to a net displacement effect despite absolute load growth.

The analysis applies cost-driven disruption logic to both value chains: EVs reach tipping points when battery pack costs decline below $100/kWh (enabling TCO parity), while SWB displaces coal when solar/wind generation costs fall below coal's marginal operating costs ($20-40/MWh). The convergence creates a structural squeeze: rising electricity demand from EVs accelerates renewable buildout, which then cannibalizes coal baseload capacity through merit-order displacement.
