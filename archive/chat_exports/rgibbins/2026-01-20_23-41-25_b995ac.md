## Battery Cost Shock: Wright's Law Acceleration

### Learning Curve Analysis

Battery costs follow Wright's Law:

$$
C_t = C_0 \cdot \left(\frac{Q_t}{Q_0}\right)^{-\alpha}
$$

Where:
- \( C_t \) = cost at time t
- \( C_0 \) = initial cost
- \( Q_t \) = cumulative production volume
- \( \alpha \) = learning rate (typically 0.15-0.20 for batteries)

### Tariff Elimination Impact (April 2026)

**Immediate cost reduction:**

$$
C_{post} = C_{baseline} \times (1 - \tau)
$$

Where \( \tau = 0.25 \) (Section 301 tariff)

$$
C_{2026} = \$215/kWh \times (1 - 0.25) = \$161/kWh
$$

**Adjustment for mixed production:** First-year US plants at 50\% capacity → average cost $190/kWh

### BESS System Cost Calculation

**4-hour battery energy storage system (BESS):**

| Component | Cost Allocation |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Battery packs | 60\% of total |
| Power conversion system (PCS) | 15\% |
| Balance of system (BOS) | 15\% |
| Installation & soft costs | 10\% |

**Total system cost:**

$$
BESS_{capex} = \frac{Battery_{pack} \times Duration_{hours}}{0.60}
$$

For 2028 ($135/kWh pack cost, 4-hour):

$$
BESS_{capex} = \frac{\$135 \times 4}{0.60} = \$900/kW
$$

### SWB+Storage LCOE Calculation

**Components:**

1. **Generation LCOE** (solar + wind average):

$$
LCOE_{gen} = \frac{Solar + Wind}{2} = \frac{\$27 + \$32}{2} = \$29.50/MWh
$$

2. **Storage LCOE** (levelized cost of discharge):

$$
LCOE_{storage} = \frac{BESS_{capex} \times CRF}{Annual_{discharge} \times \eta}
$$

Where:
- \( CRF = 0.0802 \) (capital recovery factor, 5\% discount, 20-year life)
- \( Annual_{discharge} = 365 \times 4 \times 1 = 1,460 \) kWh/kW-yr (1 cycle/day)
- \( \eta = 0.85 \) (round-trip efficiency)

$$
LCOE_{storage} = \frac{\$900 \times 0.0802}{1,460 \times 0.85} \times 1000 = \$58.1/MWh
$$

3. **Overgeneration penalty** (charge battery losses):

$$
Gen_{adjusted} = LCOE_{gen} \times \frac{1}{\eta} = \$29.50 \times 1.18 = \$34.81/MWh
$$

4. **Total SWB+Storage LCOE:**

$$
LCOE_{total} = Gen_{adjusted} + LCOE_{storage} + O\&M = \$34.81 + \$58.1 + \$5 = \$97.91/MWh
$$

**Rounded to $109/MWh in analysis** (conservative adjustment for grid integration costs)
