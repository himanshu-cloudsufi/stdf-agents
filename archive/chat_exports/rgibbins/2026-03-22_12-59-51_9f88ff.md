**The Dalio constraint is real but not binding in the base case.** The UK's current account deficit (3.2% of GDP [C1]) and above-target inflation create a sterling vulnerability that limits the BoE's easing speed. **The interaction of all five forces produces a non-linear rate path.** The Hormuz shock delays the cutting cycle in H1 2026 (hold at 4.25% in Q1). The AI displacement demand shock accelerates cuts in H2 2026 and H1 2027. The Dalio constraint caps the cutting pace at 25bp per quarter. The SWB deflation enables the final cut to 2.75% in Q4 2027. The result is a staircase descent — not a straight line — with a pause in Q3 2027 as the BoE assesses whether structural deflation has taken hold.
