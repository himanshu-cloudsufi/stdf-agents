**Asia LNG at $35–50/MMBtu adds +2.0–3.5pp to headline CPI** in Japan, South Korea, and India, where gas-fired electricity constitutes 30–45% of generation [C8].

**Europe (TTF at $35–50/MMBtu):** ECB staff already project 2026 headline inflation at 2.6% incorporating the shock [C2]. The actual shock trajectory ($35–50/MMBtu) implies upside risk to 3.0–3.8% headline if sustained through H2 2026.

**The critical constraint on pass-through:** The AI-driven demand destruction channel is simultaneously suppressing discretionary services CPI. The prior session established that UK services CPI is already in a phase transition — supply pressure at +2.95pp vs demand destruction at -0.65pp in 2026. The fossil fuel shock adds +1.5–2.5pp to goods/energy CPI but does not reverse the demand destruction in services. The result is a **bifurcated inflation profile**: energy/goods CPI spiking while services CPI is being suppressed by displacement [C9].

This bifurcation is the key analytical insight. Central banks targeting headline CPI will see a spike. Central banks looking through energy to core/services will see the demand destruction signal. The policy divergence across central banks will be driven by which signal they prioritize.

### The SWB Deflationary Constraint: Does It Limit the Inflationary Impulse?

**Yes — but with a 12–18 month lag.** The prior session established that SWB market share is shock-accelerated from 26.3% (2026) to 48.8% (2030), with the global tipping point pulled forward to 2029–2030 [C12]. The mechanism:

- **2026–2027:** SWB cannot immediately replace gas-fired generation at scale. The shock-driven energy price spike is real and inflationary. SWB deployment accelerates but the installed base cannot yet offset the supply disruption.
- **2028:** SWB at 37–42% global generation share begins structurally suppressing electricity costs. Gas-fired generation marginal cost at $80–120/MWh becomes economically irrelevant as SWB at $0–5/MWh dominates dispatch. Energy CPI begins falling structurally, not cyclically.
- **2029–2030:** SWB at 43–49% global share makes the fossil fuel shock a historical artifact. Energy CPI is structurally deflationary. The inflationary impulse from the 2026 Hormuz shock is fully absorbed and reversed by the SWB cost-curve.

**The SWB deflationary constraint does not prevent the 2026 inflation spike — it terminates it by 2028.** Central banks that hike aggressively in 2026–2027 will be hiking into a structural deflationary force that arrives with mathematical certainty in 2028. The 2022 ECB template is the warning: the ECB hiked to 4.00% and then had to cut back to 2.00% within 18 months as the structural deflationary forces (SWB + demand destruction) overwhelmed the supply-side inflation [C4][C5].
