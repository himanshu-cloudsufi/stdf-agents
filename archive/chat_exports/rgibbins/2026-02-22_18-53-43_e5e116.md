## Methodology

The artificial-labor skill models AI capability growth (7-month doubling time), AI cost decline (70% annually), and task-level automation across 341 occupations covering 143 million US workers [C3]. The model applies capability gating (AI must handle task duration), cost-parity tipping points (AI cost < 50% of human cost), and S-curve adoption schedules post-tipping [C4]. Automation is constrained to cognitive subtasks only, with an 80% replacement ceiling and 50/50 replacement-productivity split [C5].

By December 2029, the model projects **36.9 million FTE displaced** in the USA (25.8% replacement rate) [C6]. Scaling to the UK using the employment ratio (33.4M UK / 143.1M USA), this yields **8.6 million UK FTE displaced** [C7].

## Unemployment Rate Calculation

Starting from the UK baseline—33.4M employed, 1.5M unemployed, 34.9M labor force, 4.4% unemployment [C8]—and adding 8.6M displaced workers under the assumption that 100% enter unemployment with no reabsorption or new job creation, the December 2029 unemployment rate is:

**New Unemployed** = 1.5M + 8.6M = 10.1M  
**Unemployment Rate** = 10.1M / 34.9M = **29.0%** [C9]

## Critical Limitations

**This model outputs job displacement, not unemployment rate.** The 29.0% figure assumes:

1. **100% displacement-to-unemployment conversion**: All displaced workers enter unemployment immediately with no labor force reabsorption, no new job creation, and no economic growth offsetting effects [C17].
2. **USA-based model scaled to UK**: The skill uses US employment data (143M workers) and scales to the UK (33.4M) via a simple ratio, without accounting for UK-specific labor market dynamics, AI adoption rates, or policy responses [C18].
3. **Cognitive tasks only**: Physical robotics is excluded per the request. The model automates cognitive subtasks within 341 occupations but does not model physical task automation [C19].

Unemployment rate forecasting requires modeling labor force reabsorption, new job creation, wage adjustments, and policy interventions—none of which are included here [C20]. The 29.0% figure represents the **upper bound** under a zero-reabsorption scenario and should be interpreted as a displacement proxy, not a literal unemployment forecast.

## Conclusion

Under unchanged policy and a 100% displacement-to-unemployment assumption, the UK unemployment rate reaches **29.0% in December 2029**, driven by AI automation of cognitive tasks across the economy, with AV driving replacement contributing +1.0 percentage point [C21]. The actual unemployment rate will depend on labor market reabsorption dynamics, new job creation, and policy responses, which are outside the scope of this model.
