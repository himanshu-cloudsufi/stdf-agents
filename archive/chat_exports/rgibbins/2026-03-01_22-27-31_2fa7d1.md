To generate **2 GW of solar power capacity** in a high-irradiance region like Spain, you need approximately **2,000 to 2,500 hectares** (20 to 25 km²) of land.

## Methodology

The calculation is driven by three physical parameters:

**1. Solar Panel Efficiency:** Modern utility-scale solar PV modules operate at **20-22% conversion efficiency** [C1]. This analysis uses 21% as the baseline.

**2. Capacity Factor:** Spain's high-irradiance regions (Andalusia, Extremadura, Murcia) achieve capacity factors of **22-25%** [C2]. This reflects actual annual energy output relative to nameplate capacity, accounting for day/night cycles, seasonal variation, and weather.

**3. Land Use Intensity:** Utility-scale ground-mounted solar installations require **0.8 to 1.0 MW per hectare** [C3]. This accounts for:
- Panel footprint and tilt angle (typically 25-30° in Spain)
- Row spacing to minimize shading (inter-row distance ~2-3x panel height)
- Access roads, inverters, substations, and perimeter buffers
- Ground coverage ratio of 35-45%

## Calculation

For **2,000 MW (2 GW)** of installed capacity:

- At **1.0 MW/hectare**: 2,000 hectares (20 km²)
- At **0.8 MW/hectare**: 2,500 hectares (25 km²)

The range reflects site-specific factors: flatter terrain and optimized layouts approach 1.0 MW/ha, while hillier sites or those with environmental constraints trend toward 0.8 MW/ha.

## Energy Output Context

This 2 GW installation would generate approximately **4.0 to 4.4 TWh annually** [C4], calculated as:

2,000 MW × 8,760 hours/year × 23% capacity factor = 4.03 TWh/year

For reference, Spain's total electricity demand in 2025 was ~250 TWh [C5], so this installation would supply roughly **1.6% of national demand**.

## Assumptions Summary

- **Module efficiency:** 21% (monocrystalline silicon)
- **Capacity factor:** 23% (Spain high-irradiance baseline)
- **Land use intensity:** 0.8-1.0 MW/hectare
- **Technology:** Fixed-tilt ground-mounted PV (not tracking)
- **Exclusions:** Does not account for grid connection infrastructure beyond the site perimeter

The 20-25 km² land requirement is **final and non-negotiable** under these physics. Spain's solar resource and modern panel efficiency make this one of the most land-efficient solar deployments globally.
