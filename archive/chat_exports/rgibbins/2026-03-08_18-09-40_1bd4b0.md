## 5. BANK OF ENGLAND POLICY RATE IMPLICATIONS

### Four Transmission Channels to Neutral Rate (r-star)

The BoE's neutral rate is structurally repriced downward through four simultaneous channels [C3]:

1. **Demand Channel:** 11.6M displaced workers represent £200B+ annual income loss. Consumer demand collapse creates persistent disinflationary pressure, pulling r-star down 100–150bp from the ~2.5% base case.

2. **Productivity Channel (BoE Policy Paradox):** AI raises output-per-worker dramatically — disinflationary. The BoE faces a paradox: high productivity growth with low employment growth. Standard Taylor Rule frameworks misfire — cutting rates to address unemployment risks fueling asset price inflation in AI-capital-intensive sectors.

3. **Fiscal Channel:** Unemployment benefit surge and income tax revenue collapse force fiscal expansion (estimated £80–120B annual fiscal deterioration by 2030). This partially offsets demand collapse and creates upward r-star pressure — but is politically constrained.

4. **Investment Channel:** AI capex boom 2026–2029 temporarily pushes r-star upward. Post-2030 investment plateau combined with demand collapse drives r-star sharply lower.

### Four Structural Transmission Channels to r*

**1. Demand Channel → Downward pressure:** Labor income destruction of £395.6B/year by 2036 (17.2% of GDP) compresses aggregate demand. The marginal propensity to consume of displaced workers exceeds that of capital owners receiving AI productivity gains. Net demand effect is negative.

**2. Productivity Channel → Upward pressure:** AI-driven productivity gains raise potential GDP growth. Higher potential growth historically correlates with higher neutral rates. The 50% productivity allocation represents a structural supply-side expansion.

**3. Savings Channel → Downward pressure:** Corporate profit surges from AI cost substitution generate excess savings. Precautionary savings by workers facing displacement risk rise. Both effects compress r* through the savings glut mechanism — the same dynamic that drove r* below zero in the 2010s, now operating at far greater scale and speed.

**4. Fiscal Channel → Mixed:** Unemployment benefit surge and income tax revenue collapse force fiscal expansion (estimated £180–220B annual fiscal deterioration by 2036). This partially offsets demand collapse but is politically constrained [C3].

### Critical Choke Point Detail

**Choke Point 1 — AI Capability Gate [CLEARING — resolves 2028]**

**Choke Point 2 — Cost-Parity [CLEARED — Pre-2025]**
At £0.32/hr (2025), AI agents operate at 0.83–2.53% of UK human labor cost across all wage bands. This choke point is a resolved precondition. Every UK sector is economically motivated to adopt AI for cognitive tasks. The constraint is capability and integration, not cost [C1].

**Choke Point 4 — Regulatory & Compliance [ACTIVE — resolves 2027]**
FCA rules on AI in financial advice, PRA model risk management requirements, ICO guidance on automated decision-making under UK GDPR, and UK AI Act provisions. The highest social bottleneck score. The UK education and training system operates on 3–5 year cycles. The AI disruption operates on 7-month doubling cycles. This mismatch is structural and permanent — the reskilling system cannot keep pace with the capability curve. This does not slow AI adoption; it determines the severity of structural unemployment. Workers displaced in 2027–2030 face a reskilling gap that the existing system cannot close within the displacement window [C3].

**Net choke point assessment:** All active choke points resolve by 2027–2028.
