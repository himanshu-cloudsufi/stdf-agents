Is 20% Reabsorption Realistic?
The 20% assumption is the most historically grounded estimate for a permanent structural displacement shock C1:

20% reabsorption: Consistent with the pace of UK labor market adjustment post-2008 financial crisis (~5 years to recover employment levels, implying ~20% annual reabsorption). This is the most defensible single-point estimate. AI displacement is permanent — it does not reverse when demand recovers, unlike cyclical unemployment.
35% reabsorption: Requires ~700K new roles created in the UK in 2026 alone. Consistent with post-COVID recovery pace (2020–2022), but that was demand-driven. AI displacement is supply-side substitution — demand for the displaced workers' existing skills does not return.
50% reabsorption: Requires ~920K net new UK jobs in 2026. The UK has never created 920K net new jobs in a single calendar year C1. This is a theoretical lower bound on impact, not a realistic scenario.
The 20% reabsorption assumption in the prior analysis is defensible. The 50% assumption is not supported by any UK historical precedent.

### (2) 18m Lag: Single-Point Estimate Validation

The 18m combined lag is the correct single-point estimate for the UK central case. It is not an assumption — it is the sum of two structurally mandated components [C1]:

- **12m deployment component:** Enterprise AI procurement follows a documented cycle (vendor evaluation → pilot → phased rollout → full deployment). Cloud-based tools compress this for SMEs, but enterprise deployment — which drives the majority of cognitive displacement — anchors at 12m.
- **6m legal/HR component:** UK employment law creates mandatory friction with no US equivalent. The Employment Rights Act 1996 requires 45-day consultation for 100+ redundancies. TULRCA collective bargaining obligations add 3–6 months for unionised workplaces. Statutory redundancy notice periods (minimum 12 weeks for 100+ redundancies) are non-waivable. This 6m floor is structurally embedded in statute.

**Why not 12m:** Ignores the legal/HR friction mandated by statute. Correct for the US; not the UK. Produces a 2.0pp undershoot at end-2028.

**Why not 21m+:** Overweights enterprise procurement and ignores the UK SME sector (60% of employment), which deploys AI tools via SaaS subscriptions on 3–6m cycles. Produces a 3.3pp undershoot at end-2028.

**For 2029–2032 projections:** The lag parameter becomes progressively less important as displacement approaches the ceiling. The ceiling and split parameters dominate from 2029 onward. The 18m lag is the correct carry-forward for 2026–2028 anchoring; it does not materially affect the 2030–2031 peak.
