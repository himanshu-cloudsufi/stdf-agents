- **SMRs / Advanced Reactors:** Small position (5-10% of energy portfolio). Lottery ticket — if they work, 10× return. If not, write-off.

### **Short (What Batteries WILL Displace):**
- **Gas peaker plants:** The 2-4 hour "evening ramp" market is dead. California already has negative prices midday and batteries handle the evening peak.
- **Coal plants (all types):** Batteries + renewables are cheaper for *any* duration coal can serve.

**Bottom line:** I'm long nuclear for the **continuous baseload niche** (datacenters, heavy industry), not for the broader grid. Batteries will dominate <6 hour markets by 2027, but can't economically serve 24/7 load. If battery costs reach $30-40/kWh AND long-duration storage (12-24 hours) becomes viable, I'd flip bearish on nuclear.
