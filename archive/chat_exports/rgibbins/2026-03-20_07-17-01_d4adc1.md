### 1. The AI-to-Rates Transmission Mechanism — And Why It Fails in 2026

The theoretical pathway is: AI adoption → productivity surge → unit labor cost compression → disinflation → central bank rate cuts → duration rally. The mechanism is real. The timing is wrong for 2026.

PCE inflation remained near 3% year-over-year through December 2025, confirming that the H1 2025 ULC compression did not transmit to headline prices within the year [C3]. For a 2026 tactical horizon, the transmission chain from AI productivity → measured disinflation → Fed/BoE/ECB rate response is too slow and too uncertain to anchor a duration trade. The fiscal term premium, by contrast, is immediate and quantifiable.
