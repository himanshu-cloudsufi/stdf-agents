Global lead demand in 2030 will be **7,493 kt**, declining 10.7% from 8,392 kt in 2024. Battery applications drive the majority at 5,669 kt (76%), with non-battery uses (cables, ammunition, radiation shielding) contributing 1,824 kt (24%).

**Battery Lead Demand Breakdown 2030 (kt):**

1. **SLI (ICE Cars)**: 2,136 kt (37.7%) — Passenger car starter batteries decline 32% from 2024 as BEV adoption accelerates
2. **SLI (ICE+NGV Commercial)**: 1,486 kt (26.2%) — Commercial vehicle starter batteries decline 13% as electric trucks gain share
3. **Forklift LAB**: 621 kt (11.0%) — Lead-acid forklifts decline 17% as lithium-ion penetration reaches 50%+
4. **BEV/PHEV 12V Auxiliary (LAB)**: 500 kt (8.8%) — Counter-trend growth of 167% as EV fleet expands; most EVs still use lead-acid 12V auxiliary batteries
5. **E2W LAB Traction**: 363 kt (6.4%) — Electric two-wheeler lead-acid traction batteries grow 26% as E2W adoption accelerates in Asia

**Top 5 categories represent 90% of battery lead demand.**

Demand = (OEM sales × lead content) + (fleet replacement × lead content × LAB share)

Replacement rate: 25% annually (4-year battery life for automotive SLI)

**Key Drivers:**

1. **ICE fleet decline**: BEV adoption reaches 45-60% of new sales by 2030 in China, USA, Europe; ICE fleet begins absolute decline
2. **Lithium-ion substitution**: Li-ion penetration in SLI, auxiliary, and forklift applications accelerates post-2029 tipping points
3. **EV auxiliary paradox**: BEV/PHEV 12V auxiliary demand grows 167% despite most using lead-acid; lithium-ion auxiliary adoption remains <10% in 2030
4. **Commercial vehicle lag**: Commercial vehicle electrification slower than passenger cars; SLI demand declines only 13% by 2030
5. **Two-wheeler growth**: E2W LAB traction demand grows 26% as electric two-wheeler adoption accelerates in China, India, Southeast Asia; lithium-ion chemistry tipping point reached 2022-2024 but LAB remains significant

**Geographic Scope:** Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World)
