## 4. The Refinery Barrel Trap: The Critical Choke Point

A US refinery barrel yields 45% gasoline + 24% diesel = **69% road transport fuels** [C15]. When EV adoption destroys road transport demand, refineries cannot pivot to jet fuel or petrochemicals — barrel yields are fixed by crude chemistry [C16].

**The math:** Road transport oil demand destruction of 3.74 mb/d by 2036 [C17] = a **19% reduction in total US refinery throughput demand**. At that level, refinery utilization falls to ~81% nationally — below the ~85% threshold where unit economics deteriorate sharply. Refineries operating at 70–75% utilization face unit cost increases that make them uncompetitive vs. imports, triggering closures. Each closure further concentrates fixed costs on remaining refineries, accelerating the spiral.

This is the structural choke point: disrupting 56% of the barrel (road transport) destroys the economics of 100% of the refinery system.
