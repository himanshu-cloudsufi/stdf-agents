**Regional Contributions**

China dominates growth, contributing 1,296 kt (56.6% of total increase), followed by Rest of World at 544 kt (23.7%), Europe at 308 kt (13.4%), and USA at 143 kt (6.2%) [C3]. China's share reflects its continued infrastructure expansion and manufacturing dominance.

**Sectoral Breakdown**

Transportation accounts for 15.0% of total demand throughout the forecast period, rising from 4,102 kt (2024) to 4,446 kt (2030), contributing 344 kt to total growth [C4]. Non-transportation sectors (construction, electrical, industrial machinery) comprise the remaining 85%, growing from 23,245 kt to 25,192 kt [C5].

**Methodology and Limitations**

The forecast applies exponential regression to 2015-2024 consumption data, yielding a fitted CAGR of 1.75% [C6]. Regional shares and sectoral percentages are held constant at 2024 levels. This approach does not model:

- EV-driven copper intensity increases (EVs require 3-4x more copper than ICE vehicles)
- Accelerated renewable energy deployment (solar/wind installations are copper-intensive)
- Grid infrastructure expansion for electrification
- Structural shifts in regional demand patterns

The historical trend-based model reflects incremental growth dynamics, not disruption-driven acceleration. Available datasets provide only transportation sector granularity; construction, electrical, and industrial machinery demand remain aggregated in the non-transportation residual.
