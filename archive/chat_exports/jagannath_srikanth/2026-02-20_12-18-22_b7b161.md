Lead demand falls by 10% relative to 2026 levels in **2030**.

## Direct Answer

Global lead-acid battery demand reaches 5,669.3 kt in 2030, representing a 13.7% decline from the 2026 baseline of 6,570.2 kt. The 10% threshold (5,913.2 kt) is crossed in 2030.

## Economic Drivers

The decline is driven by cost-curve dynamics in three converging disruptions:

**1. ICE Fleet Collapse (89% of 2026-2030 decline)**

SLI car batteries decline 801.5 kt as the ICE passenger fleet shrinks. BEV adoption follows S-curve dynamics post-tipping point:
- China: 2022 tipping, 16.1% ICE fleet decline by 2030
- USA: 2026 tipping, 11.6% ICE fleet decline by 2030
- Europe: 2024 tipping, 15.6% ICE fleet decline by 2030

Each ICE vehicle carries 11.0 kg of lead (SLI battery). Replacement demand (25%/year, 4-year battery life) dominates: 1,868.3 kt replacement vs 268.1 kt OEM in 2030.

**2. Commercial Vehicle Electrification (26% of decline)**

SLI commercial batteries decline 233.3 kt. Commercial vehicles carry 21.13 kg of lead per vehicle. Tipping points: China 2024, USA 2026, Europe 2025.

**3. Forklift Li-ion Adoption (9% of decline)**

Lead-acid forklift batteries decline 82.4 kt as li-ion penetration accelerates. Li-ion forklifts reached cost parity in major markets by 2022-2024.

**Offsetting Growth: EV Auxiliary Batteries (+223.4 kt)**

BEVs and PHEVs initially use lead-acid 12V auxiliary batteries (10.5 kg lead). This segment grows 223.4 kt from 2026 to 2030 as the EV fleet expands. Li-ion auxiliary adoption remains low (2.8% in 2024) with tipping points in 2029-2031.

The decline accelerates post-2030 as multiple S-curves converge: ICE fleet collapse, li-ion auxiliary adoption, and forklift/UPS modernization.

## Methodology

Bottom-up vehicle-count calculation across 10 lead-acid battery segments:
- **OEM demand**: New vehicle sales × lead content (kg) × LAB share
- **Replacement demand**: Fleet size × replacement rate × lead content × LAB share
- **Geographic scope**: China, USA, Europe, Rest of World
- **Time horizon**: 2026-2040

Li-ion adoption follows logistic S-curves fitted to historical data, with tipping points detected when li-ion cost ≤ lead-acid cost (B2B) or price (B2C).

## Assumptions

- **Lead content**: 11.0 kg (ICE cars), 21.13 kg (commercial), 10.5 kg (EV auxiliary)
- **Replacement rate**: 25%/year (4-year battery life) for SLI
- **BEV adoption**: S-curve dynamics post-tipping, 10% to 80% over 10 years
- **Li-ion auxiliary tipping**: 2029-2031 (cost-based detection)
- **Scenario**: Base case (no TaaS disruption overlay)

Analysis excludes regulatory lags and assumes cost-curve-driven adoption. The 2030 milestone reflects economic gravity, not policy targets.
