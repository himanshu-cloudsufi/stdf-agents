Lead demand declines by 10% relative to 2026 baseline levels in **2030**.

## Analysis Summary

**2026 Baseline:** 6,570 kt  
**2030 Demand:** 5,669 kt  
**Decline:** 901 kt (13.7%)  
**10% Threshold:** 5,913 kt (crossed in 2030)

## Primary Drivers

The 10% decline by 2030 is driven by **ICE fleet shrinkage due to BEV adoption**, not by lithium-ion SLI replacement:

1. **SLI Cars (ICE Passenger):** -802 kt (89% of total decline)
   - China BEV share rises from 44% (2026) to 73% (2030)
   - USA BEV share rises from 17% (2026) to 41% (2030)
   - Europe BEV share rises from 30% (2026) to 57% (2030)

2. **SLI Commercial (ICE+NGV):** -233 kt (26% of total decline)
   - Commercial EV adoption accelerates across all regions
   - NGV (Natural Gas Vehicles) in China also decline post-EV tipping

3. **EV Auxiliary LAB (Offset):** +223 kt (-25% offset)
   - Growing EV fleet requires 12V auxiliary batteries
   - 97%+ remain lead-acid through 2030 (lithium-ion cost barrier)

## Methodology

The lithium-ion-demand skill uses bottom-up stock-flow modeling:

- **ICE Fleet Calculation:** Total fleet × (1 - BEV_share - PHEV_share)
- **SLI Demand:** ICE_fleet × lead_per_vehicle × LAB_share × replacement_rate
- **Lead Content:** 11.0 kg per ICE passenger car, 21.13 kg per ICE commercial vehicle
- **Replacement Rate:** 25%/year (4-year battery life)

**BEV Tipping Points (Price Parity):**
- China: 2022 (detected)
- Europe: 2024 (detected)
- USA: 2026 (fallback)
- Rest_of_World: 2028 (fallback)

**Li-ion SLI Tipping Points (Price Parity):**
- USA/Europe: 2029
- China/Rest_of_World: 2031

Lithium-ion SLI penetration remains below 10% through 2030 because lithium-ion SLI batteries are still 2-3× more expensive than lead-acid SLI in 2030. The 2026-2030 decline is entirely driven by **ICE fleet collapse**, not chemistry substitution.

## Geographic Scope

Global analysis covering four regions:
- **China:** 44.7% of 2026 baseline (2,938 kt)
- **Rest_of_World:** 31.6% of 2026 baseline (2,077 kt)
- **USA:** 11.5% of 2026 baseline (756 kt)

## Post-2030 Trajectory

Lead demand continues to collapse after 2030:
- **2035:** 2,659 kt (60% decline from 2026)
- **2040:** 1,317 kt (80% decline from 2026)

By 2035, lithium-ion SLI adoption accelerates (post-tipping), compounding the ICE fleet decline and driving lead demand toward near-zero by 2040.
