## Cost-Curve Dynamics and Tipping Points

**Lithium-ion 12V SLI batteries reach cost parity with lead-acid between 2029 and 2031:**

- **USA/Europe:** 2029 (detected cost crossover)
- **China/Rest of World:** 2031 (detected cost crossover)

**Post-tipping S-curve adoption parameters:**
- Ceiling: 95% market penetration
- Steepness (k): 0.44
- Adoption speed: 10% to 80% over ~10 years

**Li-ion SLI penetration remains <5% through 2030** because tipping points occur in 2029–2031. The replacement market transitions slowly until the S-curve inflection (~2032), after which adoption accelerates exponentially.

**Cost-curve mechanism:**
1. Li-ion battery pack costs decline ~20% per cumulative production doubling.
2. 12V Li-ion battery prices fall from $100–150 (2024) toward parity with lead-acid ($25–60).
3. Cost crossover triggers logistic S-curve adoption in the replacement market.
4. ICE fleet shrinks simultaneously due to BEV adoption (separate S-curve).
5. **Combined effect:** Declining fleet × declining LAB share = demand collapse.
