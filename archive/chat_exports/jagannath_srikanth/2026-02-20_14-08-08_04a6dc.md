## Displacement Mechanisms

### 1. Passenger Car SLI Battery Displacement (Primary Driver)

**Mechanism:** ICE → BEV transition eliminates lead-acid SLI batteries. BEVs use lithium-ion 12V auxiliary batteries with zero lead content.

**Cost-Curve Dynamics:**
- BEV price parity reached 2023-2025 (China, Europe, USA)
- Post-tipping S-curve adoption: BEV share rises from 15% (2024) to 50% (2030) to 85% (2040)
- ICE fleet shrinks via stock-flow: new BEV sales enter, ICE vehicles scrapped after 15 years
- SLI replacement demand (4-year cycle, 25% annual replacement rate) collapses with ICE fleet

**Impact:**
- Base scenario: SLI passenger car lead demand falls from 3,787.8 kt (2026) to 406.4 kt (2040) = **-89%**
- TaaS scenario: Falls to 2.9 kt (2040) = **-99.9%** (near-total elimination)

### 2. TaaS Fleet Disruption (Acceleration Mechanism)

**Mechanism:** Autonomous ride-hailing (TaaS) replaces private vehicle ownership. TaaS vehicles are 100% BEV with lithium-ion 12V auxiliary batteries.

**TaaS Economics:**
- Cost per mile: $0.05-0.10 (TaaS) vs $0.80 (private ICE) = **10x cost advantage**
- Utilization: 120,000 km/year (TaaS) vs 15,000 km/year (private) = **8x higher**
- Fleet size: 90%+ reduction in total vehicles needed (higher utilization serves same passenger-km demand)
- Zero lead content: 100% BEV fleet with Li-ion 12V

**Regulatory Approval Gate:** TaaS S-curve begins post-2026 (all regions) after autonomous vehicle regulatory approval.

**Impact:**
- Accelerates ICE fleet decline by **2-3 years** vs base scenario
- Private fleet collapses from ~1.5 billion vehicles (2024) to <150 million (2040)
- TaaS fleet grows to ~50 million vehicles (2040), serving 100% of passenger-km demand

### 3. Lithium-Ion 12V Auxiliary Penetration

**Mechanism:** Even remaining ICE and PHEV vehicles transition from lead-acid to lithium-ion 12V batteries as cost parity is reached.

**Cost-Curve Dynamics:**
- Li-ion 12V price parity: 2025-2027 (regional variation)
- S-curve adoption: 0% (2024) → 4.3% (2030) → 27.1% (2035) → 71.8% (2040)
- Capability advantage: 3x cycle life, 4x energy density, faster charging

**Impact:**
- Reduces lead demand per ICE vehicle from 11.0 kg to 3.1 kg (2040) as Li-ion share rises
- EV auxiliary LAB demand peaks at 512.3 kt (2035) then declines to 305.2 kt (2040) in base scenario
- TaaS scenario: Falls to 72.2 kt (2040) due to private fleet collapse

### 4. Commercial Vehicle Electrification

**Mechanism:** B2B cost parity triggers S-curve adoption of electric trucks and buses. Commercial EVs use lithium-ion batteries, not lead-acid SLI.

**Cost-Curve Dynamics:**
- Tipping points: 2025-2030 (segment-dependent: LCV earliest, HCV latest)
- S-curve adoption: 10% (2030) → 50% (2035) → 80% (2040)

### 5. Stationary Applications (Secondary)

**Mechanism:** Datacenter and telecom UPS transition from VRLA (valve-regulated lead-acid) to lithium-ion batteries.

**Cost-Curve Dynamics:**
- B2B cost parity: 2024-2027 (datacenter), 2025-2028 (telecom)

## Methodology

This analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill's bottom-up forecasting methodology:

1. **Tipping Point Detection:** Identify year when BEV price ≤ ICE price (B2C) or EV cost ≤ ICE cost (B2B) using 3-year rolling median smoothing.

2. **S-Curve Adoption:** Fit logistic curve to historical BEV share data:
   - `BEV_share(t) = 0.95 / (1 + exp(-k × (t - t₀)))`
   - Parameters (k, t₀) fitted to regional data
   - Fallback: 10% to 80% adoption over 10 years post-tipping

3. **Fleet Stock-Flow:** Calculate ICE fleet size via:
   - `Fleet(t+1) = Fleet(t) + Sales(t) - Scrappage(t)`
   - Scrappage = Fleet(t) / 15 years (vehicle lifetime)

4. **Lead Demand Calculation:**
   - **OEM:** `ICE_Sales × 11.0 kg × LAB_share / 1,000,000 → kt`
   - **Replacement:** `ICE_Fleet × 0.25 × 11.0 kg × LAB_share / 1,000,000 → kt`
   - LAB_share = 1 - Li_ion_SLI_share (from auxiliary battery S-curve)

5. **TaaS Adjustment:** Separate scenario layer:
   - TaaS PKM share follows S-curve post-2026 regulatory approval
   - TaaS fleet = TaaS_PKM / (120,000 km/year × 2.0 persons/vehicle)
   - Private fleet = Private_PKM / (15,000 km/year × 1.5 persons/vehicle)
   - TaaS vehicles: 100% BEV, zero lead content
