This analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill's bottom-up lead demand forecasting model, which tracks lead consumption across all battery segments: SLI batteries for ICE passenger cars and commercial vehicles, lead-acid batteries for two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and forklifts, and VRLA batteries for datacenter and telecom UPS systems. ## Key Drivers of Demand Collapse

**1. ICE Fleet Decline**

Passenger car SLI batteries (4,202 kt in 2024) are the single largest lead demand driver, representing 51% of battery demand. As BEVs reach price parity (China 2024, USA/Europe 2025-2026), ICE sales collapse. The ICE fleet declines from 1.5 billion vehicles (2024) to ~200 million (2040) in the base scenario, and to ~50 million under TaaS.

**Passenger Car SLI Lead Demand:**
- 2024: 4,202 kt (OEM 613 kt + Replacement 3,589 kt)
- 2030: 2,839 kt (Base) / 1,800 kt (TaaS)
- 2040: 406 kt (Base) / 3 kt (TaaS)

**2. Lithium-Ion 12V Substitution**

Within the remaining ICE fleet, lithium-ion 12V batteries (SLI, auxiliary, starter) disrupt lead-acid on cost and capability. Li-ion SLI adoption follows an S-curve post-tipping (2026-2028 depending on region), reaching 72% penetration by 2040.

**Li-ion 12V Penetration:**
- 2024: 0%
- 2030: 4.3% (SLI/Aux), 3.6% (Starter)
- 2035: 27.1% (SLI/Aux), 14.4% (Starter)
- 2040: 71.8% (SLI/Aux), 32.2% (Starter)

**12V Battery Lead Demand (all segments):**
- 2024: 7,853 kt
- 2030: 6,301 kt (-20%)
- 2035: 4,072 kt (-48%)
- 2040: 1,533 kt (-80%)

**3. Commercial Vehicle Transition**

Commercial vehicle SLI demand (2,955 kt in 2024) follows a similar trajectory, with EVs reaching cost parity for LCV (2026-2028), MCV (2028-2030), and HCV (2030-2032). NGV (natural gas vehicles) in China are modeled as a chimera technology with exponential decay (half-life 6 years) post-EV tipping.

## Supply-Side Dynamics

**Secondary Production Feedback Loop:**
Secondary (recycled) lead accounts for ~50% of total supply [W2][W3], primarily from battery recycling. As battery demand collapses, the installed base of lead-acid batteries shrinks, reducing recycling feedstock. This creates a negative feedback loop: lower demand → smaller installed base → less recycling supply → further oversupply pressure on primary production.

**Primary Production Inertia:**
Lead mining has 5-10 year development cycles. Web research projections [W3][W7] assume continued growth to 16-17 Mt by 2033-2035, reflecting industry expectations of sustained battery demand. These forecasts are obsolete; they do not account for EV disruption dynamics or lithium-ion 12V substitution. Primary producers face stranded asset risk as demand collapses faster than supply can adjust.

## Conclusion

Lead supply will exceed demand by 10% in **2025-2026** under the most realistic supply scenarios (flat or modest growth). The TaaS scenario accelerates this by one year, reaching 10% oversupply in **2025**. By 2040, oversupply reaches 150-250% depending on supply assumptions, as demand collapses 59-65% while supply continues modest growth or remains flat.

The lead mining industry faces structural collapse driven by two converging disruptions: (1) ICE fleet decline eliminating the primary demand driver (SLI batteries), and (2) lithium-ion 12V substitution capturing the remaining market. Secondary production will decline in tandem with the shrinking installed base, but primary production has insufficient flexibility to adjust quickly. Web research forecasts projecting growth to 16-17 Mt by 2034 are obsolete and reflect pre-disruption assumptions.
