| Parameter | Value |

### Optimal Policy Package

To achieve 2pp unemployment reduction from the central baseline in each period:

| Lever | Magnitude | Unemployment Reduction | Lag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rate cuts | 200bps (3.1% → 1.1%) | ~0.75pp | 12–18 months |
| Targeted fiscal spending | ~$690B | ~1.0–1.3pp | 6–12 months |
| Direct employment programs | ~$167–200B | ~0.5–1.0pp | 3–6 months |
| **Combined** | **~$857–890B + 200bps** | **~2.0–2.3pp** | **Staggered** |

**Critical timing constraint:** To affect December 2027 outcomes, rate cuts must be initiated by Q1 2026 (18-month lag), fiscal legislation must pass by Q2 2026 (12-month lag), and direct employment programs must launch by Q2–Q3 2027 (3–6 month lag). For December 2028, the timeline is less compressed but the structural displacement is larger (19.5% economy replacement rate [C2]), requiring ongoing programs rather than one-time stimulus.

**The fundamental constraint is structural, not cyclical.** Conventional monetary and fiscal tools were designed for demand-side unemployment. AI-driven displacement is a supply-side structural shift — it does not respond to interest rate cuts or aggregate demand stimulus in the same way. Direct employment programs (retraining, public sector job creation, wage subsidies for AI-complementary roles) are the only lever with a direct transmission mechanism to the structural unemployment component.
