**Lead demand falls 10% below 2026 baseline levels in 2030.**

## Economic Gravity

The 10% decline threshold is crossed in 2030, driven by the convergence of ICE fleet collapse and lithium-ion displacement of lead-acid batteries. The 2026 baseline of 6,570 kt drops to 5,669 kt by 2030—a reduction of 901 kt (13.7%). This is not a gradual erosion; it is an accelerating collapse triggered by S-curve EV adoption and cost-parity tipping points for lithium-ion SLI batteries.

The steepest decline occurs in 2028–2029 (-410 kt), reflecting the inflection point where multiple S-curves (BEV adoption, lithium-ion SLI penetration, commercial EV transition) enter rapid-growth phases simultaneously.

## Primary Drivers (2030 Breakdown)

1. **SLI (ICE Passenger Cars): 2,136 kt (37.7%)**  
   Global ICE fleet declines from 908.5M vehicles (2026) to 786.4M (2030)—a 13.4% reduction. Each ICE vehicle requires an 11.0 kg lead-acid SLI battery with a 4-year replacement cycle (25% annual replacement rate). Fleet contraction directly reduces OEM and replacement demand.

2. **SLI (Commercial Vehicles): 1,486 kt (26.2%)**  
   Includes ICE and NGV (natural gas vehicles) commercial fleets. Lead content: 21.13 kg per vehicle. EV commercial vehicle adoption (LCV, MCV, HCV, e-bus) accelerates post-2025 tipping points, shrinking the ICE/NGV installed base.

3. **Forklift LAB: 621 kt (11.0%)**  
   Lithium-ion forklift penetration reaches cost parity in most regions by 2024–2026. S-curve adoption displaces lead-acid batteries (LAB) in material handling applications.

4. **EV Auxiliary (LAB): 500 kt (8.8%)**  
   Transitional growth segment. BEV/PHEV fleet expansion drives demand for 12V auxiliary batteries, but lithium-ion auxiliary penetration remains low (~2.8% in 2026). This segment grows temporarily before lithium-ion dominates.

5. **E2W LAB Traction: 363 kt (6.4%)**  
   Electric two-wheeler (E2W) adoption accelerates, but within the EV segment, lithium-ion chemistry displaces lead-acid traction batteries via two-stage disruption (ICE→EV, then LAB→Li-ion within EVs).

## Underlying Dynamics

**ICE Fleet Collapse:**  
The global ICE passenger car fleet peaks around 2024–2025 and enters decline as BEV adoption crosses tipping points (China 2022, Europe 2024, USA 2026). By 2030, the ICE fleet contracts 122M vehicles globally. Each vehicle eliminated removes 11.0 kg of SLI lead demand annually (replacement) plus OEM demand for new sales.

**Lithium-Ion Displacement:**  
Lithium-ion SLI batteries reach price parity with lead-acid SLI batteries in major markets between 2029–2031. Post-tipping, S-curve adoption accelerates, capturing 10%→80% market share over 10 years. Even at low penetration rates in 2030 (~7% China, ~15% USA/Europe), the trajectory is set.

**Commercial Vehicle Transition:**  
Commercial EVs (LCV, MCV, HCV, e-bus) reach cost parity in 2024–2027 across regions. S-curve adoption is steeper than passenger cars (k=0.5–0.7 vs. 0.3–0.4) due to B2B cost-driven decision-making. NGV (natural gas vehicles) in China follow a chimera decay pattern post-2027 tipping, with a 6-year half-life.

**Replacement Demand Mechanics:**  
Lead-acid SLI batteries have a 4-year life (25% annual replacement rate). As the ICE fleet shrinks, replacement demand declines proportionally. A 13.4% fleet reduction (2026→2030) translates to a 13.4% reduction in replacement demand, compounded by lithium-ion penetration eroding the remaining LAB share.

## Methodology

**Source:** lithium-ion-demand skill (canonical bottom-up model)  
**Scope:** Battery applications only (excludes non-battery uses such as ammunition, radiation shielding, construction)  
**Geographic Coverage:** Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World)  
**Segments:** 10 use cases tracked:
- SLI (ICE cars, commercial vehicles)
- EV auxiliary (LAB)
- Starter batteries (2W, 3W)
- Traction batteries (E2W LAB, E3W LAB, forklift LAB)
- Stationary (datacenter VRLA, telecom VRLA)

**Calculation Method:**  
- **OEM Demand:** New vehicle sales × lead content (kg) × LAB share  
- **Replacement Demand:** Fleet size × replacement rate × lead content (kg) × LAB share  
- **Tipping Points:** B2C uses price parity, B2B uses cost parity  
- **Adoption Curves:** Logistic S-curve fitted to historical data (fallback: 10%→80% over 10 years post-tipping)

**Validation:**  
- Stock-flow consistency enforced across all vehicle segments  
- Cross-checked against lithium-ion growth trajectories  
- Historical calibration: bottom-up sum vs. market data (<15% variance tolerance)
