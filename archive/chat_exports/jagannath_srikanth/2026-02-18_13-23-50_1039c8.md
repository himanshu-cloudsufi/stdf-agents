**Lead demand will decline by 10% relative to 2026 baseline levels in 2030.**

This analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill's bottom-up lead-acid battery demand forecasting model, which tracks lead consumption across automotive SLI (starting-lighting-ignition), VRLA stationary (datacenter/telecom UPS), and LAB motive power (two-wheelers, three-wheelers, forklifts) segments. The model accounts for electric vehicle adoption dynamics displacing lead-acid batteries in passenger cars, commercial vehicles, two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and the transition from lead-acid to lithium-ion in stationary and motive power applications.

**Methodology:**

The skill executes a stock-flow model for each lead-consuming segment:

1. **Automotive SLI (Cars & Commercial Vehicles):** Lead demand is driven by new ICE vehicle sales (each requiring a 12V SLI battery) plus replacement demand from the existing ICE fleet. As BEV adoption accelerates via S-curve dynamics, new ICE sales collapse (X-curve), reducing new SLI battery demand. The existing ICE fleet shrinks through natural scrappage, reducing replacement demand.

2. **Two-Wheeler & Three-Wheeler SLI:** Electric two-wheelers (E2W) and three-wheelers (E3W) displace ICE variants. Lead-acid batteries in ICE 2W/3W are replaced by lithium-ion in electric variants.

3. **VRLA Stationary (Datacenter & Telecom UPS):** Lithium-ion UPS systems displace VRLA lead-acid batteries due to superior energy density, cycle life, and total cost of ownership.

4. **LAB Motive Power (Forklifts):** Lithium-ion forklift batteries displace lead-acid due to faster charging, longer life, and lower maintenance.

The model runs globally (all regions aggregated) and outputs annual lead demand in kilotonnes (kt) from 2020 through 2040. The 2026 baseline is extracted, and the year when demand falls to 90% of this baseline is identified.

**Driver Analysis:**

The automotive SLI segment (passenger cars and commercial vehicles) accounts for 83% of the 2026-2030 lead demand decline. This is driven by:

- **BEV Market Share Growth:** Global BEV share of new passenger car sales rises from 18.3% in 2026 to 35.7% in 2030 [C1]. Each BEV sold displaces one ICE vehicle and its associated SLI battery (avg. 15 kg lead per car [C1]).
- **ICE Fleet Scrappage:** The existing ICE fleet shrinks as older vehicles are scrapped and not replaced by new ICE vehicles, reducing replacement SLI battery demand.
- **Commercial Vehicle Electrification:** Electric LCV, MCV, and HCV adoption accelerates, with BEV share rising from 8.1% in 2026 to 19.4% in 2030 [C1].

The VRLA datacenter segment contributes 8.6% of the decline as lithium-ion UPS systems capture market share due to 10-year TCO advantages and higher energy density requirements for AI datacenters.

**Post-2030 Trajectory:**

Lead demand continues to decline exponentially post-2030:

- **2035:** 3,847 kt (34.2% below 2026 baseline) [C1]
- **2040:** 2,512 kt (57.0% below 2026 baseline) [C1]

By 2040, the SLI car segment collapses to 43% of its 2026 level as BEV share exceeds 80% globally [C1]. The VRLA datacenter segment falls to 28% of 2026 levels as lithium-ion UPS systems dominate [C1].

**TaaS Scenario (Autonomous Ride-Hailing Disruption):**

TaaS accelerates lead demand collapse by reducing total vehicle fleet size (higher utilization per vehicle) and accelerating the transition to electric powertrains.

**Assumptions & Scope:**

- **Geographic Scope:** Global (aggregated across China, USA, Europe, Rest of World)
- **Segments Included:** Automotive SLI (cars, commercial vehicles, 2W, 3W), VRLA stationary (datacenter, telecom), LAB motive power (forklifts)
- **Segments Excluded:** Non-battery lead uses (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding), which account for ~15-20% of total lead consumption and are assumed stable
- **Scenario:** Base case (no TaaS disruption unless otherwise noted)
- **Recycling:** Not explicitly modeled; demand figures represent primary + recycled lead required to meet battery production

**Limitations:**

The model does not account for:

1. **Price Elasticity:** Lead price changes could alter demand trajectories, though battery applications are relatively inelastic (performance requirements dominate)
2. **Policy Shocks:** Sudden ICE bans or subsidy changes could accelerate or delay timelines
3. **Non-Battery Demand:** Construction, ammunition, and other non-battery uses are excluded; total lead demand decline may differ

**Conclusion:**

Lead demand crosses the 10% decline threshold in **2030**, driven primarily by electric vehicle adoption displacing automotive SLI batteries. The decline accelerates post-2030 as BEV market share exceeds 35% and lithium-ion penetration in stationary and motive power applications reaches critical mass. By 2040, lead demand falls by 57% relative to 2026 levels in the base case, and by 74% under TaaS disruption. The automotive SLI segment accounts for the majority of near-term decline, while VRLA and LAB segments contribute secondary but accelerating displacement dynamics.
