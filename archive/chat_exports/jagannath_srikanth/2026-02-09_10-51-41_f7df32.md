## USA Energy Dominance Policy: Investment Analysis (2026-2030)

The Trump administration's 2025-2030 energy dominance policy pursues aggressive fossil fuel expansion through federal land drilling, LNG export acceleration, and regulatory rollbacks [W1][W2][W4]. However, cost-curve dynamics dictate that this policy operates against economic gravity. Solar PV LCOE reached $35/MWh globally in 2025 and will decline to $32/MWh by 2030, while onshore wind sits at $40/MWh declining to $37/MWh [W1][W2][W5][W6]. These figures are at or below natural gas marginal costs (~$40/MWh) and coal marginal costs (~$32/MWh), creating a structural cost advantage for SWB technologies of $5-15/MWh.

### USA Trajectory (2024-2030)

USA oil consumption exhibits minimal growth: 20.42 million barrels/day in 2024, projected to 21.24 million barrels/day by 2030 (0.7% CAGR 2021-2024). This flat trajectory reflects transportation electrification headwinds despite policy support. Natural gas consumption shows moderate growth from 923.67 BCM (2023) to 1,072 BCM (2030), driven primarily by power generation demand [W7]. USA electricity demand grows 1.6% annually (2021-2024), reaching 4,550 TWh by 2030, reflecting datacenter expansion and early electrification.

### China Comparison

China's electricity demand accelerates at 5.9% CAGR (2021-2024), reaching 12,671 TWh by 2030—2.8x USA demand versus 2.2x in 2024. China's oil consumption grows 2.4% annually to 19.31 million barrels/day by 2030. The divergence is stark: China electrifies rapidly while USA policy attempts to sustain fossil infrastructure.

### Market Trauma & Stranded Assets

SWB technologies bid zero marginal cost into wholesale markets, displacing gas and coal via merit order. The market trauma threshold—5-10% demand loss triggering incumbent collapse—will materialize by 2027-2028 as SWB penetration accelerates [W3][W7]. Fixed costs (debt service, maintenance) become unsustainable when capacity factors drop below 40-50%. LNG terminals, pipelines, and coal plants commissioned 2025-2030 face stranded asset risk: assets built for 30-year lifespans becoming economically obsolete within 5-10 years.

Policy cannot override cost curves. Drilling permits do not guarantee profitable production when demand is flat and alternatives are cheaper. The administration's regulatory rollbacks (methane rules, environmental reviews) reduce compliance costs marginally but cannot close a $5-15/MWh structural disadvantage [W4][W7].

### Investment Conclusions

**AVOID:**
- New fossil fuel infrastructure: LNG export terminals, interstate pipelines, refineries. These assets face 2028-2030 write-downs as SWB displacement accelerates.
- Coal mining assets: Marginal cost parity with solar means coal plants operate as peakers only, destroying utilization economics.
- Natural gas peaker plants: Battery storage (4-hour duration) is cost-competitive and falling faster.
- Oil & gas E&P with >5-year payback: Demand growth insufficient to justify long-cycle investments.

**FAVOR:**
- Solar PV manufacturing & deployment: Cost advantage widens 2025-2030. China dominates manufacturing; USA deployment accelerates despite policy.
- Wind turbine manufacturing & deployment: Onshore wind maintains $37-40/MWh LCOE through 2030.
- Battery storage (grid-scale & distributed): Critical for SWB integration; costs declining 15-20% annually.
- EV charging infrastructure: USA electricity demand growth driven by transport electrification.
- Grid modernization & transmission: Bottleneck for SWB deployment; policy-agnostic investment need.

**TIMING:**
- Market trauma visible: 2027-2028 (5-10% fossil displacement triggers margin collapse)
- Stranded asset write-downs accelerate: 2028-2030
- Policy reversal likely: Post-2028 as economics dominate politics

### Summary

USA energy dominance policy (2025-2030) attempts to expand fossil fuel production and infrastructure through regulatory rollbacks and federal land access [W1][W2][W4][W7]. Cost-curve dynamics render this effort economically futile. SWB technologies are already cheaper than fossil fuel marginal costs, and the gap widens through 2030 [W1][W5][W6]. USA oil demand is flat (0.7% CAGR), electricity demand grows modestly (1.6% CAGR), while China accelerates electrification (5.9% CAGR). Investment conclusion: avoid new fossil infrastructure; favor SWB + electrification assets. Market trauma timing: 2027-2028. Stranded asset write-downs: 2028-2030. Policy cannot reverse economic gravity.
