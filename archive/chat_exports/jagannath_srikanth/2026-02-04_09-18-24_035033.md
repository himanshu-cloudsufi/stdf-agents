God Parity is the economic threshold where the cost of self-generation (rooftop solar + battery storage) falls below the cost of grid transmission alone. Once crossed, a customer can generate and store their own power cheaper than paying for poles, wires, and grid infrastructure—even if the utility provided generation for free.

This differs fundamentally from grid parity, which compares disruptor cost to incumbent generation cost. God Parity is the true disruption point because it eliminates the utility's monopoly on delivery. A customer rationally defects when transmission costs exceed self-generation costs, regardless of what the utility charges for electrons.

Significance: God Parity triggers mass grid defection and the utility death spiral. As customers leave, fixed transmission costs spread across fewer remaining customers, raising per-unit costs and accelerating further defection. This is not a gradual transition—it is a rupture event.

Mathematically: If transmission cost = $0.08/kWh and rooftop solar + 4-hour battery = $0.06/kWh, God Parity is crossed. The utility cannot compete by lowering generation price; it must lower transmission cost, which is physically and economically impossible for centralized grids.

Role in disruption: God Parity marks the inflection point where distributed energy architecture becomes inevitable. It is the 10x rupture that collapses the incumbent utility model.
