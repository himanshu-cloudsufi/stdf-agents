**The 10% supply surplus threshold is not a future event — it is the current structural condition of the global lead market.** Under every plausible supply assumption, the 10% oversupply threshold was crossed before 2026 and widens monotonically to 113% (base case) or 184% (TaaS) by 2036 [C1].

---

### Supply Anchor

Global refined lead supply runs approximately **12,400–12,500 kt/yr** (2024–2026), comprising ~4,200–4,500 kt primary mine output and ~6,000–7,500 kt secondary/recycled lead [C2]. Supply is modeled flat at 12,500 kt for the analysis window — a conservative assumption since secondary recycling will eventually decline as the LAB fleet shrinks, but with a 10–15 year lag from battery retirement. Even at the most conservative plausible supply estimate of 10,700 kt, the 10% threshold is crossed in 2026 at 11.5% surplus [C1].

**Supply threshold test:** For the 10% threshold to NOT be crossed in 2026, global supply would need to be ≤ 10,556 kt — below any credible industry estimate. The market entered structural oversupply in 2024–2025 [C1].

### Mechanism: Why the Surplus Is Structural, Not Cyclical

**SLI passenger cars drive 69% of all demand destruction.** The segment loses 2,573 kt over the decade through two simultaneous channels [C1]:

1. **ICE fleet shrinkage:** BEV price parity triggers S-curve adoption (k=0.44, L=0.95). Each BEV sold removes one ICE vehicle from the fleet that generates replacement SLI demand. The replacement pool (ICE fleet × 25%/yr × 11 kg lead/battery) dominates at 86–96% of total SLI car lead demand throughout the decade [C1].

2. **12V Li-ion substitution within residual ICE fleet:** Even the surviving ICE fleet faces substitution as Li-ion 12V batteries reach price parity (tipping: China 2033, USA/Europe 2031, RoW 2034). By 2036, 32.8% of ICE car SLI batteries are Li-ion — removing ~600 kt of lead demand that would otherwise persist from legacy ICE vehicles [C1].

**The double-destruction effect compounds:** a shrinking fleet with a rising Li-ion share within it. No supply-side response can offset 3,723 kt of decade demand destruction against a supply base of 12,500 kt.

**The only counter-trend is VRLA datacenter growth (+214 kt over the decade)**, driven by AI compute expansion. This is insufficient to offset even a single year of SLI destruction [C1].

**TaaS amplification:** Under TaaS, autonomous ride-hailing fleets (100% BEV, zero lead content) capture 90% of passenger-km by 2035, collapsing SLI car lead demand from 1,478 kt (base 2035) to 147 kt (TaaS 2035) — a 90% reduction in five years [C1].
