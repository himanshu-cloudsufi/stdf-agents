## Drivers of Decline

| Driver | 2026–2031 Decline | % of Total Decline |

The global ICE passenger car fleet declines from 909 million vehicles (2026) to 749 million (2031), a 17.5% reduction. Each ICE vehicle requires 11 kg of lead for its SLI battery, with a 25% annual replacement rate (4-year battery life). As the ICE fleet collapses, lead demand from SLI batteries collapses in lockstep.

## Methodology

This analysis uses the **lithium-ion-demand skill**, a bottom-up stock-flow model covering:

1. **Tipping Point Detection:** Price parity (B2C) or cost parity (B2B) triggers S-curve adoption
2. **S-Curve Adoption:** Logistic curve fitted to historical data; fallback heuristic 10%→80% over 10 years post-tipping
3. **Fleet Stock-Flow:** Vehicle sales → fleet accumulation → scrappage (15-year vehicle life)
4. **Lead Demand Calculation:**
   - **OEM:** New ICE vehicle sales × 11 kg lead per SLI battery
   - **Replacement:** ICE fleet × 25% annual replacement rate × 11 kg lead
5. **Non-Battery Uses:** Construction, radiation shielding, ammunition (1,698 kt in 2024, growing 1.5%/year)
