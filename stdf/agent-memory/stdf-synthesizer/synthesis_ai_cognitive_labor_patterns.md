## Key synthesis patterns

### Incumbent-is-not-a-technology framing

When the incumbent is human labor (not a product or technology), the X-curve maps to a labor market decline, not a product market. The five market trauma mechanisms must be reframed: wage deflation replaces fixed-cost spread; entry-level job collapse replaces investment drought; education/credential erosion replaces talent flight; BPO geographic disruption replaces panic pricing; social/political disruption replaces policy lobbying. Document the reframing explicitly in the xcurve-analyst summary.

**How to apply:** Any future synthesis where the incumbent is human labor (robotics, future AGI analyses) should use this five-mechanism reframing table.

### Deflationary shock framing — integration required from 4 agents

Answering "is this technology a deflationary shock?" requires integrating:
1. cost-fitter (rate of cost decline + incumbent cost rise)
2. xcurve-analyst (wage bill compression estimate, market trauma)
3. tipping-synthesizer (universal input displacement mechanism)
4. domain-disruption (Stellar classification, Jevons excluded = non-equilibrating)

The key insight: displacement of a UNIVERSAL input (cognitive labor embedded in every sector) propagates deflation economy-wide simultaneously, unlike sectoral disruptions (EVs, solar) that affect specific industries. Frame this as the structural distinction in Phase 5 and Phase 7.

**How to apply:** For any cognitive labor / AL analysis, address the deflationary shock question directly in Phase 5 (mechanism) and Key Conclusion (quantified estimate). Use the xcurve-analyst's $3–6T/yr directional estimate but always label it [model-derived, directional].

### USA leads over China — structural deviation from standard STDF pattern

In AI cognitive labor disruption, USA leads China by approximately 0.9 years. This is a structural deviation from every prior STDF physical technology analysis where China leads by 3–7 years. Root cause: Stellar technology delivery mechanism (cloud compute) means no manufacturing scale advantage for China. Leadership is determined by ecosystem investment density ($109B USA vs $9.3B China private AI investment, 2024), knowledge-worker concentration, and regulatory posture.

**How to apply:** For any Stellar technology disruption (AI/AL), do not assume China leads. Check the ecosystem investment and regulatory dimensions before applying the standard STDF regional pattern. Flag the deviation explicitly in Phase 6 Regional Dynamics.

The steeper k in this analysis has a clear structural explanation: Stellar technology (zero marginal cost, no infrastructure buildout lag) means supply is unconstrained, so k is governed entirely by demand-side enterprise workflow re-engineering velocity.

**Why:** Physical disruptions are throttled by manufacturing ramp-up (limits k to ~0.25–0.35). Stellar technologies face no such constraint — k can reach 0.5–0.65 as supply is instantaneously scalable via cloud compute. This k structural rule should be applied to future AL/AI analyses.

### Big Bang disruption pattern — single-window tipping ramp

Big Bang disruption (arriving simultaneously cheap AND capable) compresses the standard from-below adoption ramp. The typical from-below pattern has a multi-year low-growth pre-inflection period where the disruptor captures low-end markets before moving upmarket. Big Bang skips this — the disruptor arrives at high capability, so the adoption ramp starts steep. Evidence: AI at 3% substitution share in 2023, 9% in 2025 — a 3x increase in 2 years, compared to BEV which took 5 years to move from 3% to 9% global share.

**How to apply:** For Big Bang disruptions (confirmed by domain-disruption), expect shorter pre-inflection phases, steeper k, and an earlier 5% threshold crossing relative to the disruption onset year.

### S-curve dependent variable construction caveat

Unlike physical technology disruptions where the S-curve dependent variable is directly observed (EV sales, solar capacity additions), the AI cognitive labor substitution share has no direct primary-source time series. The dependent variable is a constructed proxy triangulated from enterprise adoption surveys, sector-specific productivity studies, and code generation statistics. This means R²=0.9910 reflects fit to a proxy, not to a directly observed quantity. Always note this in the S-curve section and in data gaps.

**How to apply:** For any future AL/cognitive labor S-curve analysis, maintain this caveat. The high R² is a necessary but not sufficient condition for confidence — the proxy construction methodology must be documented transparently.
