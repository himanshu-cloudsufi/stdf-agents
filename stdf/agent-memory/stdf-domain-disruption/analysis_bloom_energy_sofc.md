**Analysis:** Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE) / SOFC disruption by SWB in distributed on-site generation sub-domain. Analysis date: 2026-03-25.

**Key findings:**
- Cost crossing already occurred (~2022-2023): SWB blended LCOE $59/MWh vs Bloom minimum $99/MWh (Series 10)
- SWB 1.46x cheaper than Bloom's absolute structural cost floor ($86/MWh at historic-low gas $2.21/MMBtu)
- Even SWB with 8hr BESS for 24/7 data center uptime ($44/MWh) is 2.25x cheaper than Bloom
- Bloom SOFC cost improvement: ~-2%/yr CAGR; SWB components: -13.5%/yr (solar) and -15.8%/yr (BESS)
- BESS deployment CAGR 74.9%/yr (5yr); Bloom growth ~10-15%/yr — structural divergence widening

**Disruption classification:** From Above + Systemic (SWB entered utility-scale and cascaded to distributed)

**Technology flow classifications:**
- Bloom SOFC (any fuel): X-Flow — structural fuel cost dependency
- Solar PV + LFP BESS + SWB: Stellar — zero marginal fuel cost

**Chimeras identified:**
1. SOFC + BESS hybrid: retains gas infrastructure dependency
2. Solar + natural gas peaker backup ("solar-plus-peaker"): evolves to pure SWB as BESS duration grows
3. Biogas SOFC: X-Flow chimera, geographic constraints
4. Electrolytic hydrogen SOFC: X-Flow chimera, $4-10/kg hydrogen cost prohibitive

**Short thesis timing:** Peak Bloom revenue likely 2026-2028 (data center AI tailwind sustaining growth past cost crossing); structural decline begins when enterprise procurement shifts to SWB+BESS-first for new data center builds.
