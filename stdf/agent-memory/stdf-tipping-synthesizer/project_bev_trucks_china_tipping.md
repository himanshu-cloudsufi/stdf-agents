Tipping year 2025–2026 (long-haul full-network); urban/regional already tipped 2022.

**Why:** Cost parity was met 6–7 years before capability and adoption readiness conditions. The binding constraints (capability_parity and adoption_readiness) were co-binding at 2026 — both trace to the same LFP cost-curve mechanism, creating a convergence effect that compresses the range to 2025–2026.

**How to apply:** When synthesizing transport disruptions with segment heterogeneity (urban vs. long-haul), always decompose the tipping year by segment — the headline tipping year applies to the last segment, but S-curve adoption is already underway in earlier-tipping segments. The 22% overall BEV-HDT share in 2025 reflected a >50% urban segment already tipped, not a pre-tipping market.

Key numbers to carry forward for downstream agents:
- Tipping year: 2025–2026
- S-curve parameters (provisional): L=95, k=0.30, x0=2026
- 80% completion: 2030–2033 (model-derived target_year 2031.6)
- Binding constraint: capability_parity + adoption_readiness (co-binding)
