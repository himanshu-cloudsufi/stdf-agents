## Big Bang Disruption Pattern Notes

- AI is the clearest Big Bang disruption yet analyzed in this pipeline: cost parity arrived simultaneously with a large portion of capability parity, rather than trickling up from the low end.
- This compresses the early S-curve adoption ramp vs. typical from-below disruptions (BEV, solar PV, lead-acid).
- In Big Bang disruptions, k parameter is higher (0.30–0.40 range) because adopters face no cost barrier from the start.
- The usual "early cost premium deters early adoption" dynamic is absent; adoption is gated almost entirely on capability and enterprise integration readiness.

## Co-Binding Condition Pattern

- At the lower tipping bound (2026.5), both capability parity (agentic duration threshold) and adoption readiness (EU regulatory compliance) resolve simultaneously within 3 months.
- This is functionally identical to the BEV-HDT China co-binding pattern — two conditions from different causal chains converging in the same window.
- The convergence avoids the usual sequential delay (if EU regulatory blocked for 6 months after cap parity, tipping would be 6 months later). Instead, simultaneous resolution holds the tipping year at 2026.5 for the lower bound.

## Disruptor Virtuous Cycle Domain-Specific Numbers

- Inference cost decline: ~$0.002/CTE (2025) → ~$0.0006/CTE (2027) → ~$0.0002/CTE (2029) [model-derived at 69.5%/yr]

- API ecosystem lock-in: enterprises building AI-native workflows increase switching cost to human labor (disruptor-favorable lock-in, inverse of typical incumbent lock-in)
