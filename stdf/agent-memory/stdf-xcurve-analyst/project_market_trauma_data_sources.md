For LNG trucking X-curve analysis, source reliability by mechanism:

**Asset stranding:** IEEFA reports are the best primary source. IIFIIR has fleet size data. Bloomberg/S&P Global have LNG price data.

**Financial contagion:** Bloomberg (domestic LNG price at 5-yr low), IEEFA (fleet operator economics, LNG-diesel spread collapse). No source found for secondhand LNG truck auction prices or dealer residual value data.

**Workforce displacement:** Data gap — no source found with specific LNG station operator headcounts or mechanic employment numbers. Must use model-derived estimates (3–5 staff/station × station count).

**Supply chain disruption:** Bloomberg/Kpler for LNG import volumes. Westport Fuel Systems earnings calls are the most direct evidence of LNG engine investment drought — use investor relations sources for OEM-level supply chain data.

**Policy lobbying:** Energy Intelligence covers Chinese subsidy programs well. IEEFA confirms lobbying outcomes. China NDRC announcements are primary sources but not always accessible in English.

**How to apply:** Always check Westport/Weichai earnings calls for LNG engine order data. IEEFA is the most comprehensive single source for China LNG trucking. Bloomberg covers price collapse. Station closure/utilization data is a persistent gap — flag it explicitly.
