Key observed metrics (end-2024):

- 2,300 GW actively in US interconnection queue (down from 2,600 GW in 2023 — first decline in a decade) [LBNL]
- Breakdown: 956 GW solar, 890 GW storage, 271 GW wind, 136 GW gas
- Average time from interconnection request to commercial operation: nearly 5 years (2024 cohort) [LBNL]
- CAISO and PJM accepted zero new applications in 2024 (processing backlog only)
- FERC Order 2023 reforms in early implementation: 33% more agreements processed in 2024 vs 2023
- Only 13% of capacity requesting interconnection from 2000–2019 reached commercial operation by end-2024

**Why:** The US grid interconnection queue is the single largest identifiable constraint on the pace of solar+BESS displacing gas generation in the US power sector. Even with cost parity already achieved (solar+BESS crossed NGCC LCOE in 2023–2024), the pipeline bottleneck prevents mass deployment.

**How to apply:** For V2 disruption analyses (solar+BESS vs gas generation), rate US grid interconnection as PARTIAL. Curve-trajectory-implied improvement from FERC Order 2023 by 2027–2028. Europe does not have a comparable bottleneck (1–3 year timelines). China also not a bottleneck.
