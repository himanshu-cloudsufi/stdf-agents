**Infrastructure Readiness: MET**
- Manufacturing: 3 TWh global capacity, 33% utilization (2:1 to 3:1 overcapacity) [observed 2024-2025]
- Installation: 50%+ annual BESS deployment growth; 315 GWh deployed globally 2025; US 18.9 GW 2025 [observed]
- Grid interconnection: PARTIAL — US queue 2,300 GW with 5-year average wait; EU/China 1-3 years; FERC Order 2023 showing 33% improvement in queue processing [observed 2024]

**Why:** Manufacturing overcapacity and installation capability are scaling rapidly. Grid interconnection is a process bottleneck in US (not a capacity shortage), driven by RTO/ISO review timelines. FERC reforms are working; queue wait times improving.

**How to apply:** Rate infrastructure as MET with caveat that US grid interconnection creates 2-3 year lag between cost-parity and grid-connected deployment. EU and China have no comparable bottleneck. When modeling deployment curves, account for regional queue processing speeds (California faster than PJM).

**Supply Chain Readiness: PARTIAL (unconstrained 2026-27, strategic vulnerability 2028+)**
- Cell manufacturing: 3 TWh capacity, oversupplied; pack prices falling 16.81% CAGR [observed]
- Critical minerals: China 60% lithium, 75% cobalt, 90% graphite refining [observed IEA 2025]
- Cobalt risk: DRC 74% mine production; 4-month export suspension Feb 2025 [observed]
- Mitigation: LFP chemistry adoption 64% of China market eliminates cobalt/reduces nickel
- Recycling: 879 ktpa capacity 2024; 0.5% of supply 2025 → 14% by 2050 projection [observed]

**Why:** Cell manufacturing is not a constraint; oversupply is structural. Critical minerals refining concentration (China dominance) is a geopolitical vulnerability, not operational constraint for 2026-27. LFP shift mitigates cobalt risk. Recycling immature but scaling steeply.

**How to apply:** Rate supply chain as PARTIAL. For 2026-27 deployment, mineral supply is unconstrained. For 2028+ analysis, incorporate geopolitical risk from China refining dominance and DRC instability. Recycling will become material contributor 2028+ (5-14% of supply projected). LFP adoption (already 64% of China market) is primary constraint mitigation.

**Regulatory Readiness: MET (and accelerating in EU)**
- US FERC: Orders 841/2222/2023 complete; storage market participation enabled; interconnection queue reforms showing 33% YoY acceleration [observed 2024]
- EU: Electricity Market Reform Sept 2024; Grids Package Dec 2025 sets 6-month permitting target for BESS >100kW [observed]
- China: State-directed, no regulatory friction; fastest deployment timelines
- Safety standards: IEC 62619, 62923, IEEE 1547, EN 50549 harmonized [observed 2024]

**Why:** Regulatory environment has matured rapidly 2023-2025. FERC Order 2023 reforms are improving queue processing. EU Grids Package represents first binding EU-level permitting timelines (6 months for batteries). China faces no regulatory constraints. International safety standards are complete and harmonized globally.

**How to apply:** Rate regulatory environment as MET. For US analysis, note that legal/regulatory framework is enabling but operational (queue processing) is bottleneck. For EU/China, regulatory environment is fully enabling and becoming a competitive advantage vs. US. When modeling acceleration timelines, account for FERC Order 2023 queue improvements 2026-2028 easing US bottleneck.
