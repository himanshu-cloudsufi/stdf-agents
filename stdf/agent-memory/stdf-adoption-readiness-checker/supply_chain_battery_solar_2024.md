---
name: Battery and solar manufacturing supply chain — global 2024 baseline
description: Manufacturing capacity vs demand ratios for battery cells and solar panels as of 2024; both show structural oversupply, NOT supply-side constraint
type: project
---

**Battery manufacturing (2024):**
- Global capacity: 3 TWh [IEA 2024]
- Annual demand: 1 TWh [IEA 2024]
- Utilization rate: 33% (3:1 surplus)
- Annual EV battery output: 867.8 GWh (+21.2% YoY)
- Pack prices: fell below $100/kWh for first time; China -30% in 2024 alone
- US battery mfg capacity: doubled since 2022 to 200+ GWh (IRA incentives)

**Critical mineral risk (2024):**
- China: 75% of global cobalt refining, ~60% of lithium refining [USGS 2024]
- Top-3 refiner concentration across key minerals: 86% (up from 82% in 2020)
- DRC: 74% of cobalt mine production; 4-month export suspension Feb 2025
- LFP chemistry (64% China market) reduces cobalt exposure
- Not a current production constraint but a strategic vulnerability

**Solar panel manufacturing (2024):**
- Global capacity: 1,100+ GW/year
- Annual installation: 553–601 GW
- Utilization rate: ~50% (2:1 surplus)
- Module prices fell 50%+ since early 2023 (overcapacity driven)
- China: 80% of global solar production

**BESS manufacturing (2024):**
- No evidence of supply-side constraint
- Global deployment CAGR: 71.6% (2015–2024)
- 370 GWh installed capacity by end-2024

**Why:** Both battery and solar supply chains are structurally oversupplied. The adoption readiness constraint for V1 (BEV) and V2 (solar+BESS) lies in infrastructure (charging coverage, grid interconnection) not in manufacturing capacity.

**How to apply:** Rate supply chain as READY for BEV (V1) and solar+BESS (V2) in all regions. Flag critical mineral refining concentration as a strategic risk (low-to-medium severity) but not a current production blocker.
