Key observed regulatory facts (2024-2025):

**China (READY):**
- NEV dual-credit: 20% target 2024, 22% 2025, 48% 2026, 58% 2027 [China MIIT, Nov 2025]
- Non-compliance blocks product sales — enforcement is operational
- Purchase tax exemption: up to RMB 30,000 (~$4,170) per vehicle, Jan 2024–Dec 2025 [China MIIT 2024]
- Safety standards (GB 38031, GB 18384) mature and updated

**Europe (PARTIAL):**
- AFIR in force April 13, 2024: 150 kW DCFC every 60 km on TEN-T core network from 2025
- EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542 in force Feb 2024
- 2035 mandate WEAKENED Dec 2025: 90% CO2 reduction (from full ICE ban) — allows e-fuels/biofuels for residual 10%
- EU countervailing duties on Chinese BEVs (Oct 2024): +17-35.3% on top of 10% MFN tariff
- Regulatory friction: mandate weakening reduces OEM BEV investment certainty beyond 2025

**USA (PARTIAL):**
- 100% tariff on Chinese BEV imports (Section 301, effective Aug 2024, raised from 25%) [USTR Sept 2024]
- EV battery tariffs: 7.5% -> 25% effective Aug 2024
- IRA $7,500 EV tax credit in place for qualifying vehicles (US-assembled, sourcing requirements)
- NO federal BEV production mandate (unlike China NEV dual-credit)
- Tariff effect: eliminates lowest-cost global BEV supply ($7,800-$16,000 China LFP) from US market

**Why:** China's regulatory environment is the global benchmark — escalating production mandates + consumer incentives + operational enforcement = READY. USA and EU face friction from trade barriers and mandate uncertainty. Cost-curve dynamics drive BEV adoption regardless of mandate; policy affects speed, not direction.

**How to apply:** Rate China regulatory as READY, USA and EU as PARTIAL for BEV passenger car analyses. Note the 100% US tariff as a supply-side friction reducing low-cost model availability, not a demand-side barrier. EU AFIR is a strong enabler; the weakened 2035 mandate is a low-severity friction for OEM planning.
