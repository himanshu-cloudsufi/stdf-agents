### Key Data Points (all observed, 2024)

**Infrastructure:**
- Li-ion manufacturing: 3.0 TWh capacity / 1.0 TWh demand = 33% utilization (3x surplus)
- CATL: 646 GWh installed capacity; BYD: 135 GWh
- USA EV highway corridor: 59.1% (Q4 2024, FHWA/Joint Office); build-out rate +5.3 pp/yr; model-derived 80% ~2028
- China EV corridor: ~98% (end-2024)
- Global Li-ion recycling: 450 GWh (2024), 75% China; USA: 35,500 tonnes reclaimed (2023)
- Lead-acid recycling: USA 99%, Europe 99%, China 48% (formal sector, 2023)

**Supply Chain:**
- Lithium production 2024: 1,323,000 MT LCE vs 1,189,000 MT LCE demand (134,000 MT LCE = 11.3% surplus)
- Lithium carbonate China price: $14,500/t (Jan 2024) -> $9,400/t (Nov 2024)
- LFP cathode consumed China: 1.934 million tonnes (2024)
- LFP installations China: 409 GWh (74.6% share of total), up 56.7% YoY
- China: 87% of global cathode production capacity; 65% of lithium refining
- Australia LFP mine: 386,000 MT LCE; South America: 265,000 MT LCE; China: 238,000 MT LCE

**Regulatory:**
- EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542: in force Feb 18, 2024; recycled content from Aug 2031 (85% lead, 6% Li)
- EU 2035 vehicle mandate: REVISED Dec 2025 — 90% CO2 reduction (not full ICE ban)
- China NEV penetration: 47.9% passenger cars 2024 (Dec 2024: 52.6%)
- US Section 301 tariffs: Li-ion EV batteries 7.5% -> 25% (Sept 27, 2024); non-EV batteries follow Jan 1, 2026

### Five Disruption Vectors — Readiness Notes

1. **BEV new-vehicle SLI** (810 kt): Infrastructure PARTIAL (USA 59.1% corridor); otherwise ready
2. **BEV fleet turnover SLI** (3,377 kt): Binding on USA corridor coverage; China already MET
3. **LFP UPS stationary** (1,987 kt): No infrastructure or supply chain barrier; regulatory READY in all regions
4. **LFP motive/forklifts** (913 kt): No barriers; 65% EV forklift share 2024 already demonstrates readiness
5. **LFP direct 12V SLI substitution**: Supply chain READY; SLI unit cost parity USA 2027-2028 still closing

---

**Key pattern for future runs:** Li-ion manufacturing supply chain is always READY for battery disruption analyses at current volumes. The binding constraints are (a) EV charging for fleet-turnover vectors and (b) regulatory friction (tariffs, mandate uncertainty) in non-China markets. China is reliably MET or PARTIAL; USA is the laggard region for battery disruptions.
