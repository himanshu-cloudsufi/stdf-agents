## Bloom Gas Segment: Key Validated Facts

**Fleet gas consumption (2024):**
- USA fleet (840 MW): 1.229 BCM/yr = 43.4 BCF/yr [model-derived, validated]
- Full fleet (1.2 GW): 1.756 BCM/yr = 62.0 BCF/yr [model-derived]
- % of US total gas: 0.133% (USA) to 0.190% (full fleet) — micro-segment
- This is ~8% of the US enterprise C&I distributed gas DG segment (~15.3 BCM)

**Supply source:** 100% domestic US shale gas — NO LNG exposure on input side
- USA production (2023): 1,070.5 BCM vs consumption 923.7 BCM = net exporter by 147 BCM
- Bloom's gas displacement (2024→2040) is too small to observe in Henry Hub pricing

**Gas price structural finding:**
- At 2024 all-time-low gas ($2.19/MMBtu), Bloom MC = $22.9/MWh
- 8hr BESS SCOE = $6.1/MWh (2024), declining to $3.4/MWh (2030)
- For Bloom to match BESS on marginal cost: gas would need to go NEGATIVE
- Conclusion: SWB always dispatches before Bloom regardless of gas price — a permanent cost-curve dynamics advantage

**Korea caveat:** Bloom Korea (~120 MW, ~30% of non-USA fleet) IS LNG-supplied
- Korea Gas Corporation sources predominantly LNG
- This creates LNG input exposure for ~15–18% of Bloom revenue
- Not modeled in this analysis due to Korean gas catalog data limitations

**Annual fuel cost exposure (full fleet, 1.756 BCM):**
- At $2.19/MMBtu: $0.139B/yr = 9% of Bloom revenue
- At $3.42/MMBtu (5yr avg): $0.216B/yr = 15% of Bloom revenue
- At $6.45/MMBtu (2022 spike): $0.408B/yr = 28% of Bloom revenue
- Most contracts pass fuel cost to customers — high gas prices motivate SWB switch at renewal

**China observed data (2023):**
- Total consumption: 395.3 BCM; domestic production: 239.4 BCM; pipeline imports: ~64 BCM; LNG: 97.8 BCM
- China LNG at 24.4% of supply mix — still growing in 2024 (107.6 BCM)
- LNG is the marginal (most expensive) source → displaced first by SWB disruption

**Europe observed data:**
- LNG imports: 172.1 BCM (2022 surge), 169.1 BCM (2023)
- US LNG = most expensive delivered source; displaced first in SWB disruption cascade

## Why

**How to apply:** When running future gas supply analyses for Bloom or similar distributed gas generation incumbents:
1. Always use technology-specific efficiency (0.58 for SOFC, 0.35–0.40 for reciprocating engines, 0.45 for CCGT)
2. Bloom's US fleet = 100% domestic shale — skip LNG supply chain analysis for US operations
3. The gas supply story is fuel COST EXPOSURE, not supply source displacement
4. Korea operations require separate analysis (LNG input exposure) when data available
