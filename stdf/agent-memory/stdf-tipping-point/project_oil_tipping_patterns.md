## Oil Demand Tipping Point (March 2026 Analysis)

**Key finding:** For oil demand as a multi-sector system, the tipping point is NOT determined by a single technology. It is the compound moment when transport + power gen + heating disruption collectively push oil demand into irreversible structural decline exceeding petrochemical demand growth (~1.5 Mbpd/yr offset through 2030).

**Tipping year determined:** 2027–2030 (global structural demand peak)

**Why:** Cost parity already crossed in transport (2015–2016) and power (pre-2013). Capability parity met for transport (7/8 dimensions) and power (5/6 as system). The binding constraint is fleet turnover rate (~6%/yr of 1.5B global fleet), creating 8–12 year lag between new-sale disruption and fleet-level oil demand destruction.

**How to apply:** In any multi-sector disruption (multiple sub-domains, each at different S-curve stages), always frame the "tipping point" as the demand peak (or supply-side inflection) rather than a single technology parity crossing. The slowest-moving sub-sector (heating) sets the structural floor but the dominant volume sub-sector (transport, 57% of demand) determines the peak timing.

## Fleet Turnover Math (Critical for Oil Demand Models)

- Global ICE fleet: ~1.5 billion vehicles
- Annual fleet replacement: ~90 million vehicles/yr (~6%/yr turnover rate)
- BEV fleet 2025: ~50 million (~3.3% of total fleet)
- BEV new-sale tipping (80% share) → fleet-level 20% BEV: ~2033
- BEV new-sale tipping → fleet-level 30% BEV: ~2036
- Each 10% of fleet converted to BEV ≈ ~5.9 Mbpd oil demand destruction
- Oil demand structural peak (net of petrochemical growth): 2027–2030

**Key rule for future analyses:** In transport disruption, always compute BOTH the new-sale tipping year AND the fleet-level displacement year. The gap is 8–12 years for automotive (6%/yr turnover). This gap determines the slope of incumbent demand decline, not the tipping point itself.

## Key Numbers for Post-Tipping Cycles

**Incumbent vicious cycle anchors (oil):**
- Retail fuel station throughput breakeven: ~2–3M liters/yr per station
- Station closures already underway: 25% of US gas stations closed 2000–2020
- Oil major upstream capex: $0.72T/yr (2014) → $0.45T/yr (2024), −37%
- Field natural decline rates: 4–6%/yr (requires continuous capex to maintain production)
- Each +$10 WTI spike → ICE +$0.003/km (which widens BEV advantage and accelerates adoption)

**Disruptor virtuous cycle anchors (BEV/SWB):**
- Battery learning rate: 16.8%/yr (empirically derived, Li-ion)
- LFP cell-level (China): ~$60–65/kWh (2025) → ~$45–50/kWh (2028–2030 model-derived)
- Charging infrastructure CAGR: 45.7% (2015–2024 observed)
- Target: <$22,000 manufactured cost for 60 kWh BEV ≈ 2028–2030
- V2G grid services revenue adds positive feedback loop

## Convergence Acceleration (Oil Context)

- SWB-EV: compresses oil demand peak by ~2–3 years vs BEV-alone scenario
- ASHP-SWB: 15–20 year heating system replacement cycle makes each installation ~15 years of permanent demand destruction
- Oil price spike → BOTH accelerates BEV and ASHP adoption simultaneously (2022 was the empirical proof point)
- Asymmetric irreversibility: adoption spikes are irreversible; price corrections only slow future switching rate (do NOT reverse already-completed switches)
