## BEV vs. ICE (Transport)
- **Pattern:** Sequential, 2010–2026 (14-year window)
- **Dimensions:** 12 total, 10 MET, 2 APPROACHING
- **APPROACHING gap sizes at PARTIAL parity:** battery_energy_density 2.5%, towing_range 10.0%
- **Multi-dimensional density event:** 5 dimensions in 2022–2024 window (range, TCO, purchase price, charge time, cold weather)
- **Implication:** Sequential pattern with late-window density spike is characteristic of Stellar technology disruptions — subsystem maturation sequence drives staggered threshold crossings

## General Observations
- APPROACHING gaps at PARTIAL status are typically 2–12% for Stellar technologies; gaps > 15% push status to NOT_MET
- Niche-segment blockers (towing, extreme cold) cross later than wide-population blockers (range, price, charge time)
- Sequential convergence does not prevent S-curve acceleration — the multi-dimensional parity event (when several dimensions cross in a 2–3 year window) triggers adoption inflection even before all dimensions are MET
- God Parity (ALL dimensions superior to incumbent) typically lags PARTIAL parity by 2–4 years in transport disruptions
