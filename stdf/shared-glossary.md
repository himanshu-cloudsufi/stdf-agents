# STDF Glossary

**All STDF agents must read this file for concept definitions.** These terms have precise meanings in the STDF framework and must be used consistently.

---

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **God Parity** | The point at which a disruptive technology achieves superiority over the incumbent across ALL major performance dimensions simultaneously — not just cost. |
| **X-Flow** | A technology whose value proposition is tied to physical resource throughput (e.g., oil refining, mining, freight logistics). Jevons Paradox may apply. |
| **Stellar** | A technology with zero or near-zero marginal cost characteristics (solar PV, wind, battery storage, AI/AL). Jevons Paradox does not apply — cost decline accelerates adoption without proportional resource rebound. |
| **Hybrid** | A technology exhibiting both X-Flow and Stellar characteristics. Classify by dominant component for downstream analysis. |
| **SWB** | Solar-Wind-Battery — the convergent energy system where solar PV, wind generation, and battery storage form an integrated disruption platform. |
| **BSAF** | Battery-Solar-Autonomous-Fleet — the convergent transport disruption combining battery EVs, solar charging, autonomous driving, and fleet/ride-hailing models. |
| **Chimera** | An incumbent-disruptor hybrid product that combines elements of both (e.g., plug-in hybrid vehicles). Chimeras exhibit a hump-shaped demand curve — rising during disruption transition, then declining as the pure disruptor dominates. |
| **Convergence** | When two or more independent disruptions combine to create emergent capabilities exceeding the sum of parts (e.g., SWB, BSAF). |
| **AL** | Artificial Labor — AI systems capable of performing economically productive work previously done by human workers. Treated as a Stellar technology. |
| **Cost-Curve Dynamics** | The empirical observation that technology costs decline predictably as a function of cumulative production/deployment. Replaces "Wright's Law" in STDF vocabulary. |
| **Incumbent Displacement** | The process by which a cost-superior disruptive technology replaces an incumbent. Replaces "phase-out" or "retirement" in STDF vocabulary. |
| **S-Curve Adoption** | The logistic growth pattern characteristic of technology disruptions: slow start, rapid middle phase, saturation. Replaces "linear growth" or "gradual adoption." |
| **Disruption** | A market-driven process where a technology with superior cost-curve dynamics displaces an incumbent. Replaces "transition" in STDF vocabulary. |
| **AI Capability Improvement** | The trajectory of AI/AL performance gains over time. Always use this term — never "AI capability growth." |
| **Digital Worker** | An AI/AL system deployed as a direct substitute for human labor in a specific economic function. Subset of AL. |
| **10x Factor** | The disruption threshold — a technology becomes 10x cheaper or better than the incumbent, triggering rapid displacement. Do not replace with "cost improvement." |
| **Phase Change** | A systemic transformation where the entire system reorganizes (caterpillar to butterfly). Do not replace with "transition" or "evolution." |
| **Metamorphosis Principle** | "Nothing in the caterpillar tells you it's going to be a butterfly." Past system behavior does not predict post-disruption outcomes. |
| **Rupture Point** | The moment a system moves out of equilibrium — typically at 2-5% market share. Do not replace with "tipping point" or "inflection." |
| **Death Spiral** | The incumbent vicious cycle: volume falls → unit costs rise → more customers defect → volume falls further. Do not replace with "decline" or "downturn." |
| **Market Trauma** | Financial collapse of incumbents that occurs before physical obsolescence — asset repricing, credit downgrades, stranded capital. Do not replace with "market decline." |
| **Stranded Assets** | Assets that lose economic value well before their physical end-of-life due to disruption. Do not replace with "obsolete assets." |
| **X-Curve** | The mirror of the S-Curve — maps the decline trajectory of the incumbent as the disruptor's S-curve rises. Do not replace with "decline curve." |
| **Choke Point** | A small percentage of a value chain that contains all the commercial value. Do not replace with "bottleneck" or "weak point." |
| **Stellar Energy** | Intentional massive energy surplus from the Clean Energy U-Curve. Also called "Superpower." Do not replace with "surplus energy" or "excess power." |
| **Clean Energy U-Curve** | The SWB generation overbuild optimization function — building more capacity than needed because marginal cost is near zero. Do not replace with "duck curve." |
| **Seed-Flow** | A one-time material/energy input that enables ongoing extraction-free flows. The economic model of Stellar technologies. |
| **Ignition Point** | The point at which a Stellar system becomes fully self-sustaining — energy/material inputs are themselves produced by Stellar systems. |
| **TaaS** | Transport-as-a-Service — autonomous electric vehicle fleets providing mobility as a service. Do not replace with "ride-sharing" or "MaaS." |
| **A-EV** | Autonomous Electric Vehicle. Do not replace with "self-driving EV." |
| **IO** | Individual Ownership — the legacy model of personal vehicle ownership that TaaS disrupts. |
| **Jevons Paradox** | Cost collapse expands demand 10x-100x. Applies to X-Flow technologies only. Do not replace with "rebound effect." |
| **Service Unit** | The fundamental unit of value in a disruption analysis ($/mile, $/kWh, $/task-hour). All cost comparisons must use the correct service unit. |
| **Economic Gravity** | The force of cost curves pulling adoption toward the cheaper technology. Markets follow cost curves. |
| **Virtuous Cycle** | Disruptor positive feedback: cost falls → adoption rises → scale increases → cost falls faster. |
| **Vicious Cycle** | Incumbent negative feedback: volume falls → unit cost rises → more defection → volume falls further. |
| **Precision Fermentation (PF)** | Microorganisms producing specific molecules at industrial scale — the core food disruption technology. Do not replace with "lab-grown" or "cultured." |
| **Second Domestication** | The shift from macro-organisms (livestock, crops) to micro-organisms (PF, cellular agriculture) as the primary food production system. |
| **Stellar Stack** | SWB Energy + AI/Robotics + Applications — the converging technology platform. |
| **Age of Freedom** | The post-disruption era of decentralization and zero marginal costs. |
| **Age of Superabundance** | The post-disruption era where cost collapse makes basic goods and services effectively free at point of use. |
