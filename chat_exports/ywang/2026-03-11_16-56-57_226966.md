# Data Center Connectivity: Scenario-Differentiated Winner/Loser Analysis (2026–2036)

## The Forcing Function: Power Physics, Not Preference

Data center electricity demand compounds at **12.0% CAGR globally** — from 521 TWh (2026) to 1,617 TWh (2036) [C1]. The USA alone scales from 226 TWh to 701 TWh (3.1×) [C1]. This is not a demand management problem; it is a phase change in compute economics. The optical interconnect consequence is deterministic: annual US optics CAPEX expands from **$14.7B (2026) to $164.6B (2036)** — an 11.2× multiplication [C2]. Four transceiver generations deploy in ten years: 800G → 1.6T → 3.2T → 6.4T [C2]. The technology that wins each generation captures an exponentially larger market than the one before.

Analysis reflects cost-curve dynamics; regulatory/adoption lags excluded unless specifically requested.

---

## Technology Cost Curves (The Non-Negotiable Foundation)

| Technology | 2024 $/Gbps | 2030 $/Gbps | Reduction | Learning Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| EML Pluggable | $1.50 | $0.60 | −60% | 22% [C3] |
| SiPh Pluggable | $1.20 | $0.30 | −75% | 28% [C3] |
| LPO (Linear-drive) | $0.90 | $0.22 | −76% | 30% [C3] |
| CPO (Co-packaged) | $0.80 (2026) | $0.18 | −78% | 35% [C3] |

CPO's 35% learning rate is the fastest of any interconnect architecture. At 3.2T, CPO delivers **8–12W/port** vs. pluggable's **60–100W/port** — an 8× power advantage that crosses the disruption threshold [C3]. The cost-curve math is unambiguous: CPO wins the AI cluster fabric by 2029–2031 [C3].

## Scenario 1: Silicon Photonics Dominance (2026–2030 primary window)

**Thesis:** SiPh's 28% learning rate vs. EML's 22% means SiPh cost/Gbps falls 3× faster per production doubling. STMicroelectronics [[COMPANY:CO1]] entered high-volume 300mm SiPh production in March 2026 [C4], locking in the cost trajectory. By 2028, SiPh pluggable at $0.70/Gbps undercuts EML at $1.00/Gbps by 30% — crossing the cost parity threshold.

**Winners:**

- **Broadcom [[COMPANY:CO2]] (AVGO.US)** — Tomahawk5 51.2T switch ASIC with integrated SiPh; CPO partnership with Ayar Labs; dominant hyperscaler design wins across AWS, Meta, Google, Microsoft. Wins in every scenario. Composite disruption score: 0.902 [C5].
- **Marvell Technology [[COMPANY:CO3]] (MRVL.US)** — Custom AI ASIC platform (Amazon Trainium/Inferentia); SiPh DSP platform; Teralynx switch ASICs; 1.6T DSP chips in sampling. Appears in all four investment baskets. Composite score: 0.853 [C5].
- **Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing [[COMPANY:CO4]] (TSM.US)** — Sole foundry for advanced SiPh at N3/N2; CoWoS advanced packaging = CPO manufacturing backbone. Every SiPh ASIC and CPO chiplet runs through TSMC. Composite score: 0.783 [C5].

The ASIC design cycle (3–4 years) is the primary timing constraint — cost curves already favor CPO at 1.6T.

**Critical path:** Hyperscaler CPO ASIC commitment (2026–2027) → tape-out (2027–2028) → validation (2028–2029) → volume deployment (2029–2030) [C3].

## Supply Chain Concentration Risk

The single most critical systemic risk in this analysis: **TSMC's 300mm SiPh and CoWoS capacity concentration in Taiwan**. A geopolitical disruption to TSMC's SiPh production would delay CPO rupture by 3–5 years and extend the pluggable supercycle, creating a temporary reprieve for EML incumbents [C3]. This is not a reason to avoid TSMC — it is the reason TSMC commands a structural premium. Intel Foundry Services and STMicroelectronics [[COMPANY:CO1]] are the only credible alternative SiPh foundry nodes, but both are 1–2 generations behind TSMC's SiPh process.

**Secondary concentration risk:** China's domestic optical supply chain (Eoptolink, Innolight) is captive to SMIC N7 ceiling, creating a structural CPO capability gap that cannot be closed without TSMC access [C3].
