## I. DISRUPTION ARCHITECTURE: DUAL-VECTOR FRAMEWORK

Two independent cost-curve forces converge on the same rupture window:

**Vector A — Chinese ODM Cost Dominance (Rupture: 2025–2026)**
Chinese ODMs deliver 800G pluggable modules at a 33–38% ASP discount vs. Western incumbents ($1,200 vs. $1,800 per module in 2024) [C1]. The structural driver is a 22% cost-curve learning rate vs. 15% for Western branded modules [C1]. With 800G volume growing ~2.8x annually, the gap widens — not converges. By 2026, Chinese 800G modules are projected at ~$550 vs. Western ~$900 — a 39% discount [C1]. Hyperscalers are actively qualifying Chinese ODMs directly, bypassing Western module assemblers. This is not a future risk; it is an active margin compression event.

**Vector B — CPO Architectural Disruption (Rupture: 2027–2028)**
Co-packaged optics eliminates the pluggable module form factor by integrating photonics directly onto the switch ASIC package. CPO delivers 2–3x better power efficiency (~5.0 pJ/bit for pluggable 800G → ~1.5 pJ/bit for CPO 1.6T) and a projected cost/Gbps of $0.90 by 2027 — undercutting Western pluggable ($1.75/Gbps) by 49% and Chinese ODM pluggable ($1.13/Gbps) by 20% [C2]. The three tipping-point conditions converge in 2027: CPO cost crossover, first hyperscaler deployments, and Broadcom [[COMPANY:CO1]] / Marvell [[COMPANY:CO2]] CPO switch ASICs entering production [C2].

**Convergence Logic:** Vector A destroys Western/Taiwanese assembler economics via cost competition by 2026. Vector B eliminates the pluggable assembly model for new AI cluster builds by 2028. The assembly layer faces sequential double disruption — first margin compression, then structural elimination.
