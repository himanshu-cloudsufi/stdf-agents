### Methodology

This analysis uses bottom-up task-level modeling across 186 occupational categories:
- **AI capability growth:** 7-month doubling time (based on METR empirical data 2020-2025)
- **Automation threshold:** 80% reliability (autonomous execution standard)
- **Task weighting:** Relevance × Importance scores per task
- **Binary model:** Tasks either automatable (1) or not (0) based on AI capability vs. task duration

### Required Caveats

1. **Coverage:** Analysis covers 93.1M US jobs (~60% of workforce). Scaling assumes uncovered occupations have similar profiles.

2. **Adoption Lag:** Model assumes instant adoption when AI capability is reached. In practice, corporate adoption lags 12-24 months behind technical capability.

3. **Job Creation Not Modeled:** This forecasts displacement only. Does not account for new jobs created by AI or economic adaptation (historically 30-50% offset in major technology transitions).

4. **Binary Simplification:** Tasks modeled as fully automatable or not. In practice, partial automation and human-AI collaboration are common transition states.

5. **Physical Automation:** Jobs requiring physical manipulation (manual labor, healthcare physical tasks) depend on robotics deployment, which may lag cognitive AI capabilities.

### Step 1: AI Capability Growth Model

AI capability at 80% reliability follows exponential growth with 7-month doubling time:

$$C(t) = C_0 \cdot 2^{t/T_d}$$

Where:
- \( C(t) \) = AI capability in seconds at time \( t \)
- \( C_0 \) = 935 seconds (baseline capability, 2024)
- \( T_d \) = 7 months (doubling time)
- \( t \) = months since 2024

| Year | Months from 2024 | Capability (seconds) | Human Time Equivalent |
|------|------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| 2024 | 0 | 935 | 15.6 minutes |
| 2025 | 12 | 1,560 | 26.0 minutes |
| 2026 | 24 | 5,190 | 1.4 hours |

| 2028 | 48 | 57,450 | 16.0 hours |

### Step 2: Job-Level Automation Calculation

For each job category \( j \), automation rate is calculated as:

$$A_j(t) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \cdot \mathbb{1}[C(t) \geq d_i]}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i}$$

Where:
- \( A_j(t) \) = Automation percentage for job \( j \) at time \( t \)
- \( w_i = \frac{r_i}{100} \times \frac{im_i}{100} \) = Weight (relevance × importance)
- \( d_i \) = Task duration in seconds
- \( \mathbb{1}[C(t) \geq d_i] \) = Binary indicator (1 if task is automatable, 0 otherwise)

Jobs displaced in job category \( j \):

$$D_j(t) = N_j \cdot A_j(t)$$

Where \( N_j \) = Total employment in job category \( j \)

### Step 3: Sector-Level Aggregation

For each sector \( s \) containing job categories \( J_s \):

$$D_s(t) = \sum_{j \in J_s} D_j(t)$$

$$A_s(t) = \frac{D_s(t)}{\sum_{j \in J_s} N_j} \times 100\%$$
