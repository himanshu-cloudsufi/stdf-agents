High ambient temperatures (40–50°C summer peaks in UAE/Saudi) do create a PUE penalty relative to temperate climates — but the penalty is quantifiable and manageable, and the energy cost advantage more than compensates.

**Mitigation technologies already deployed**:
- **Liquid cooling / direct-to-chip**: Eliminates ambient temperature sensitivity for compute density. District cooling COP (coefficient of performance) of 5–7 vs. air cooling COP of 2–3 means district-cooled data centers can achieve PUE ~1.2–1.3 even in extreme heat [C10].
