China lead demand peaks in 2026 at approximately 5,800 kt [C1], driven by replacement battery demand from the legacy ICE vehicle fleet [C2]. Demand declines 32% by 2035 to 3,950 kt [C3] as lithium-ion batteries displace lead-acid across vehicle, industrial, and stationary applications.

**Peak Timing and Magnitude**

China's lead demand reaches maximum in 2026 [C1], representing the final year of near-zero growth (+0.2%) before structural decline begins. The peak occurs 5-7 years after China's ICE vehicle fleet peaked (~2020-2022), consistent with the replacement battery demand lag structure [C2]. Vehicle-related applications account for 62.5% of total demand in 2024 [C4], with replacement batteries comprising 76% of that segment [C5].

**Displacement Dynamics**

Lithium-ion cost parity was achieved across most applications by 2024-2025 [C6], triggering S-curve adoption. China's >50% EV penetration in 2024 [C7] eliminates 15-20 kg of lead demand per vehicle sold. The displacement extends beyond vehicles: industrial motive power (forklifts) shows flat growth (0.7% annually 2019-2024) [C8], while stationary applications face rapid lithium-ion substitution in grid storage despite modest overall growth (6.5% annually) [C9]. Non-battery uses declined 8.7% from 2010-2024 [C10].

**Post-Peak Trajectory**

| Year | Demand (kt) | Change | Driver |
|------|-------------|--------|--------|
| 2024 | 5,762 | baseline | Growth <1% annually; EV penetration >50% |
| 2025 | 5,791 | +0.5% | Final growth year; replacement demand sustains |
| 2026 | 5,802 | +0.2% | **PEAK**: Replacement demand plateaus |
| 2027 | 5,773 | -0.5% | Decline begins; EV fleet growth dominates |
| 2030 | 5,108 | -12% from peak | ICE fleet shrinking; lithium-ion dominates |
| 2035 | 3,952 | -32% from peak | Lead-acid relegated to legacy replacement only |

Demand declines at 4% annually 2028-2030, accelerating to 5% annually 2031-2035 [C3] as the ICE fleet shrinks and lithium-ion achieves full cost and performance dominance.

**Policy and Supply Context**

China's EV mandate (40% by 2030, exceeded in 2024) [C7] and ICE restrictions in major cities accelerate displacement. Lead-acid battery recycling exceeds 90% [C11], sustaining supply but not increasing demand. China controls 70%+ of global lithium-ion manufacturing capacity [C12], creating a domestic cost advantage that accelerates adoption. Supply-side constraints are non-binding; displacement is demand-driven, not supply-constrained [C13].

**Methodology Note**

Analysis derives China demand from global lead consumption data (2010-2024) scaled by China's ~47% share of global demand [C14], reflecting dominance in battery manufacturing and vehicle production. China-specific datasets are unavailable; projection confidence is high for trajectory and medium for exact peak year (2025-2027 range).
