**Lead demand will decline by 10% from 2026 baseline levels in 2030.**

From a 2026 baseline of 6,566 kilotonnes, global lead demand for battery applications drops to 5,667 kt in 2030—a 13.7% decline (899 kt absolute reduction). The 10% threshold (5,910 kt) is crossed in 2030, driven by lithium-ion displacement across automotive, industrial, and stationary applications.

## Displacement Dynamics

The 899 kt decline between 2026 and 2030 results from three converging mechanisms:

**1. ICE Passenger Car Fleet Shrinkage (799 kt decline, 88.8% of total)**

The global ICE passenger car fleet contracts from 906 million vehicles (2026) to 785 million (2030)—a 121 million vehicle decline. Each ICE vehicle contains approximately 11 kg of lead in its SLI (starting, lighting, ignition) battery. With a 4-year replacement cycle, this fleet reduction eliminates ~800 kt of annual lead demand. Meanwhile, the BEV fleet expands from 55 million to 139 million vehicles (+152%), but most BEVs still use lead-acid 12V auxiliary batteries in 2030 (Li-ion auxiliary penetration remains below 5% globally).

**2. Commercial Vehicle Electrification + Li-ion SLI Adoption (233 kt decline, 25.9% of total)**

Commercial vehicle electrification accelerates sharply: China reaches 66% EV share, USA and Europe reach 78% and 73% respectively by 2030. Simultaneously, Li-ion SLI batteries achieve cost parity in B2B applications (2026-2027 tipping point), driving rapid adoption in the remaining ICE/NGV commercial fleet. USA and Europe reach 29% Li-ion SLI penetration by 2030; China and Rest of World lag at 4% due to cost sensitivity.

**3. Industrial and Stationary Applications (116 kt decline, 12.9% of total)**

Forklifts: Li-ion reaches TCO parity in 2024-2025, driving S-curve adoption (10% → 80% over 10 years). Lead demand declines 82 kt.

Datacenter and Telecom UPS: Li-ion adoption accelerates in B2B stationary applications due to superior cycle life (1,000+ cycles vs. 500 for VRLA) and energy density. Combined decline: 34 kt.

**Offsetting Growth: E2W/E3W LAB Traction (+149 kt)**

Electric two- and three-wheeler fleets expand rapidly in Asia, and many still use lead-acid traction batteries (Li-ion adoption incomplete). This growth partially offsets declines in other segments, reducing net displacement by 149 kt.

## Cost-Curve Dynamics

**Lithium-ion: Wright's Law trajectory**
- 2010: ~$1,000/kWh → 2024: ~$100/kWh (China: $84/kWh) → 2030: <$60/kWh projected
- Learning rate: ~18% cost reduction per cumulative doubling of production
- Superior capability: 150+ Wh/kg energy density, 1,000+ cycle life, 1C+ discharge rate

**Lead-acid: Commodity with no learning curve**
- Lead metal price: ~$2,100/tonne (2024), flat over time
- LAB battery: ~$100-150/kWh, no improvement trend
- Inferior capability: 40 Wh/kg energy density, 500 cycle life, 0.2C discharge rate

**TCO Parity Timing:**
- Commercial Vehicle SLI: 2026-2027 (B2B applications tip first)
- Forklift: 2024-2025 (TCO parity achieved)
- Grid/Datacenter UPS: 2023-2025 (cycle life advantage dominates)
- EV 12V Auxiliary: 2025-2027 (performance + integration benefits)
- Passenger Car SLI: Not yet reached (Li-ion remains premium in B2C)

The displacement follows classic S-curve adoption: B2B segments tip first (cost-driven), B2C segments follow (price parity + performance). Post-tipping, adoption accelerates from 10% to 80% over approximately 10 years.

## (2) S-Curve Adoption Parameters

**S-Curve Formula:**
```
Li_ion_Share_SLI(t) = L / (1 + exp(-k × (t - t₀)))
```

**Parameters:**
- **L (Ceiling):** 1.0 (100% saturation). The skill explicitly documents: "Tony's feedback: 'When cost ruptures, full disruption is expected.'" All SLI ceilings (AUX_LI_ION_CEILING, SLI_LI_ION_CEILING, CV_SLI_LI_ION_CEILING) are set to 1.0.
- **k (Growth Rate):** DEFAULT_K = 0.44 (standard disruption rate). This value equals ln(81)/10, calibrated to achieve 10% to 90% adoption over 10 years post-tipping.
- **t₀ (Inflection Point):** tipping_year + 3 (inflection occurs ~3 years after cost parity is reached)

**Pre-Tipping Adoption:**

**Post-Tipping Adoption:**
- Standard logistic S-curve with k=0.44
- 10% → 50% (inflection) → 90% over ~10 years
- Reaches 100% saturation (L=1.0) by ~15-20 years post-tipping

**Fallback Heuristic (if insufficient historical data):**
- 10% to 80% adoption over 10 years post-tipping
- Used when historical data points < 3 or fitting fails
