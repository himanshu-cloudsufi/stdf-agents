# Military Drone Disruption Analysis: Tanks & Artillery Displacement

## Analysis Approach

This analysis applies the Tony Seba disruption framework to military systems, treating battlefield effectiveness as the "service delivered." The core thesis: **Drone swarms + autonomous loitering munitions represent a cost-capability convergence that creates an asymmetric advantage**, similar to how EVs disrupt ICE vehicles—not through direct 1:1 replacement, but by delivering the same service (ground attack, fire support) at radically lower cost per engagement with superior attributes (expendability, precision, real-time ISR integration, swarm coordination).

**Key disruption drivers:**
- **Cost curve decline:** Commercial drone tech + AI/autonomy learning curves drive exponential cost reductions (Wright's Law dynamics in semiconductors, batteries, sensors)
- **Capability convergence:** Precision-guided munitions + real-time targeting + swarm tactics enable drones to deliver tank/artillery firepower effects at 1/100th to 1/1000th the platform cost
- **Asymmetric economics:** $500-$5,000 FPV drones destroying $2M-$10M tanks creates unsustainable cost exchange ratio for legacy armor
- **Rapid iteration cycles:** Software-defined warfare allows monthly capability upgrades vs. 10-20 year tank development cycles
