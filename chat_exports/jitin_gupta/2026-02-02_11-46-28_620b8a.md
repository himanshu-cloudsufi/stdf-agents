China's 12V SLI lead-acid battery market enters terminal decline from 2026 through 2030, driven by the cost-curve economics of electric vehicle disruption. Demand collapses from 81.2 million units in 2026 to 71.0 million units in 2030 [C1], a 12.6% decline [C2]. Revenue falls from $9.69 billion to $8.47 billion over the same period [C1]. This is not cyclical downturn—it is permanent displacement.

**Market Size Trajectory**

Annual SLI battery demand declines at an accelerating rate: -2.4% in 2027, -3.0% in 2028, -3.6% in 2029, and -4.2% in 2030 [C3]. The CAGR from 2026 to 2030 is -3.3% [C4]. The acceleration pattern reflects the steepening S-curve adoption of BEVs in China, where BEV market share rises from 43.9% in 2026 to 72.8% in 2030 [C5].

**Growth Drivers (All Negative)**

1. **ICE Vehicle Production Collapse**: ICE vehicle production (passenger + commercial) falls from 11.5 million units in 2026 to 5.6 million units in 2030, a 50.9% decline [C6]. Fewer new ICE vehicles means fewer OEM SLI batteries sold.

2. **ICE Fleet Decline**: The installed base of ICE vehicles (passenger + commercial) shrinks from 268.7 million in 2026 to 227.4 million in 2030, a 15.4% decline [C7]. A shrinking fleet directly reduces replacement battery demand.

3. **BEV Displacement**: China's BEV fleet grows from 31.6 million vehicles in 2026 to 72.3 million in 2030 [C8]. Every BEV uses a lithium-ion 12V auxiliary battery, not lead-acid SLI. By 2030, BEVs displace 20.7 million SLI battery replacement cycles annually [C9]—equivalent to 29% of 2026 total demand.

4. **Industrial Applications**: Industrial SLI demand (backup power, forklifts, stationary) is stable at ~15-20 million units per year but represents only 20% of the market. This stability does not offset the 10.2 million unit decline in automotive demand [C2].

**Competitive Pressures**

1. **Lithium-Ion 12V Auxiliary Batteries**: All BEVs use lithium-ion 12V batteries. These offer 5-10x longer cycle life (2,000-3,000 cycles vs. 300-500), 75% lower weight, and wider operating temperature range. Cost is falling along Wright's Law: from $250 per unit in 2024 to an estimated $145 in 2030 and $120 by 2032 [C10]. Cost parity with lead-acid SLI ($100) occurs around 2032, after which lithium-ion becomes cheaper. Post-2032, even PHEVs and remaining ICE vehicles may transition to lithium-ion 12V batteries, accelerating lead-acid displacement beyond the BEV-driven collapse.

2. **AGM Battery Cannibalization**: Absorbent Glass Mat (AGM) batteries—a premium lead-acid technology—are gaining share in the declining SLI market, rising from ~12% in 2026 to ~20% in 2030 [C11]. AGM offers better performance (faster charging, deeper discharge) but costs $150-200 vs. $80-120 for flooded lead-acid. AGM growth is a share shift within a shrinking market: absolute AGM unit growth (+4.5 million units, 2026-2030) does not offset total SLI decline (-10.2 million units) [C2].

**Regional Supply Chain Dynamics**

China produces ~60% of global lead-acid batteries, with major manufacturers including Tianneng, Chaowei, Camel Group, and Leoch [C12]. Total production capacity is ~300 million units per year (automotive + industrial). Domestic automotive SLI demand declining at 3-4% per year creates overcapacity risk. Capacity utilization is projected to fall from ~85% in 2025 to ~70% in 2030 [C13].

Export markets offer no relief. Global ICE vehicle production peaks between 2025 and 2028, then declines as EV adoption accelerates worldwide. Lead-acid SLI battery plants have 15-20 year economic lives; investments made from 2015-2020 face premature obsolescence and stranded asset risk [C14].

**Methodology**

This analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill, a canonical bottom-up model for vehicle fleet projections. SLI-requiring vehicles include ICE passenger cars, PHEV passenger cars, ICE commercial vehicles, and NGV commercial vehicles. BEVs are excluded (they use lithium-ion 12V auxiliary batteries). Annual SLI demand = fleet × replacement rate, where replacement cycle = 3.5 years (industry standard 3-4 years, conservative mid-point) [C15]. Revenue estimates use $100 per passenger vehicle battery and $200 per commercial vehicle battery [C16].

Fleet projections are derived from S-curve adoption modeling with tipping points detected at 2022 (passenger cars) and 2024 (commercial vehicles) based on cost-parity crossover [C17]. The model uses weighted regression to fit logistic curves and includes chimera modeling for transitional technologies (NGV commercial vehicles).

**Conclusion**

China's 12V SLI lead-acid battery market has zero positive growth drivers. ICE vehicle production falls 50.9%, the ICE fleet shrinks 15.4%, and BEV displacement eliminates 20.7 million replacement cycles annually by 2030 [C6][C7][C9]. This is structural collapse, not cyclical downturn. Each BEV added to the fleet eliminates one SLI battery replacement cycle forever.

Lead-acid SLI manufacturers face stranded asset risk. The market window is closing: by 2035, demand will be a fraction of 2025 levels, confined to legacy ICE vehicles and low-cost replacement segments. Manufacturers must pivot to lithium-ion production or exit. AGM adoption and industrial applications provide marginal support but cannot reverse the trajectory dictated by cost-curve economics.
