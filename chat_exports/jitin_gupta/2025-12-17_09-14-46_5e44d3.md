## AI Capability Growth Model

The core displacement driver is exponential AI capability growth at 80% reliability threshold:

$$
C(t) = C_0 \times 2^{(t - t_0)/\tau}
$$

Where:
- \( C(t) \) = AI capability in seconds at year \( t \)
- \( C_0 \) = Baseline capability (1,560 seconds in 2025)
- \( \tau \) = Doubling time (7 months = 0.583 years)
- \( t_0 \) = Base year (2025)

**2027 Calculation**:
$$
C(2027) = 1,560 \times 2^{(2027-2025)/0.583} = 1,560 \times 2^{3.43} = 17,270 \text{ seconds} = 4.8 \text{ hours}
$$

## Task Automation Scoring

For each job category, automation percentage calculated as:

$$
A_j = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \times \mathbb{1}_{C(t) \geq d_i}}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i}
$$

Where:
- \( A_j \) = Automation percentage for job \( j \)
- \( w_i \) = Weight for task \( i \) = (relevance/100) × (importance/100)
- \( d_i \) = Duration of task \( i \) in seconds
- \( \mathbb{1}_{C(t) \geq d_i} \) = Binary indicator (1 if automatable, 0 otherwise)

**Example: Customer Service Representatives**

| Task | Duration | Relevance | Importance | Weight | 2027 Automatable? |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|--------|-------------------|
| Handle customer inquiries | 1,800s (30 min) | 95 | 90 | 0.855 | ✓ (4.8h > 30min) |
| Process returns | 900s (15 min) | 85 | 80 | 0.680 | ✓ |
| Escalate complex issues | 3,600s (1 hour) | 70 | 95 | 0.665 | ✓ |

Weighted automation: (0.855 + 0.680 + 0.665) / (0.855 + 0.680 + 0.665) = **100%**

### **Key Risks to Thesis**

**Bull Case Risks** (Could Reduce Displacement):
1. **Adoption Lag**: Model assumes instant deployment when AI capable. Reality: corporate adoption lags 12-24 months. Could delay 2027 displacement to 2028-2029.

2. **Robotics Bottleneck**: 100% automation for manual labor (6.95M) and transportation (4.92M) requires physical hardware matching cognitive AI. May lag 2-3 years.

3. **Regulatory Friction**: Labor protection laws, union resistance, AI safety regulations could slow deployment 1-2 years. EU AI Act already imposing high-risk robotics assessments.

4. **Human-AI Collaboration**: Partial automation (humans handle 20-30% high-judgment tasks, AI handles 70-80% routine) reduces net displacement by 40-50%.

**Bear Case Risks** (Could Accelerate Displacement):
1. **Faster Capability Growth**: If doubling time accelerates from 7 months → 5 months, 2027 automation rate jumps to 95%+ (vs. 90.7%).

2. **Cost Parity Earlier**: If AI inference costs drop faster than expected (<$0.05/hour by 2026), enterprises accelerate adoption. Current trajectory: $0.10-$1/hour by 2027.

3. **Unemployment Feedback Loop**: 84M displaced workers → reduced consumer spending → recession → enterprises accelerate automation to cut costs further.

## Conclusion

The AI automation ecosystem creates a **binary outcome** for value chain participants:

**Structural Winners**: AI infrastructure (NVIDIA, AMD), hyperscale cloud (Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet), and enterprise automation platforms (ServiceNow, Workday) capture exponential growth as 84.4M jobs transition to AI execution.

**Structural Losers**: Traditional labor intermediaries (staffing agencies, BPO providers, offshore IT services) face 30-95% revenue decline as their core business model (arbitraging human labor costs) becomes obsolete.

The critical insight: **This is not a cyclical labor market shift**. AI's 7-month capability doubling time and 95-98% cost advantage create a permanent structural displacement. Companies concentrated in declining nodes cannot "wait out" the disruption—they must fundamentally transform business models or face extinction.

**Investment positioning** (2024-2027):
- **Short**: Labor-intensive services with no credible AI pivot (staffing, BPO, offshore IT)
- **Long**: AI infrastructure picks-and-shovels (NVIDIA, Microsoft Azure, AWS)
- **Selective Long**: Enterprise automation platforms capturing mid-market deployment (ServiceNow, Workday)
- **Avoid**: Companies with ambiguous exposure requiring complex segmentation analysis (IBM, Oracle, Salesforce)

The **2027 inflection point** (4.8-hour AI capability, 90.7% automation) represents the moment when AI transitions from "automating tasks" to "displacing occupations." Investors positioned ahead of this shift capture 5-10× returns. Those caught on the wrong side face 50-90% drawdowns.
