## Disruption Dynamics

**Primary collapse driver: SLI battery displacement in passenger cars.**

SLI (starter-lighting-ignition) batteries in ICE passenger cars represent 37% of 2026 lead demand (2,938 kt). This segment collapses 27% by 2030 and 82% by 2036 as BEV adoption accelerates globally. China reaches BEV cost parity in 2022; USA and Europe follow by 2024-2026. Post-tipping, S-curve adoption drives ICE fleet decline at 10-15% annually.

**Commercial vehicle SLI demand declines slower** due to longer asset lifetimes (20 years vs 15 years for passenger cars) and later EV tipping points (2024-2027 by region). NGV (natural gas vehicles) in China add a chimera layer but decay post-2027 as EV cost advantage widens.

**BEV/PHEV 12V auxiliary batteries temporarily boost lead demand** (+81% by 2030) as EV fleets grow, but lithium-ion 12V auxiliary adoption accelerates post-2029, reversing this trend (-50% by 2036).

**Two-wheeler and three-wheeler segments** show mixed dynamics: electrification increases LAB traction battery demand through 2030 (+12% for 2W/3W traction), but lithium-ion chemistry adoption within the EV segment drives subsequent decline post-2030 (-23% by 2036).

**Forklift LAB demand declines steadily** (-12% by 2030, -17% by 2036) as lithium-ion forklifts reach cost parity and capture market share via superior cycle life and energy density.

## Methodology

**Source:** Lithium-ion demand skill (canonical bottom-up model)

**Scope:** Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World aggregated)

**Segments modeled:**
- SLI batteries: ICE passenger cars, ICE+NGV commercial vehicles
- EV auxiliary: BEV/PHEV 12V batteries (LAB share)
- Starter batteries: ICE two-wheelers, ICE three-wheelers
- Traction batteries: E2W LAB, E3W LAB
- Forklift batteries: LAB share
- Stationary: Datacenter VRLA, Telecom VRLA
- Non-battery uses: 20% of battery demand (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding)

**Key assumptions:**
- Lead content: 11.0 kg/ICE car, 10.5 kg/PHEV, 0.0 kg/BEV (lithium-ion auxiliary)
- SLI replacement rate: 25%/year (4-year battery life)
- Vehicle lifetime: 15 years (passenger), 20 years (commercial)
- EV tipping points: China 2022 (passenger), USA 2026, Europe 2024
- S-curve adoption: 10% to 80% over 10 years post-tipping (fitted to historical data where available)

**Calculation:**
- OEM demand = Vehicle sales × Lead content × LAB share
- Replacement demand = Fleet size × Replacement rate × Lead content × LAB share
- Total = OEM + Replacement + Non-battery uses

## Implications

Lead demand enters a structural decline driven by vehicle electrification. The 10% reduction milestone (2030) marks the inflection point where EV adoption accelerates beyond early-market dynamics. Post-2030, lead demand collapses at 8-11% annually as ICE fleets shrink and lithium-ion displaces lead-acid across all battery applications.

By 2036, lead demand falls to 47% of 2026 levels (3,780 kt), driven primarily by the near-complete collapse of ICE passenger car SLI demand (-87% from 2026). Commercial vehicle SLI and forklift LAB applications provide temporary demand floors due to slower fleet turnover, but these segments also decline as EV cost advantages widen and lithium-ion chemistry adoption accelerates.

The lead-acid battery industry faces market trauma: a 12% demand loss by 2030 compresses margins, triggering capacity closures and stranded asset write-downs. Fixed costs (smelting, refining, recycling infrastructure) become unsustainable as utilization rates fall. Investment capital exits the sector, accelerating the vicious cycle of volume loss, price pressure, and capacity rationalization.

This analysis excludes regulatory interventions (e.g., accelerated ICE phase-outs, lead-acid battery bans) and assumes base-case EV adoption dynamics. Faster EV adoption or policy-driven ICE restrictions would accelerate the timeline; slower adoption would delay but not reverse the structural decline.
