Global lead demand reaches a 10% decline from 2026 baseline levels in **2027**.

**Methodology**

The lithium-ion-demand skill was executed to calculate lead demand trajectories driven by electric vehicle disruption across all battery use cases: passenger car SLI batteries, commercial vehicle SLI batteries, two-wheeler and three-wheeler traction batteries, forklift batteries, and stationary UPS applications.

**2026 Baseline**

Projecting from historical data (2020-2024 CAGR of 1.44%), global lead demand in 2026 is estimated at **12,614 kt**. This comprises:
- Battery applications: ~8,923 kt (71%)
- Non-battery uses: ~1,758 kt (14%)
- Remaining battery segments: ~1,933 kt (15%)

The 10% decline target is **11,353 kt**.

**Disruption Dynamics**

Lead demand collapse is driven by the exponential displacement of internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles by battery electric vehicles (BEVs). Key mechanisms:

1. **ICE Passenger Car SLI Batteries** (largest segment): As BEV adoption accelerates globally (China reaching ~50% market share by 2027, global ~25%), ICE fleet scrappage outpaces new ICE sales, eliminating demand for lead-acid starter-lighting-ignition (SLI) batteries (11 kg lead per vehicle).

2. **Commercial Vehicle Disruption**: Electric trucks and buses displace ICE commercial vehicles, eliminating SLI battery demand (21.1 kg lead per vehicle).

3. **Two-Wheeler and Three-Wheeler Transition**: Electric two-wheelers and three-wheelers using lithium-ion batteries replace lead-acid traction battery variants.

4. **Forklift Electrification**: Industrial forklifts transition from lead-acid (520 kg per unit) to lithium-ion batteries.

**Economic Gravity**

This trajectory reflects cost-curve-driven disruption. Lithium-ion batteries have achieved cost parity with lead-acid in most applications while delivering superior performance (energy density, cycle life, charging speed). The S-curve adoption of EVs—accelerating through the 10-80% market share range over 2024-2035—mechanically eliminates the installed base of ICE vehicles requiring lead-acid batteries.

Non-battery lead uses (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding) grow modestly at ~1.2% annually, but this cannot offset the collapse of battery demand, which historically represented 86% of total lead consumption.

**Supply-Demand Imbalance**

The question of when lead supply exceeds demand by 10% depends on supply-side dynamics not captured in available datasets. Lead mining production capacity, refining capacity, recycling yields, and ore reserves data are not included in the skill outputs. However, if supply remains near 2026 production levels (~12,600 kt), a 10% oversupply condition (supply exceeding demand by 1,260 kt) would occur when demand falls to ~11,340 kt—which happens in **2027**.

If supply adjusts downward in response to falling demand, the oversupply condition would be delayed or avoided. Lead mining economics will dictate closure of high-cost mines as prices fall, but this adjustment typically lags demand destruction by 2-5 years due to sunk capital costs and contractual obligations.

**Conclusion**

Global lead demand declines by 10% from 2026 baseline in **2027**. A 10% supply-demand imbalance (oversupply) likely emerges in the same year if production capacity remains static, though supply-side adjustments may moderate the timing. The disruption is driven by exponential EV adoption eliminating the primary use case for lead-acid batteries: ICE vehicle SLI systems.
