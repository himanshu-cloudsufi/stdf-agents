### Methodology: Task-Level Bottom-Up Forecasting

This analysis uses **capability-threshold displacement modeling** rather than traditional econometric approaches. Jobs are decomposed into constituent tasks, each evaluated for automation readiness.

#### Step 1: AI Capability Growth Projection

AI task-handling capability follows empirical 7-month doubling time:

$$
C(t) = C_0 \times 2^{(t - t_0)/\tau}
$$

Where:
- \( C(t) \) = AI capability at time \( t \) (seconds of task complexity at 80% reliability)
- \( C_0 \) = Baseline capability (1,560 seconds in 2025)
- \( \tau \) = 7 months (doubling period)
- \( t_0 \) = 2025 (baseline year)

**December 2026 Calculation:**
$$
C(2026) = 1560 \times 2^{12/7} = 1560 \times 3.33 = 5,190 \text{ seconds} = 1.4 \text{ hours}
$$

#### Step 2: Task Automation Determination

Each subtask receives binary automation status:

$$
A_{task} = \begin{cases} 
1 & \text{if } C(t) \geq T_{duration} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

Where \( T_{duration} \) is human time required to complete the task.

#### Step 3: Weighted Job-Level Aggregation

Job automation percentage uses relevance × importance weighting:

$$
P_{job} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \times A_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i}
$$

Where:
- \( w_i = \frac{relevance_i}{100} \times \frac{importance_i}{100} \)
- \( relevance_i \) = How core the task is to the job (0-100)
- \( importance_i \) = How critical the task is to job function (0-100)

**Example: Customer Service Representatives**

| Task | Duration | Relevance | Importance | Weight | Automatable (2026)? |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Handle customer inquiries | 0.5 hrs | 95 | 90 | 0.855 | ✓ Yes |
| Process returns | 0.3 hrs | 85 | 85 | 0.723 | ✓ Yes |
| Escalate complex issues | 1.8 hrs | 70 | 95 | 0.665 | No (>1.4 hrs) |
| Update customer records | 0.2 hrs | 80 | 75 | 0.600 | ✓ Yes |

$$
P_{CSR} = \frac{(0.855 \times 1) + (0.723 \times 1) + (0.665 \times 0) + (0.600 \times 1)}{0.855 + 0.723 + 0.665 + 0.600} = \frac{2.178}{2.843} = 76.6\%
$$

Jobs displaced = 2,814,000 × 0.766 = **2,156,000 customer service jobs**

#### Step 4: Economy-Wide Aggregation

Total displacement across 186 job categories:

$$
D_{total} = \sum_{j=1}^{186} N_j \times P_j
$$

Where:
- \( N_j \) = Employment in job category \( j \) (2024 baseline)
- \( P_j \) = Automation percentage for job \( j \)

**2026 Result:**
$$
D_{2026} = 58,261,333 \text{ jobs displaced out of } 93,079,300 \text{ analyzed}
$$

**Automation Rate:**
$$
R_{automation} = \frac{58,261,333}{93,079,300} = 62.6\%
$$

#### Step 5: Full Economy Scaling (Optional)

To extrapolate to full US workforce (155M):

$$
D_{scaled} = D_{covered} \times \frac{W_{total}}{W_{covered}} = 58.3M \times \frac{155M}{93.1M} = 97.3M
$$

**Caveat:** Assumes uncovered occupations (primarily public sector, gig economy, self-employed) have similar automation profiles. Actual displacement may differ.
