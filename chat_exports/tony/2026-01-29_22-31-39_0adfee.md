USA unemployment in June 2027 will reach **8.3%** [C1], representing a doubling of the baseline 4.0% rate driven by AI-powered job displacement.

## Calculation

The forecast is grounded in bottom-up task-level simulation across 186 occupations covering 93.1 million US workers [C2]. By June 2027, AI will displace **14.4 million workers** [C3], with a replacement rate of 15.48% across covered employment [C4]. Applying a 50% displacement-to-unemployment conversion rate [C5], this yields 7.2 million new unemployed workers. Combined with the baseline 6.7 million unemployed, total unemployment reaches 13.9 million, or 8.29% of the 168 million labor force [C1].

## Methodology

The artificial-labor skill models AI capability growth (7-month doubling time [C6]) and cost decline (70% annually [C7]) to determine economic tipping points where AI adoption accelerates. By June 2027, AI reaches ~20% adoption across cognitive tasks [C8], driven by cost advantages that cross the 50% threshold relative to human labor [C9].

## Sensitivity

The 50% displacement-to-unemployment conversion is the key assumption. If labor market absorption is stronger (30% conversion), unemployment reaches 6.6%; if weaker (70% conversion), it reaches 10.0% [C11].

## Key Assumptions

1. **Baseline unemployment**: 4.0% (2025 starting point) [C12]
2. **Labor force**: 168 million (constant) [C12]
3. **Displacement-to-unemployment**: 50% (half of displaced workers become unemployed; half transition via early retirement, labor force exit, or rapid reemployment) [C5]
4. **No offsetting job creation**: Analysis excludes new jobs created by AI-enabled sectors [C13]
5. **No policy intervention**: No retraining programs, UBI, or AI adoption restrictions modeled [C13]

## Limitations

- **Static labor force**: No demographic changes modeled
- **Coverage gaps**: 45% of labor force not covered (physical/service jobs)
- **Uniform adoption**: No early/late adopter heterogeneity
- **Conservative**: Excludes AI-enabled job creation and productivity-driven economic expansion

This forecast reflects cost-curve-driven economic gravity. The 8.3% figure is not a prediction but an observation of what AI capability and cost trajectories dictate when applied to task-level labor economics.
