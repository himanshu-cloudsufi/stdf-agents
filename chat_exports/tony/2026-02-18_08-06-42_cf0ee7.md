2. **Sensors (15–20% of 2026 cost)**: LiDAR, cameras, IMUs, force/torque sensors. Cost falls from $3,000–$6,000 (2026) to $50–$150 (2046). Smartphone and autonomous vehicle sensor supply chains drive 90%+ cost reductions; LiDAR costs already dropped 10x between 2015–2025 [C4].

3. **Compute (10–15% of 2026 cost)**: Edge AI processors for real-time control and vision. Cost falls from $2,000–$4,500 (2026) to $50–$100 (2046). AI inference chip costs decline 30–40% annually due to Moore's Law continuation in specialized silicon and algorithmic efficiency gains [C5].

4. **Assembly & Integration (20–25% of 2026 cost)**: Labor, testing, quality control. Cost falls from $4,000–$7,500 (2026) to $200–$350 (2046) as assembly itself becomes automated (robots building robots) and manufacturing throughput increases 100x [C6].

5. **Structural Components & Power (10–15% of 2026 cost)**: Chassis, batteries, wiring. Cost falls from $2,000–$4,500 (2026) to $100–$200 (2046). Lithium-ion battery costs reach $20–$30/kWh by 2035; structural materials benefit from composite manufacturing scale [C7].
