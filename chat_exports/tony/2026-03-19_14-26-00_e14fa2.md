### The Core Architecture: Capability Factor (CF)

The pre-existing model used a binary tipping-point structure: once AI cost crosses 50% of human labor cost, adoption follows an S-curve to 80%. That structure is accurate for the *ceiling* of potential displacement, but it collapses the deployment gap into the capability curve — conflating what AI *can* do with what organizations *are* doing.

The Capability Factor (CF) separates these two questions:

**Three-Way Decomposition:**
```
Total Possible AI Impact = 100%
├── (1 − CF) = Unutilized Gap: AI capability exists but is not deployed
│              (adoption gap, integration friction, organizational inertia)
├── CF × replacement_share = Replacement: deployed AI driving headcount reduction
└── CF × productivity_share = Productivity: deployed AI driving output expansion
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Constraint: (1−CF) + replacement% + productivity% = 1.0 ✓
```

This decomposition is critical because it separates two structurally different phenomena: the *existence* of AI capability (METR curve) from the *utilization* of that capability (CF). The METR curve tells you what AI can do. CF tells you how much of that is actually deployed in production workflows.
