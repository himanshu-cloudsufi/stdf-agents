**Layer 3 — The critical accounting distinction: Gross vs Net**

| Metric | 2024–2025 Value | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Model net demand** | **9,909.4 kt** | Lead *content* in new batteries + non-battery products manufactured |
| **Internal top-down** | **12,259.0 kt** | Implied total demand across all segments (broader scope) |
| **ILZSG gross refined consumption** | **~13,400 kt** | Total refined lead (primary + secondary) consumed by manufacturers |
| **Primary mine production** | **~4,000–4,500 kt** | New lead extracted from earth |

The 13,400 kt figure does **not** conflate lead with another commodity, and it does **not** double-count recycled flows in the traditional sense. ILZSG refined consumption is a legitimate, internally consistent metric — it counts total refined lead throughput entering manufacturing. The model's 9,909.4 kt is equally consistent — it counts net lead content embedded in finished products. The ~3,500 kt gap represents recycled lead that is re-refined and re-consumed but does not represent *new* lead entering the global stock [C1].

For disruption trajectory analysis (Li-ion displacement, SLI collapse), the model's net framework is the correct instrument. For commodity market positioning (smelter throughput, LME price forecasting, secondary lead economics), the ILZSG gross framework anchored at 13,400 kt is the appropriate baseline.
