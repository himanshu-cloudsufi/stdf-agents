China copper demand will NOT peak within the 2036 forecast horizon. Demand grows 6.3x from 15.3 million tonnes (2024) to 96.5 million tonnes (2036), driven by exponential grid generation copper requirements.

**Demand Trajectory (China)**

| Year | Total (Mt) | Transport | Grid Gen | Grid T&D | Residual |
|------|------------|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| 2024 | 15.3       | 1.7 (11%) | 1.9 (12%) | 2.1 (14%) | 9.6 (63%) |
| 2030 | 28.8       | 2.9 (10%) | 11.0 (38%) | 5.2 (18%) | 9.6 (33%) |
| 2036 | 96.5       | 3.6 (4%)  | 75.0 (78%) | 8.4 (9%)  | 9.6 (10%) |

**Growth Dynamics**

2024-2030: 11.1% CAGR (89% total growth)
2030-2036: 22.4% CAGR (236% total growth)
2024-2036: 16.6% CAGR (533% total growth)

The acceleration post-2030 reflects exponential solar PV deployment. Solar copper demand grows 48x from 1.4 Mt (2024) to 66.3 Mt (2036), with consistent 39% year-over-year growth from 2026 onward.

**Sector Contribution to Growth (2024-2036)**

Grid Generation: +73.1 Mt (90% of total growth)
- Solar PV: +64.9 Mt (primary driver)
- Wind: +8.2 Mt

Grid T&D: +6.3 Mt (8%)
Transportation: +1.8 Mt (2%)
Residual: 0 Mt (0%)

**Key Findings**

1. **No Peak Observed**: Demand continues exponential trajectory through 2036. Peak timing depends on solar/wind deployment saturation, which extends beyond this horizon.

2. **Grid Generation Dominance**: Solar PV copper demand alone exceeds total 2024 consumption by 2034. Grid generation (solar + wind + T&D) accounts for 87% of 2036 demand.

3. **Legacy Demand Plateau**: Construction/industrial copper (residual) frozen at 9.6 Mt from 2025 onward (0% CAGR). This reflects China's construction sector maturation and real estate cycle peak [W2][W6]. Historical data shows residual declining from 10.5 Mt (2020) to 9.6 Mt (2024).

4. **Transportation Copper Growth Masked**: Absolute transportation copper grows 2.1x (1.7 Mt → 3.6 Mt), but share collapses from 11% to 4% as grid generation overwhelms the total.

5. **Solar Doubling Period**: Solar copper doubles every ~3 years (2024: 1.4 Mt → 2027: 3.4 Mt → 2030: 9.1 Mt).

**Methodology**

Bottom-up 4-category model:
- **Transportation**: S-curve adoption for BEV/PHEV/HEV with fitted parameters; copper coefficients 83 kg/BEV vs 23 kg/ICE for passenger cars
- **Grid Generation**: Capacity additions × copper intensity (5.0 t/MW solar, 6.0 t/MW onshore wind, 10.0 t/MW offshore wind)
- **Grid T&D**: Proportional to total capacity growth (sensitivity parameter p=0.5)
- **Residual**: Total minus above three categories; frozen at 2024 level for forecast period

**Peak Timing (Beyond 2036)**

China copper demand will peak when:
1. Solar/wind capacity additions saturate (100% SWB grid achieved)
2. EV fleet replacement cycle stabilizes (post-2040)
3. Grid T&D buildout completes

Current trajectory suggests peak no earlier than 2040-2045, contingent on SWB deployment ceiling and grid infrastructure completion.

**Implications**

China's copper demand growth is decoupled from traditional construction/industrial drivers. The electrification transformation (transport + grid) creates structural demand growth that overwhelms legacy sector decline. The 2024-2036 period represents the steepest phase of the copper demand S-curve, driven by the solar PV exponential.
