Lead demand will decline to 90% of its 2025 baseline value in **2029**.

## Methodology

This analysis uses the lithium-ion-demand skill's bottom-up lead demand forecasting model, which tracks lead-acid battery (LAB) and valve-regulated lead-acid (VRLA) demand across ten use cases: SLI batteries for ICE passenger cars and commercial vehicles, 12V auxiliary batteries in EVs (LAB share), starter batteries for ICE 2-wheelers and 3-wheelers, traction batteries for electric 2-wheelers/3-wheelers/forklifts (LAB share), and stationary UPS batteries for datacenters and telecom infrastructure (VRLA). The model calculates lead demand in kilotonnes (kt) by multiplying vehicle/equipment counts by lead content per unit (kg), accounting for both OEM and replacement demand, and applying S-curve adoption dynamics for lithium-ion displacement.

Lead demand crosses the 90% threshold between 2028 (6,355 kt, still 95.6% of baseline) and 2029 (5,945 kt, 89.5% of baseline). The cumulative decline from 2025 to 2029 is **699 kt (-10.5%)**.

## Disruption Drivers (2025-2029)

The 10% decline is driven by the collapse of ICE vehicle fleets as battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and electric commercial vehicles (EVs) capture market share post-tipping point:

**1. SLI (ICE Passenger Cars): -692 kt (-22.7%)**
- China BEV share rises from 47.5% (2025) to 81.1% (2029), eliminating SLI demand as ICE sales collapse.
- USA and Europe BEV shares rise from 37.2% to 75.0% over the same period.
- Each ICE vehicle requires 11.0 kg of lead for start-stop SLI batteries; BEVs require zero.

**2. SLI (ICE Commercial Vehicles): -153 kt (-8.9%)**
- Commercial vehicle EV shares surge: China 14.3%→54.4%, USA 10.0%→65.3%, Europe 20.9%→63.5%.
- ICE commercial vehicles use 21.1 kg of lead per vehicle; EVs use zero.

**3. Offset: EV Auxiliary (LAB) Growth: +212 kt (+92.4%)**
- EVs use 12V auxiliary batteries, which remain predominantly lead-acid through 2029 (lithium-ion 12V auxiliary cost parity occurs 2029-2031).
- Growing EV fleet partially offsets SLI decline, but auxiliary batteries contain only 10.5 kg lead vs. 11.0 kg for SLI.

**Net Effect:** SLI decline (-845 kt) overwhelms auxiliary growth (+212 kt), producing a 10.5% cumulative drop by 2029.

## Post-2029 Acceleration

The decline accelerates sharply after 2029:
- **2029-2030:** -4.6% YoY
- **2030-2031:** -14.7% YoY (lithium-ion 12V auxiliary batteries reach cost parity and displace LAB)
- **2031-2035:** -15% to -16% YoY (ICE fleet approaches terminal decline)

By 2035, lead demand falls to 2,659 kt (40% of 2025 baseline), driven by near-complete ICE passenger car phase-out (BEV shares >93% globally) and lithium-ion displacement of 12V auxiliary batteries.

## Conclusion

Lead demand reaches 90% of its 2025 baseline in **2029**, driven by the economic collapse of ICE vehicle fleets as BEVs achieve cost parity and capture 50-80% market share across major regions. The 10% threshold is crossed four years after China's BEV tipping point (2022) and three years after Europe's (2024), consistent with S-curve adoption dynamics where the bulk of disruption occurs in the decade following tipping. Post-2029, lead demand enters terminal decline as lithium-ion displaces lead-acid across all remaining use cases (12V auxiliary, forklifts, stationary UPS), falling to 40% of baseline by 2035.
