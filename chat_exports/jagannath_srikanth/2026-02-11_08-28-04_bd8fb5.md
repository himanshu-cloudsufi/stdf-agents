## INTERPRETATION: LABOR CAPACITY vs GDP PRODUCTIVITY

The skill outputs represent **labor capacity changes**, not GDP productivity.

**FTE Displaced (Replacement):** Headcount reduction due to AI automation. 31.7M FTE displaced by 2031 means 31.7M fewer workers are needed. This is a labor supply reduction, not a productivity gain.

**FTE Productivity Gain (Augmentation):** Augmentation of remaining workers. 31.7M FTE productivity gain means remaining workers produce 31.7M FTE-equivalent more output. This IS a productivity gain, but measured in FTE-equivalent capacity, not GDP.

**Combined Impact:** Total capacity added is 63.4M FTE-equivalent (31.7M displaced + 31.7M productivity). The economy gains 68% additional labor capacity (63.4M / 93.1M baseline). This is a **labor capacity expansion**, not a GDP productivity metric.

**Why This Is Not GDP Productivity:** GDP productivity = GDP / labor input (e.g., GDP per worker, GDP per hour). Skill outputs are in FTE units, not dollar units. Converting FTE to GDP requires baseline GDP per worker (external assumption), sector-specific output per worker (external data), and capital stock/TFP adjustments (external model). None of these conversions are in the skill.
