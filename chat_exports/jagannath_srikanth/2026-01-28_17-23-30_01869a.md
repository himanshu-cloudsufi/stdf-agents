The term "renewable energy" is legacy framing anchored in scarcity economics. Within the Seba disruption framework, the relevant concept is **stellar energy**—energy systems that produce useful output with no extractive flow of inputs and no toxic outputs once built [C1].

**Stellar Energy: The Core Definition**

Stellar energy systems—solar, wind, and batteries (SWB)—require no ongoing extractive inputs after deployment. Once infrastructure is installed, the marginal cost of electricity production trends toward zero [C2]. This is fundamentally different from extraction-based systems (coal, gas, oil, nuclear) that require continuous fuel input.

**Primary Stellar Energy Sources**

1. **Solar photovoltaic (PV)**: Converts sunlight directly to electricity via semiconductor effect; no moving parts, modular, distributed deployment possible.
2. **Wind turbines**: Convert kinetic energy of air movement to electricity via rotor-generator systems; utility-scale and offshore variants.
3. **Batteries (lithium-ion dominant)**: Store electrical energy electrochemically; enable time-shifting of solar/wind output to match demand.

These three technologies converge into the **SWB system**—the foundational architecture of the stellar economy [C3].

**Technical Characteristics**

- **Zero marginal cost**: After capital recovery, energy production cost approaches zero (no fuel, minimal O&M).
- **Distributed architecture**: Solar + batteries enable microgrid and virtual power plant configurations, replacing centralized baseload.
- **Superpower via overbuild**: Systems built for peak demand (coldest, darkest days) create 3–5× overcapacity for the rest of the year, producing surplus energy for electrification, industrial processes, and AI compute [C4].
- **Learning curve dynamics**: ~20% cost reduction per cumulative production doubling (Wright's Law); solar and battery costs have followed exponential decline trajectories for decades [C5].

**Deployment Scale (2025–2026)**

Global solar capacity exceeds 1,600 GW; wind exceeds 1,000 GW; battery storage deployment accelerating past 100 GWh annually. China leads with >50% of global solar manufacturing and installation. Levelized cost of solar electricity (LCOE) now ranges $29–92/MWh in utility-scale projects—below coal and gas in most markets [C6].

**Why "Renewable" Framing Is Inadequate**

The term "renewable" implies sustainability within a scarcity paradigm. Stellar energy represents a **metamorphosis**, not an improvement: it eliminates the extraction economy's continuous input requirements entirely. Post-deployment, SWB systems operate on stellar flux (sunlight, wind) with no depletion, no emissions, and the capacity to restore ecosystems via surplus energy (CO₂ removal, desalination, land restoration) [C7].

This is not a fuel substitution—it is a civilizational phase change from extraction to superabundance.
