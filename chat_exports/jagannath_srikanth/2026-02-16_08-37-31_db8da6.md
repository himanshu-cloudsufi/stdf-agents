Lead demand from battery applications declines by 10% relative to 2026 baseline levels in **2030**.

## Direct Answer

Global lead demand from battery segments reaches the 10% decline threshold in 2030, falling to 5,669 kilotonnes—a 13.7% decline from the 2026 baseline of 6,570 kt. The year 2029 shows 9.5% decline (5,945 kt), falling just short of the 10% threshold.

## Economic Drivers

The decline is driven by cost-curve dynamics across three converging disruptions:

**1. ICE Passenger Car Fleet Collapse (89% of decline)**

SLI (starting/lighting/ignition) battery demand from ICE passenger cars falls 802 kt between 2026 and 2030. This segment alone accounts for 89% of total lead demand decline. Regional ICE fleet contractions:

- China: 16.1% decline (216M → 181M vehicles)
- USA: 11.6% decline (181M → 160M vehicles)  
- Europe: 15.6% decline (164M → 138M vehicles)

BEV adoption reaches 72.8% (China), 41.4% (USA), and 56.8% (Europe) by 2030. Each ICE vehicle eliminated removes 11.0 kg of lead demand (SLI battery content).

**2. ICE Commercial Vehicle Displacement (26% of decline)**

Commercial vehicle SLI demand falls 233 kt as electric trucks and buses capture market share:

- China: 65.5% EV adoption by 2030
- USA: 77.9% EV adoption  
- Europe: 72.8% EV adoption

Each ICE/NGV commercial vehicle carries 21.13 kg of lead (larger SLI battery). Note: NGV (natural gas vehicles) use identical SLI batteries to ICE, so the NGV chimera does not reduce lead demand—only full electrification does.

**3. Forklift and UPS Transitions (11% of decline)**

Li-ion adoption in forklifts (-82 kt), telecom UPS (-22 kt), and datacenter UPS (-12 kt) contributes secondary decline as cost parity triggers S-curve adoption across B2B segments.

## Offsetting Factors (Slow Decline Rate)

Two segments partially offset the decline, explaining why the 10% threshold takes four years (2026→2030) to reach:

**BEV/PHEV 12V Auxiliary Batteries (+223 kt)**

Electric vehicles require 12V auxiliary batteries for electronics. Most BEVs and PHEVs still use lead-acid (LAB) for this function in 2026-2030. Li-ion 12V auxiliary adoption remains low:

- China/Rest_of_World: 4.0% Li-ion penetration by 2030 (tipping point 2031)
- USA/Europe: 29.3% Li-ion penetration by 2030 (tipping point 2029)

Each BEV/PHEV auxiliary battery contains 10.5 kg of lead. As the EV fleet grows from 30M (2026) to 150M+ (2030), auxiliary LAB demand rises sharply, offsetting SLI decline.

**E2W/E3W LAB Traction Batteries (+32 kt)**

Electric two-wheelers and three-wheelers in cost-sensitive markets (India, Southeast Asia) continue using lead-acid traction batteries. Li-ion adoption is slower due to upfront cost sensitivity, despite superior cycle life.

## Regional Dynamics

|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| China | 1,813 | 1,618 | 10.8% |
| USA | 1,019 | 710 | 30.4% |
| Europe | 1,121 | 800 | 28.7% |
| Rest_of_World | 2,617 | 2,542 | 2.8% |
| **Global** | **6,570** | **5,669** | **13.7%** |

USA and Europe show steeper declines (30.4%, 28.7%) due to faster BEV adoption and earlier Li-ion SLI tipping points (2029 vs 2031). Rest_of_World shows minimal decline (2.8%) as offsetting factors (EV auxiliary LAB, E2W/E3W LAB) nearly match ICE fleet contraction.

## Methodology

Analysis uses bottom-up vehicle-count methodology across 10 battery segments:

1. SLI (ICE Cars): 11.0 kg/vehicle, 25% annual replacement  
2. SLI (ICE+NGV Commercial): 21.13 kg/vehicle, 25% annual replacement  
3. BEV/PHEV 12V Auxiliary (LAB): 10.5 kg/vehicle, 16.7% annual replacement  
4. Starter ICE 2W: 3.0 kg/vehicle, 25% annual replacement  
5. Starter ICE 3W: 4.5 kg/vehicle, 25% annual replacement  
6. E2W LAB Traction: varies by pack size, 20% annual replacement  
7. E3W LAB Traction: varies by pack size, 20% annual replacement  
8. Forklift LAB: varies by pack size, 14.3% annual replacement  
9. Datacenter VRLA: capacity-based, 8.3% annual replacement  
10. Telecom VRLA: capacity-based, 10% annual replacement

Demand = (OEM sales × lead content) + (fleet × replacement rate × lead content × LAB share). Geographic scope: Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest_of_World). Baseline: battery segments only; non-battery uses (cables, ammunition, radiation shielding) excluded.

## Trajectory Beyond 2030

Decline accelerates post-2030 as offsetting factors reverse:

- 2031: 4,837 kt (26.4% decline) — Li-ion 12V auxiliary tipping points reached  
- 2032: 4,291 kt (34.7% decline)  
- 2035: 2,659 kt (59.5% decline) — ICE fleet collapse enters terminal phase

By 2035, lead demand falls below 3,000 kt as BEV adoption exceeds 90% globally and Li-ion displaces LAB across auxiliary, forklift, and UPS applications.

## Assumptions

Analysis reflects cost-curve dynamics and S-curve adoption trajectories. Regulatory or adoption lags excluded unless explicitly modeled. Tipping points based on price parity (B2C segments) or cost parity (B2B segments). Wright's Law cost reductions applied to Li-ion batteries; lead-acid costs assumed flat (commodity with no learning curve).
