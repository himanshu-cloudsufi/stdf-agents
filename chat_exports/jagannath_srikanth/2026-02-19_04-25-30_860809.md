Lithium demand does NOT peak within the 2024-2040 forecast horizon. Demand continues exponential growth through 2040, driven by ongoing EV adoption across multiple regions and vehicle segments. Peak demand occurs AFTER 2040, likely in the 2045-2050 timeframe when global EV adoption saturates and replacement cycles stabilize.

**Key Findings:**

**1. Exponential Growth Trajectory**
- 2024: 1,442 GWh battery demand → 1,153 kt lithium
- 2030: 10,517 GWh → 8,414 kt lithium (7.3× increase)
- 2035: 63,385 GWh → 50,708 kt lithium (6.0× increase)
- 2040: 394,818 GWh → 315,854 kt lithium (6.2× increase)

**2. Accelerating Growth Rates**
- 2024-2030 CAGR: 39.3%
- 2030-2035 CAGR: 43.2%
- 2035-2040 CAGR: 44.2% ← Growth ACCELERATES, not decelerates

The acceleration from 43.2% to 44.2% CAGR demonstrates that demand has not entered saturation dynamics. This is exponential trajectory, not S-curve deceleration.

**3. Demand Drivers by Application (2040)**
- Passenger cars: 67% (263,111 GWh)
- Commercial vehicles: 18% (71,287 GWh)
- Grid BESS: 14% (53,606 GWh)
- Two/Three-wheelers: 2% (6,795 GWh)
- Other (forklifts, datacenter/telecom UPS): <1%

**4. Why No Peak?**

**Regional EV Adoption Asynchrony:** China reaches 95% EV share by ~2035, but USA, Europe, and Rest of World lag by 5-10 years. The global S-curve is a composite of staggered regional curves, extending the growth phase.

**Replacement Demand Ramp:** Battery replacement cycles (12-15 years for passenger cars, 8 years for commercial vehicles) create a second wave of demand post-2035 as the installed base from 2020s adoption reaches end-of-life.

**Grid BESS Acceleration:** As SWB (solar-wind-battery) systems scale to displace fossil generation, grid storage deployment accelerates exponentially. BESS demand grows from 200 GWh (2024) to 53,606 GWh (2040), a 268× increase.

**Commercial Vehicle Electrification:** Light, medium, and heavy commercial vehicles enter rapid adoption phase post-2030 as cost parity is achieved. This segment grows from 164 GWh (2024) to 71,287 GWh (2040), a 435× increase.

**5. Methodology**

Bottom-up model covering 8 use cases across 4 regions (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World):
- Tipping point detection: price parity (B2C) or cost parity (B2B)
- S-curve adoption modeling post-tipping
- Stock-flow accounting: OEM + replacement demand
- Lithium content conversion: 0.8 tonnes Li per GWh (NMC/NCA average; LFP is ~0.4 t/GWh)

Geographic scope: Global (China, USA, Europe, Rest of World aggregated).

**Conclusion:**

Lithium demand exhibits no peak signal through 2040. The 44.2% CAGR in the 2035-2040 period—faster than the prior 2030-2035 period—confirms ongoing exponential growth. Peak demand will occur post-2040 when: (1) global EV adoption saturates at 95%+ across all regions, (2) replacement demand stabilizes as the installed base matures, and (3) grid BESS deployment completes the SWB transformation. Based on regional adoption trajectories, peak lithium demand is projected in the 2045-2050 timeframe.
