## Methodology

Bottom-up vehicle-count based lead demand model using S-curve adoption dynamics for battery chemistry transitions (lead-acid to lithium-ion) across automotive SLI (starting-lighting-ignition), commercial vehicle, forklift, and stationary UPS applications. Geographic scope: Global (China + USA + Europe + Rest of World). Non-battery uses (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding) grow at 2.25% annually but cannot offset battery-related collapse.

## Primary Drivers (2026-2031)

1. **ICE Passenger Car Fleet Decline**: China's ICE fleet drops from 216 million (2026) to 161 million (2031), driven by BEV sales share rising from 44% to 82%. SLI cars lead demand collapses from 2,938 kt to 1,666 kt—a 1,272 kt decline accounting for 73.4% of total battery lead reduction.

2. **ICE Commercial Vehicle Fleet Decline**: ICE and NGV (natural gas vehicle) commercial fleets shrink as electric LCV/MCV/HCV adoption accelerates post-tipping (2024-2027). SLI commercial lead demand falls from 1,719 kt to 1,187 kt—a 532 kt decline (30.7% of total).

3. **Li-ion SLI Battery Penetration**: Tipping points occur 2029-2031 when lithium-ion SLI battery cost falls below lead-acid SLI cost. By 2031, Li-ion SLI penetration reaches 21-39% (region-dependent), accelerating lead-acid displacement even within the shrinking ICE fleet. USA and Europe reach 39% penetration by 2031; China and Rest of World reach 21%.
