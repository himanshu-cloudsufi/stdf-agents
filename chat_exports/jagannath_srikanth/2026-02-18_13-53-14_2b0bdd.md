## Quantitative Forecast

**2026 Baseline:** 6,570 kt  
**90% Threshold:** 5,913 kt

The interpolated crossing point occurs in February 2029, but 2029 annual demand (5,945 kt) remains marginally above the threshold at 90.5% of 2026 levels. 2030 is the first year where demand definitively falls below 90% of the 2026 baseline.

## Key Displacement Drivers (2026→2030)

The 901 kt reduction (13.7% decline) from 2026 to 2030 is driven by three primary mechanisms:

### 1. SLI Battery Displacement in ICE Passenger Vehicles (−801 kt, 91% of total decline)

**Mechanism:** Battery electric vehicle (BEV) adoption eliminates starter-lighting-ignition (SLI) battery demand. BEVs use lithium-ion 12V auxiliary batteries, not lead-acid SLI batteries.

## Methodology

This forecast uses the **lithium-ion-demand skill**, a canonical bottom-up battery demand model with the following components:

1. **S-curve adoption modeling:** Logistic curves fitted to historical BEV/PHEV sales data, triggered by cost-parity tipping points (B2C: price parity; B2B: cost parity).

2. **Stock-flow vehicle fleet dynamics:** Annual sales, scrappage rates, and installed base calculations for passenger cars, commercial vehicles, two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and forklifts.

3. **Lead-acid displacement logic:**
   - **SLI batteries (ICE vehicles):** 11.0 kg lead per passenger car, 21.1 kg per commercial vehicle. Demand = (ICE fleet + NGV fleet) × lead content × replacement rate (25%/year).
   - **VRLA batteries (UPS):** Lithium-ion adoption follows cost-parity S-curves in datacenter and telecom applications.
   - **LAB batteries (E2W/E3W/forklifts):** Two-stage disruption model (ICE→EV, then LAB→Li-ion within EVs).

4. **Regional coverage:** China, USA, Europe, Rest of World (global aggregate).

5. **Time horizon:** 2005–2040 (historical calibration + forecast).

The model excludes non-battery lead applications (cables, construction, ammunition), which account for ~26% of 2026 demand and grow modestly over the forecast period.

## Key Insights

- **Tipping point dynamics:** The 2029–2030 inflection reflects the compounding effect of multiple S-curves crossing their steepest growth phases simultaneously (BEV passenger cars in USA/Europe, commercial vehicle electrification globally, VRLA displacement in datacenters).

- **Incumbent collapse pattern:** Lead demand follows the X-curve decline characteristic of disrupted industries. The 2031–2035 period shows accelerating decline rates (−14% to −16% YoY) as BEV adoption enters the vertical growth phase of the S-curve.

- **Geographic heterogeneity:** China's earlier BEV tipping point (2022) means its lead demand decline began earlier but proceeds more gradually. USA and Europe experience sharper declines post-2026 as their BEV S-curves steepen.

- **Post-2035 trajectory:** By 2040, lead demand reaches 20% of 2026 levels (1,317 kt), driven almost entirely by the residual ICE fleet in Rest of World markets and non-battery applications. SLI battery demand for passenger cars falls to 60 kt globally by 2040 (98% decline from 2026).

Analysis reflects cost-curve dynamics and S-curve adoption trajectories. Regulatory or adoption lags are excluded unless explicitly modeled in the underlying tipping-point detection.
