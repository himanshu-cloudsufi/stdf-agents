Global lead demand for battery applications peaked in **2017 at 7,154 kt** [C1]. By 2030, battery-only lead demand will collapse to **4,428 kt**—a 38% decline from the peak [C2].

## Peak Year: 2017

Battery-only lead demand reached its maximum in 2017, driven by the global ICE vehicle fleet reaching saturation before EV adoption accelerated [C1]. This peak occurred seven years before total lead demand (which includes non-battery uses like construction and ammunition) peaked in 2024 at 12,259 kt [C3].

## 2030 Forecast: 4,428 kt

By 2030, battery lead demand falls to 4,428 kt—a 32% decline from 2024 levels [C2]. The collapse is driven by three converging disruptions:

**1. ICE Fleet Collapse (48% of 2030 demand)**
- SLI batteries for passenger ICE vehicles: 2,136 kt in 2030 [C4]
- BEV adoption S-curves drive ICE sales below replacement rates across all major regions
- China BEV share reaches 94% by 2040; USA 90%; Europe 92% [C5]

**2. Commercial Vehicle Electrification (34% of 2030 demand)**
- SLI batteries for commercial ICE/NGV vehicles: 1,486 kt in 2030 [C6]
- Cost parity reached 2024-2027 across regions; S-curve adoption follows [C7]
- NGV (natural gas vehicles) modeled as chimera technology—rises then decays with 6-year half-life post-EV tipping [C8]

**3. Forklift Li-ion Adoption (14% of 2030 demand)**
- Forklift LAB demand: 621 kt in 2030 [C9]
- Li-ion cost parity already achieved; S-curve adoption underway [C10]
- Li-ion share reaches 93-97% by 2040 across regions [C11]

## Methodology

Bottom-up stock-flow model across 10 battery use cases: SLI (cars, commercial), 12V auxiliary (BEV/PHEV), starter batteries (2W/3W), traction batteries (E2W/E3W LAB), forklifts, and UPS (datacenter/telecom VRLA) [C12]. Each segment models:

1. **Tipping point detection**: Price parity (B2C) or cost parity (B2B) between Li-ion and lead-acid [C13]
2. **S-curve adoption**: Logistic fit to historical data; fallback heuristic 10%→80% over 10 years post-tipping [C14]
3. **Fleet stock-flow**: OEM + replacement demand using vehicle/battery lifetimes [C15]

The 2017 peak reflects maximum ICE fleet size before EV disruption began accelerating. Post-2017, lead demand enters permanent decline as:
- ICE vehicle sales fall below scrappage rates (negative net fleet growth)
- Li-ion displaces lead-acid in auxiliary, starter, and industrial applications
- Commercial vehicle electrification accelerates post-2025 cost parity
