## Segment Breakdown: Drivers of Decline

**SLI Passenger Cars** (60% of 2026 baseline): The largest contributor to absolute decline. BEV penetration accelerates from 18% in 2026 to 35% in 2031 and 58% in 2036, eliminating the need for 12V lead-acid starter batteries. SLI car demand falls from 7,845 kt (2026) to 6,532 kt (2031) and 4,098 kt (2036)—a 48% collapse over the decade.

**SLI Commercial Vehicles** (20% of 2026 baseline): Commercial vehicle electrification lags passenger cars but follows the same trajectory. Demand drops from 2,610 kt (2026) to 2,313 kt (2031) and 1,748 kt (2036) as electric trucks and vans displace diesel fleets.

**VRLA Datacenter/Telecom UPS** (13% of 2026 baseline): Lithium-ion batteries offer higher energy density, longer cycle life, and lower total cost of ownership for stationary storage. VRLA demand declines from 1,740 kt (2026) to 1,488 kt (2031) and 1,074 kt (2036)—a 38% reduction.

**LAB Two-Wheelers/Three-Wheelers/Forklifts** (6% of 2026 baseline): Electric two-wheelers and three-wheelers in Asia eliminate lead-acid traction batteries. Forklift electrification accelerates in warehouses. Demand rises slightly through 2031 due to fleet growth in non-electrified segments, then declines as electrification penetrates deeper.

## Methodology

This analysis executes the **lithium-ion-demand skill** bottom-up forecasting methodology. Lead demand is calculated as the inverse of lithium-ion adoption across six application segments:

1. **SLI passenger cars**: Fleet stock-flow model with BEV/PHEV/ICE shares, scrappage rates, and 12V lead-acid content per ICE vehicle.
2. **SLI commercial vehicles**: Parallel stock-flow model for LCV/MCV/HCV with electrification curves.
3. **VRLA datacenter/telecom UPS**: Stationary storage displacement by lithium-ion based on cost-parity tipping points and replacement cycles.
4. **LAB two-wheelers/three-wheelers/forklifts**: Motive power applications with regional electrification rates (China, India, Southeast Asia leading).
5. **Replacement demand**: Calculated from installed base and battery replacement cycles (3-5 years for SLI, 5-8 years for industrial).
6. **Non-battery uses**: Held constant (construction, ammunition, radiation shielding).

The model uses S-curve adoption dynamics with cost-curve-driven tipping points. BEV cost parity was reached in China (2023) and will be reached globally by 2027-2028. Lithium-ion UPS cost parity occurred in 2024. These economic triggers drive exponential displacement of lead-acid across all segments.

## Geographic Scope

Global aggregate. The decline is led by China (50% BEV share by 2030) and Europe (55% BEV share by 2032), with the United States and Rest of World following 2-3 years behind.

## Implications

The 10% decline threshold in 2031 marks the beginning of the lead industry's death spiral. Lead-acid battery manufacturers face declining volumes, stranded smelting capacity, and margin compression. By 2036, global lead demand will have fallen 35% from 2026 levels—a collapse driven purely by the economics of lithium-ion displacement, not by regulation or environmental policy. The lead mining and recycling industries will contract accordingly, with high-cost producers exiting first.
