## Methodology

The artificial-labor skill uses:
- **Capability gating:** AI must be capable of task duration (7-month doubling time from METR data) [C1]
- **Cost-parity tipping:** Adoption accelerates when AI cost < 50% of human cost [C1]
- **S-curve adoption:** Post-tipping adoption follows 5% → 10% → 20% → 40% → 80% schedule [C1]
- **Replacement ceiling:** 80% maximum replacement per occupation (human-in-the-loop minimum) [C1]
- **Cognitive-only scope:** Only cognitive subtasks are automatable; physical tasks excluded [C1]

**Critical limitation:** This analysis models displacement within 341 existing occupations. It does not model:
- Job creation in new sectors
- Labor force participation changes
- Wage dynamics or retraining
- Policy interventions

The 32.6% replacement rate reflects the **economic gravity** of AI cost-curve dynamics applied to cognitive labor. Unemployment rate is a separate question requiring labor market reabsorption modeling.
