## Critical Caveats

**1. Coverage**: This analysis covers 93.1M US jobs across 186 occupations (~60% of US workforce).

**2. Adoption Lag**: This model assumes instant adoption when AI capability is reached. In practice, corporate adoption typically lags 12-24 months behind technical capability. Real-world displacement will likely peak in **2028-2029** rather than 2027.

**3. Job Creation**: This model forecasts displacement only. It does not account for new jobs created by AI (AI trainers, prompt engineers, AI system architects, human-AI collaboration roles).

**4. Binary Simplification**: Tasks are modeled as fully automatable or not. In practice, partial automation and human-AI collaboration are common, especially in 2025-2027 transition period.

**5. Capability ≠ Cost Parity**: This model uses technical capability thresholds (80% reliability), not economic cost parity. Even when AI can perform a task, human labor may remain cheaper in some contexts until 2026-2028.
