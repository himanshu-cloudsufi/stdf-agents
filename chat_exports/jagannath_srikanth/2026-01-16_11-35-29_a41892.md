## Key Uncertainties & Caveats

### Model Assumptions

1. **7-month AI doubling time** (HIGH confidence - empirical data)
2. **80% reliability threshold** (Standard for autonomous work)
3. **Instant adoption** (Reality: 12-24 month corporate lag)
4. **Binary automation** (Reality: Partial automation likely)
5. **No job creation** (Reality: 50,000-100,000 new AI-related roles)

### Adjusted Central Estimate

**Technical capability:** 93.3% automation by 2030
**Market reality (with adoption lag):** 70-85% displacement by 2030
**Net displacement (after job creation):** **1.6M - 1.7M jobs lost** (85-90%)

### What Could Change This?

**Slower disruption:**
- AI plateau (doubling time slows to 10-12 months)
- Regulatory barriers (licensing, liability laws)
- Quality issues (AI errors create liability)

**Faster disruption:**
- AI breakthrough (GPT-5 with coding specialization)
- Rapid corporate adoption (cost pressure)
- Startup disruption (AI-native competitors)
