## Oil Displacement Methodology

### Assumptions

**Passenger Vehicle Oil Consumption:**
- Average ICE vehicle: 500 gallons/year
- Conversion: 500 gallons ÷ 42 gallons/barrel = 11.9 barrels/year per vehicle
- Daily consumption: 11.9 barrels/year ÷ 365 days = **0.0326 barrels/day per vehicle**
- Rounded: **42 barrels/year = 0.115 bpd per vehicle**

**PHEV Factor:**
- PHEVs operate ~50% on electricity, 50% on gasoline
- Oil displacement: 0.115 bpd × 0.5 = **0.0575 bpd per PHEV**

**BEV Factor:**
- BEVs use zero oil for propulsion
- Oil displacement: **0.115 bpd per BEV** (full ICE replacement)

### Calculation Formula

$$\text{Oil Displaced (bpd)} = \left(\text{Cumulative BEV Fleet} \times 0.115\right) + \left(\text{Cumulative PHEV Fleet} \times 0.0575\right)$$

- 2010: $1,100/kWh

- 2023: $100/kWh (crossing critical threshold)
- 2035 forecast: $50/kWh (Wright's Law trajectory)

**Learning Rate:** ~18% cost reduction per doubling of cumulative production

This exponential cost decline is the **primary driver** of EV cost parity. Once battery packs fall below $100/kWh, EVs achieve total cost of ownership (TCO) parity with ICE vehicles without subsidies.

### Vehicle Platform Integration

**Cost Structure Transformation:**
- **ICE powertrain:** 2,000+ moving parts, complex supply chain
- **EV powertrain:** ~20 moving parts, simplified manufacturing

**Cost Convergence Timeline:**
- 2022: EV upfront cost parity in China
- 2023: EV upfront cost parity in USA/Europe
- 2025: EVs become **structurally cheaper** than ICE in all markets
- 2030: EVs cost 30-40% less than ICE equivalents

### Manufacturing Scale and Learning Curves

**Global EV Production Capacity:**
- 2020: ~5M units/year
- 2024: ~20M units/year (4× scale-up)
- 2030 forecast: ~65M units/year
- 2035 forecast: ~85M units/year

Each doubling of production volume drives:
- 15-20% cost reduction (Wright's Law)
- Supply chain maturation
- Manufacturing process optimization

### Disruption Synergies

**Key Convergence Points:**
1. **Battery costs** (-18% learning rate) × **Manufacturing scale** (4× in 4 years) → Exponential TCO advantage
2. **Charging infrastructure** deployment × **Grid modernization** → Range anxiety elimination
3. **Renewable energy** cost parity × **EV charging** demand → Clean energy ecosystem
4. **Autonomous driving** technology × **EV platforms** → Future TaaS (Transport-as-a-Service) disruption

These technologies don't evolve independently—they create **non-linear synergies** that accelerate adoption beyond linear forecasts.
