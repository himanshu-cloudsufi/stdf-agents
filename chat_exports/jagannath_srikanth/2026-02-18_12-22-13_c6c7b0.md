## Gap Assessment

To calculate unemployment rate, three additional inputs are required:

1. **Displacement-to-unemployment conversion factor**: What percentage of displaced workers become unemployed versus reabsorbed into new sectors? Historical automation waves show 40-60% reabsorption over 5-10 years, but AI's speed and scope may reduce this.

2. **New job creation offset**: AI-enabled sectors (robotics manufacturing, AI training/oversight, precision services) will create jobs, but at what rate? Historical precedent: automation creates 1 new job for every 3-5 displaced.

3. **Labor force dynamics**: Will displaced workers exit the labor force (early retirement, discouraged workers), reducing the denominator?

## Methodology & Limitations

**Skill Methodology:**
- Bottom-up simulation of 186 cognitive occupations, 48,404 subtasks [C2]
- Capability gating: AI must handle task duration before automation [C2]
- Cost-parity tipping: Adoption accelerates when AI cost < 50% human cost [C2]
- S-curve adoption post-tipping: 5% → 10% → 20% → 40% → 80% over 5 years [C2]
- 50/50 split: Hours freed allocated equally to replacement and productivity [C2]
- 80% ceiling: Maximum 80% replacement per occupation (human-in-the-loop minimum) [C2]

**What Is NOT Modeled:**
- Physical labor occupations (40% of workforce)
- New job creation in AI-enabled sectors
- Labor force participation rate changes
- Policy interventions (UBI, job guarantees, retraining programs)
- Behavioral responses (early retirement, gig economy shifts)

**Skill Coverage:** 93.1M cognitive workers (~60% of 155M total US employment). Remaining 40% (construction, food service, manual labor, gig economy) not modeled.

## Economic Dynamics

The 34% replacement rate by December 2027 reflects **post-tipping acceleration** [C2]. AI capability reaches 4.75 hours by January 2027 [C2], unlocking automation of complex cognitive tasks (legal research, medical diagnosis, financial analysis). AI cost falls to $0.45 per million tokens by 2027 [C2], making automation economically irresistible for employers facing 10x-100x cost advantages.

**Market trauma occurs at 5-10% displacement** [C4]. By December 2027, cognitive sectors face 34% displacement—well past the rupture point where incumbent labor economics collapse. Fixed costs (benefits, training, management overhead) become unsustainable, accelerating adoption.
