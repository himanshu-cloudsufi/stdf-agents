## Mechanism 3: Competitive Displacement Pathway

### Pathway Mechanics

AI-adopting firms gain a 15–25% structural cost advantage → non-adopting rivals lose market share → revenue decline → hiring freeze → natural attrition → eventual closure. This pathway is distinct from direct automation: it affects workers at **non-adopting firms** through business failure and headcount attrition rather than direct layoffs [C1].

**UK SME context:** 5.5M UK SMEs employ ~16.7M workers (49% of UK private sector employment). SME AI adoption lags large firms by 18–36 months — the competitive displacement window opens precisely as large-firm direct displacement peaks [C1].

### Competitive Displacement FTE Estimation

### Does Competitive Displacement Accelerate or Decelerate Total Cumulative Displacement?

**Near-term (2026–2028): DECELERATES visible unemployment.** Only 5–8% of total displaced FTE accrues via the competitive channel in this window. The pathway is slow to start — 93K FTE in 2026 vs 1,776K direct [C1].

**Medium-term (2029–2032): EXTENDS the displacement tail.** Competitive FTE peaks at 526K/year in 2029, adding a second wave after the direct displacement S-curve plateaus. This adds 2–3 years to the disruption window [C1].

**Long-term: SAME CEILING, LONGER TAIL.** The total cumulative FTE displaced is unchanged — the competitive pathway redistributes ~30% of FTE over a longer horizon, not adds to it. However, competitive pressure ultimately **forces adoption or exit** across the entire non-adopting segment: non-adopters either adopt AI (collapsing back into the direct displacement channel) or fail (completing the competitive displacement). The pathway delays the initial signal but accelerates the terminal phase [C1].
