**Where Nuclear Loses:**
- **Merchant power markets:** Can't compete with $30/MWh solar+battery on economics
- **Rapid deployment:** SWB + gas built 5-10× faster
- **Scalability:** Nuclear adds 5-10 GW/year (optimistic), SWB adds 60-80 GW/year

### **Bottom Line:**

**Nuclear's role:** Specialized, niche provider of **premium baseload power** for specific applications willing to pay $80-100/MWh for carbon-free, always-on electricity. Total capacity grows modestly (95 GW → 120-130 GW by 2040), maintaining 12-15% generation share.

**Not the hero of the energy transformation.** That's SWB + gas backup.

**Investment Implication:**
- **Long:** Established nuclear operators with restart potential (Constellation Energy), SMR technology leaders with federal contracts (GE Vernova, Westinghouse/Brookfield)
- **Avoid:** Pure-play SMR startups with no federal backing (high execution risk, Vogtle-style cost overruns likely)
- **Nuclear uranium miners** (Cameco) benefit from fleet life extensions + modest new build activity

Nuclear gets a **supporting role**, not the starring role policy assumes.
