## Peaking Price Impact Analysis: 100 GW (2027) and 300 GW (2029)

### Current Baseline (2024)
- **26 GW installed**
- Texas August 2024 peak prices: **$160/MWh lower** than August 2023
- California/Texas evening peak prices: **$80-150/MWh** (down from $200-400/MWh pre-battery era)

---

### 100 GW Impact (Mid-2027)

**Capacity Context:**
- 100 GW = ~22% of US peak demand (~450 GW)
- At 4-hour average duration = 400 GWh storage capacity
- 4× current deployment

**Peaking Price Consequences:**

**Mechanism 1: Arbitrage Compression**
- Batteries charge during low-price solar hours, discharge during evening peaks
- Every GW of batteries deployed reduces peak-to-trough price spread
- At 26 GW: Spreads compressed ~60% vs. pre-battery baseline
- At 100 GW: **Spreads compress 80-85%**

**Mechanism 2: Ancillary Services Saturation**
- California regulation market saturated at ~10 GW battery capacity (market only needed several hundred MW)
- At 100 GW nationally: **All ancillary service markets fully saturated**
- Batteries forced entirely into energy arbitrage, intensifying peak price suppression

**Mechanism 3: Gas Peaker Displacement**
- Gas peakers require $150-250/MWh to be economic (startup costs, fuel, O&M)
- With 100 GW batteries providing peak capacity: **Gas peakers lose 70-80% of high-price operating hours**
- Remaining peakers only dispatch during multi-hour extreme events or battery state-of-charge exhaustion

**2027 Evening Peak Pricing Prediction:**
- **High solar regions (CA, TX, AZ):** $40-70/MWh (down from current $80-150/MWh)
- **Other regions:** $60-100/MWh (as batteries spread nationally)
- **Extreme weather events:** Still spike to $200-500/MWh, but for 5-10 hours/year instead of 50-100 hours/year

**Price volatility:** Down 75% vs. 2023 baseline

---

### 300 GW Impact (Early 2029)

**Capacity Context:**
- 300 GW = ~67% of US peak demand
- At 4-hour duration = 1,200 GWh storage capacity
- 11.5× current deployment

**Peaking Price Consequences:**

**Mechanism 1: Structural Price Floor Reached**
- Battery cycling cost becomes the marginal price setter
- Cycling cost = battery degradation per kWh cycled
- Current Li-ion degradation cost: **~$10-15/MWh**
- With continued cost declines: **~$8-12/MWh by 2029**

**Mechanism 2: Geographic Price Convergence**
- 300 GW distributed nationally eliminates regional scarcity premiums
- Transmission congestion matters less when every region has local storage
- Evening peak prices converge within **$10-20/MWh range nationally** (excluding extreme events)

**Mechanism 3: Gas Combined-Cycle Displacement Begins**
- Gas peakers: Already uneconomic at 100 GW, **fully stranded by 300 GW**
- Gas combined-cycle plants: Capacity factors drop from 50-60% to **30-40%**
- Only baseload combined-cycle with long-term contracts remain economic

**Mechanism 4: Time-of-Day Price Inversion**
- Traditional pattern: Low prices at night, high prices evening peak
- New pattern: **Negative prices during solar hours, modest positive prices evening**
- Solar hours (10am-3pm): -$5 to $0/MWh (generators pay to export)
- Evening (6pm-9pm): **$20-40/MWh** (battery cycling cost floor)

**2029 Evening Peak Pricing Prediction:**
- **Normal conditions:** $20-40/MWh nationally
- **High demand days (95°F+):** $50-80/MWh (4-6 hour duration)
- **Extreme events (heatwave >100°F, multi-day):** $100-300/MWh (battery SOC depleted, gas must run)

**Key structural shift:** Evening "peak" prices are now **lower than midday prices were in 2020** ($35-50/MWh typical).

---

### Strategic Implications

**For Gas Peaker Owners:**
- **2027 (100 GW):** Operating hours cut by 75%, revenues collapse
- **2029 (300 GW):** Plants sit idle 95% of year, **economically stranded**

**For Battery Developers:**
- **2027 (100 GW):** Still capturing $40-70/MWh evening premiums, strong returns
- **2029 (300 GW):** Returns compress to **commodity levels** ($20-40/MWh), only cost leaders profitable

**For Industrial Electricity Users:**
- **2027:** Evening electricity costs down 50-60% vs. 2024
- **2029:** **Near-24/7 low-cost power availability**, enabling new industrial processes (hydrogen, desalination, data centers operating flexibly)

**For Renewable Developers:**
- **2027-2029:** Solar curtailment becomes routine, **only solar+storage projects economic**
- Standalone solar projects face negative pricing 4-6 hours/day

---

### The Disruption Tipping Point

**Between 100 GW and 300 GW (2027-2029), the US electricity system crosses the disruption threshold:**
- Gas peakers transition from "economically challenged" to "fully stranded"
- Evening peak pricing compressed by **70-80%** from current levels
- **Market structure fundamentally transforms:** From scarcity-priced peaks to abundance-priced commodity power

This is the classic disruption pattern—not gradual decline, but **rapid phase shift** once the disruptor reaches critical scale.
