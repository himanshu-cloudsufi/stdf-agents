The critical measures needed to unburden rooftop solar and batteries in the US fall into four categories:

## 1. **Eliminate Utility-Imposed Grid Connection Barriers**

**Interconnection reform:**
- Mandate standardized, automated interconnection approval (≤30 days for systems <25 kW, ≤90 days for larger)
- Remove utility discretion over connection denials
- Prohibit utilities from requiring expensive grid upgrade studies for standard residential systems
- Pre-approve inverter models to avoid case-by-case engineering reviews

**End discriminatory fees:**
- Ban fixed charges, standby charges, or "grid access fees" that penalize solar owners
- Prohibit minimum bill requirements that nullify solar savings
- Eliminate demand charges on residential solar customers

## 2. **Mandate Fair Compensation for Grid Services**

**Net metering protection:**
- Federal mandate for full retail-rate net metering (or restore state-level mandates where utilities have eroded them)
- Alternatively: Require utilities to compensate rooftop solar at their **avoided cost** (fuel + capacity + transmission/distribution losses), which is typically higher than wholesale rates

**Virtual power plant (VPP) market access:**
- Require utilities to allow third-party aggregators to bundle rooftop batteries for grid services
- Mandate participation in wholesale markets (frequency regulation, demand response, capacity markets)
- Force utilities to pay market rates for services currently monopolized

## 3. **Break Utility Monopoly on Distributed Energy**

**Allow peer-to-peer energy trading:**
- Legalize energy sharing within communities without utility middleman taxation
- Enable microgrid islanding (neighborhoods can disconnect during outages)

**End exclusive service territories for solar:**
- Allow homeowners to choose competitive retail energy providers or community solar programs
- Separate utility ownership from grid operation (regulated grid operator + competitive energy suppliers)

## 4. **Remove Permitting and Code Friction**

**Streamline local permitting:**
- Federal preemption of local zoning restrictions on solar (similar to FCC rules for antennas)
- Standardized national building/electrical codes for solar + storage
- Same-day or online-only permitting for prescreened system designs

**Fire code rationalization:**
- Update NEC (National Electrical Code) rapid shutdown rules to reduce equipment costs
- Eliminate rooftop setback requirements that waste space (not justified by fire safety data)

---

## **Why These Matter: The "God Parity" Threshold**

You've likely reached **God Parity** in sunny US states: rooftop solar + battery can deliver electricity cheaper than **just the transmission and distribution charges** of the utility grid (even if generation were free).

At this point, the utility business model collapses because:
- Customers defect → Remaining customers pay more (death spiral)
- Utilities respond by lobbying for punitive fees to block defection
- The rational economic outcome (distributed SWB) is artificially suppressed by regulatory capture

**The core conflict:** Utilities earn returns on capital investments (poles, wires, power plants). Rooftop solar + batteries make much of this infrastructure obsolete. Utilities will not voluntarily embrace obsolescence—only federal/state intervention can force the transition.

---

## **Political Pathway**

The most effective single measure would be **federal preemption** modeled on telecom deregulation:
- Separate grid operation (regulated natural monopoly) from energy generation/storage (competitive market)
- Mandate open access: anyone can connect distributed energy resources under standardized technical rules
- Utilities become "wires-only" companies with regulated returns, not energy merchants

This mirrors how AT&T's monopoly was broken—forced structural separation between network infrastructure and competitive services.
