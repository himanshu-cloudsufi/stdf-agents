#### Step 3: Wright's Law Cost Curve Acceleration

**Battery pack cost trajectory**:

Wright's Law: \( C_t = C_0 \cdot Q^{-\alpha} \)

Where:
- \( C_t \) = cost at time t
- \( C_0 \) = initial cost
- \( Q \) = cumulative production volume
- \( \alpha \) = experience parameter (learning rate)

**Baseline scenario**:

- Learning rate: 18% per cumulative doubling
- 2029: $85/kWh (modest scale increase)

**Accelerated scenario (with deal)**:

- Learning rate increases to 20-22% (economies of scale, supply chain integration)
- 2029: $65-70/kWh (15-25% cost reduction vs baseline)

$$Cost_{2029, accel} = 88 \times (2.5)^{-0.22} \approx \$68/kWh$$

**Grid storage LCOE calculation** (4-hour lithium-ion):

$$LCOE = \frac{CAPEX \times CRF + Fixed\ O\&M}{Annual\ Throughput} + Variable\ O\&M$$

Where CRF (Capital Recovery Factor):

$$CRF = \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1}$$

Assumptions:
- CAPEX: $68/kWh (accelerated) × 4 hours = $272/kWh
- Fixed O&M: 2.5% of CAPEX
- Cycle life: 5,000 cycles (80% DoD)
- Discount rate: 7%
- Project life: 15 years

$$LCOE_{storage} = \frac{272 \times 0.0979 + 6.8}{4 \times 365 \times 0.8} + 0.5 \approx \$92/MWh$$

**Tipping point**: Gas peaker marginal cost $32-47/MWh (fuel + variable O&M), but full capital recovery needs $60-80/MWh. Grid storage at $92/MWh crosses threshold for new builds by 2028.
