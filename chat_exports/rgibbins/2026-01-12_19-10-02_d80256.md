## Fiscal Response Considerations

### 1. **Structural Reality**
This is **not** cyclical unemployment requiring stimulus. This is **permanent labor displacement** from exponential AI capability growth (7-month doubling time).

### 2. **Timeline Pressure**
- **12-24 month adoption lag** means 2027 capability triggers 2028-2029 corporate deployment at scale
- UK has approximately **18-30 months** to design and implement fiscal architecture

### 3. **Fiscal Policy Options**

#### **Option A: Universal Basic Income (UBI)**
- **Scale Required**: If 30M jobs displaced, need ~£15,000-20,000/person/year = £450-600B annually
- **Funding**: Requires productivity taxation (AI compute tax, corporate automation levy, robot tax)
- **Challenge**: Political feasibility, inflation management, work incentive preservation

#### **Option B: Job Guarantee + Retraining**
- **Scale Required**: Create 10-15M public sector jobs (infrastructure, care, education, research)
- **Cost**: £200-400B annually (wages + program administration)
- **Challenge**: Speed of job creation vs. displacement velocity, skill mismatch

#### **Option C: Sovereign Wealth Fund from AI Productivity**
- **Mechanism**: Tax AI-generated productivity gains, distribute dividends
- **Model**: Alaska Permanent Fund, Norway oil fund applied to AI economic surplus
- **Challenge**: Global tax coordination, capital flight risk

#### **Option D: Reduced Work Week + Income補充**
- **Mechanism**: Mandate 20-30 hour work week, spread remaining work, income support for gap
- **Cost**: £100-200B annually (income补充 + transition support)
- **Challenge**: Productivity maintenance, sector coordination

### 4. **Critical Constraints**

**Adoption Lag Caveat**: Corporate deployment typically lags technical capability by 12-24 months. This provides a **narrow policy window** but does NOT change the structural endpoint.

**Job Creation Omitted**: This model forecasts displacement only. New jobs will emerge (AI trainers, robot maintenance, prompt engineers), but:
- New job volume unlikely to match displacement scale
- Skill requirements create transition barriers
- Geographic/demographic mismatch likely

**Binary Simplification**: Many jobs will experience **partial automation** (human-AI collaboration) rather than full displacement. However, even 50% automation per role = 50% workforce reduction.

## Strategic Implications

### For UK Government:
1. **Immediate**: Commission national AI displacement audit using task-level analysis
2. **2025-2026**: Design fiscal architecture (tax base shift from labor to capital/productivity)
3. **2026-2027**: Pilot UBI or job guarantee in high-displacement regions
4. **2027-2028**: Scale intervention ahead of corporate adoption wave

### Key Risks:
- **Delayed Response**: Waiting for unemployment to spike before acting = political crisis
- **Insufficient Scale**: Incremental programs insufficient for 90%+ automation rate
- **Tax Base Collapse**: Income tax revenue drops as employment drops, requiring new tax paradigm
- **Social Cohesion**: Mass unemployment without income support = instability

### Key Opportunities:
- **Productivity Windfall**: AI-driven productivity surge creates economic surplus to fund redistribution
- **Quality of Life**: Reduced work requirements if income maintained
- **Public Goods**: Freed labor can shift to care, education, research, infrastructure
