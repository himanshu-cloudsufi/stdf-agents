## 1. Transmission Channels: AI Displacement → r* Repricing

The neutral rate r* is the real rate consistent with the economy at potential with stable inflation. AI displacement attacks it through five simultaneous channels:

**Channel 1 — Labor Income Share Collapse**
Displaced workers have a marginal propensity to consume near 1.0; AI system owners near 0.1. The redistribution from labor to capital compresses aggregate demand and reduces the equilibrium real rate required to clear the savings-investment market. Each 5 percentage points of displacement rate reduces r* by approximately 10 basis points (net of productivity offset) [C6].

**Channel 2 — Investment Demand Destruction (Net Negative)**
AI capex surges initially but peaks in 2029 at +£42bn/year, then declines as per-unit AI costs collapse. This is more than offset by the collapse of human capital investment, commercial real estate demand, and consumption-funded business investment. Net investment change reaches **-£118bn/year by 2033** (-4.72% of GDP) [C7]. Lower investment demand at any given real rate forces the equilibrium rate down.

**Channel 3 — Potential Output Ambiguity**
AI raises total factor productivity — the 50/50 displacement/productivity split means measured GDP may rise even as wage income collapses. The MPC faces a novel analytical problem: potential output appears intact while effective demand potential is destroyed. This causes systematic underestimation of the output gap, delaying the easing response by 12–18 months [C8].

**Channel 4 — Inflation Dynamics Inversion**
AI displacement is simultaneously deflationary (wage income destruction → demand collapse) and disinflationary (AI-produced services at near-zero marginal cost). UK CPI will experience persistent undershoot of the 2% target, anchoring inflation expectations below target and compressing the nominal rate required to achieve any given real rate [C9].

**Channel 5 — Risk Premium Expansion**
Structural uncertainty from AI displacement — its speed, breadth, and absence of historical precedent — expands the risk premium embedded in r*. Firms delay investment; households increase precautionary savings. Both effects are contractionary [C10].

## 4. Historical Precedents for Structural Rate Compression

### Japan: Lost Decade (1990s–2000s)

Bank of Japan cut from 6% (1991) to 0.50% (1995) to 0.10% (1999) — 590bp over 8 years. Trigger: asset price deflation (real estate + equity bubble collapse), banking insolvency, corporate balance sheet repair. The deflationary pressure was demand-side; the supply side remained intact. Japan's deflation was a **balance sheet recession** — solvable in principle by fiscal expansion and banking recapitalization. Japan's net output gap peaked at approximately -3% to -5% of GDP [C25].

### Post-GFC UK (2007–2009)

BoE cut from 5.75% to 0.50% in 18 months — 525bp. Trigger: financial sector deleveraging, demand shock. Effective because the underlying productive structure was intact; workers could be reabsorbed into recovering sectors over time. Peak output gap: approximately -6.3% of GDP in 2009 [C26].

1. **Supply-side transformation**: AI does not just destroy demand — it simultaneously creates a new, vastly more productive supply-side. The deflationary pressure is permanent and structural. Prices of AI-produced services fall toward zero marginal cost regardless of monetary policy [C27].

2. **Non-reversibility**: Displaced workers cannot return to previous occupations. The jobs do not exist. Monetary easing cannot recreate demand for human cognitive labor when AI performs those tasks at 1/17,500th the cost [C1].

3. **Speed**: Japan's deflation developed over a decade. The post-GFC shock played out over 18 months. AI displacement at 7-month capability doubling time compresses the entire disruption cycle into 4–6 years (2026–2031) [C28]. The BoE's institutional response speed — quarterly MPC meetings, 18-month transmission lags — is mismatched to the disruption timeline by an order of magnitude.

**Calibration**: The AI displacement net output gap of -9.43% by 2033 [C29] is approximately **double the Japan Lost Decade trigger level** and **1.5× the post-GFC UK peak**. The BoE will be forced to the effective lower bound faster than either precedent — not slower.
