- **Interstate electricity market** (like EU energy union)
- **Federal grid planning authority** (overrides state blocking of transmission)
- **Wholesale market reform**: Capacity markets value reliability, not just energy (rewards batteries for grid stability)

**Impact**:
- Unlocks $200B in stranded transmission investment
- Enables 70-80% SWB penetration by 2035 (vs. 50% under current fragmentation)

**Timing**: Legislation 2028, implementation 2029-2032.

#### **11. Managed Fossil Asset Retirement - "No Bailouts, But Bridge Financing"**

**Recognition**: By 2028-2029, coal/gas plants will be stranded. Utilities will seek bailouts.

**Policy**:

- **But**: Federal financing for conversion
  - Coal site → solar+storage (use existing transmission)
  - Gas plant → battery storage + peaking backup (preserve reliability role)

**Example**: 
- 200 MW coal plant (uneconomic) → retire
- Same site: 800 MW solar + 1.6 GWh storage (uses existing substation)
- Jobs: 150 coal plant workers → 80 O&M for solar+storage + 200 construction jobs

**Timing**: Launch 2028, execute 2029-2032.

## PHASE 4: RISK MANAGEMENT - What Could Go Wrong

### **Scenario Planning**

#### **Risk 1: China Dumps Solar Panels (Predatory Pricing)**

**Probability**: High (60%)

**Impact**: Could undercut USA manufacturing before it scales

**Mitigation**:
- **Tariffs**: 25-40% on below-cost Chinese solar (WTO-compliant if proven dumping)
- **Buy American provision**: Federal/state procurement requires domestic content
- **Timing**: Implement tariffs Q2 2026 (before factories break ground)

---

#### **Risk 2: Grid Instability During Transition**

**Probability**: Medium (40%)

**Impact**: Blackouts blamed on "renewables" (even if caused by fossil plant retirements)

**Mitigation**:
- **Overcapacity mandate**: Require 150% SWB capacity vs. peak demand (ensures redundancy)
- **Strategic coal reserve**: Keep 10-15 GW coal plants on paid standby 2026-2029 (insurance)
- **Battery storage targets**: 50 GW by 2028, 100 GW by 2030 (grid stability)

---

#### **Risk 3: Oil/Gas Industry Collapse (Uncontrolled)**

**Probability**: Medium-High (50%)

**Impact**: If oil falls to $40-45/barrel, 30-40% of shale producers bankrupt, 200,000+ job losses

**Mitigation**:
- **SPR buybacks**: Support price floor at $50-55/barrel via strategic reserve replenishment
- **Export optimization**: LNG growth provides revenue cushion for gas producers
- **Transition timeline**: 2026-2032 (6-year glide path allows industry adjustment)

---

#### **Risk 4: Nuclear Overoptimism**

**Probability**: High (70%)

**Impact**: SMRs delayed to 2033+, restarts face regulatory/cost issues

**Mitigation**:
- **Don't overpromise**: Frame nuclear as 10-15% solution, not 30-40%
- **Hedge with SWB**: Primary strategy is solar/wind/battery (proven, scalable)
- **Nuclear as complement**: Baseload + hydrogen production (longer-term)
