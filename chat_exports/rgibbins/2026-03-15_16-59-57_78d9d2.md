**Headwinds (amplifying displacement impact):**

**Trade exposure:** UK services exports — financial services, professional services, IT consulting — face AI-driven margin compression in global markets. The UK's services trade surplus is structurally at risk [W2].

**Demographic concentration:** The UK working-age population is aging, with a high concentration of workers aged 45–64 in precisely the occupational categories most exposed: Administrative, Healthcare support, Financial services. **Productivity surplus:** The 50/50 replacement/productivity split means each displaced FTE generates an equivalent productivity gain. ### Section 6: The Unemployment Rate as a Lagging Indicator

The 19.10% headline rate by Q4 2028 is a conservative floor, not a ceiling, for three structural reasons [C1]:

3. **Reabsorption rate compression:** The 20% reabsorption assumption is calibrated for the early disruption phase. As displacement accelerates in 2027–2028, the absorptive capacity of new AI-adjacent roles is overwhelmed by volume. The effective reabsorption rate falls below 20% in later quarters — meaning net displacement figures are a floor.
