China lead demand peaked in 2024 at 5,762 kt and will decline 54% to 2,657 kt by 2036, driven by the collapse of automotive starter battery demand as electric vehicles displace internal combustion engines [C1].

## Peak and Decline Trajectory

China's lead demand reached its maximum in 2024 and enters immediate, sustained decline through 2036 [C1]. The 3,105 kt reduction (-54%) occurs in two phases: an accelerated 49% drop from 2024 to 2030 as EV penetration surges from 35% to 73%, followed by a slower 10% decline from 2030 to 2036 as penetration approaches 91% [C1].

## Automotive Segment Collapse

The automotive starter battery segment—which represented 50% (2,881 kt) of China's lead demand in 2024—collapses 94% to just 180 kt by 2036 [C1]. This segment drives the overall demand destruction. Each percentage point of EV penetration eliminates demand for lead-acid starter batteries in newly sold vehicles and reduces replacement battery demand as the ICE fleet shrinks. By 2030, automotive demand falls to 648 kt; by 2036, it becomes marginal at 6.8% of total demand [C1].

## Industrial and Non-Battery Segments

Industrial batteries (stationary backup power, telecom, data centers, and motive power applications) grow modestly from 1,363 kt (2024) to 1,729 kt (2036), a 27% increase [C1]. Stationary applications expand due to grid infrastructure and data center growth, offsetting lithium-ion displacement in forklifts and material handling equipment. Non-battery uses (construction, radiation shielding, chemicals) decline slowly from 795 kt to 748 kt (-6%) [C1]. By 2036, industrial batteries dominate at 65% of total demand, with non-battery uses at 28% [C1].

## Cost-Curve Dynamics

Lithium-ion batteries achieved cost parity with lead-acid in automotive applications by 2024 (China pack prices: $84/kWh) and now offer superior energy density, cycle life, and weight advantages [C2]. Lead-acid retains cost advantages only in stationary backup applications where weight is irrelevant and deep-cycle performance is not required. The automotive displacement is economically irreversible: even if lead-acid prices fell further, the 10x performance gap (energy density, lifespan, charging speed) in transportation applications cannot be closed [C2].

## Methodology and Scope

Analysis uses global lead demand datasets segmented by application (automotive sales and replacement, two/three-wheelers, industrial motive and stationary, non-battery) and applies China's estimated 47% share of global lead consumption [C1]. Automotive projections incorporate China's documented EV penetration trajectory (35% in 2024, accelerating to 91% by 2036) and model both new vehicle displacement and replacement battery demand decline as the ICE fleet contracts [C1][C2]. Industrial demand assumes 2% annual growth reflecting stationary application expansion offset by lithium-ion substitution in motive power. Non-battery uses decline 0.5% annually. The analysis excludes regulatory lag; China's policy environment actively accelerates EV adoption, making demand destruction faster than cost curves alone would dictate.

**Peak year: 2024. Magnitude of decline: 54% (3,105 kt) by 2036. Primary driver: 94% collapse in automotive starter battery demand due to electric vehicle displacement.**
