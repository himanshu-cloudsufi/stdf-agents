- **NEPA Reviews**: Large solar/wind projects require 2-4 year environmental impact studies

**3. Transmission Constraints (800 GW Shortfall)**
- **Need**: 1,000 GW of new long-distance HVDC lines by 2035 to connect renewable-rich regions (Texas, Great Plains) to demand centers (East Coast, California)
- **Current Pace**: 20-30 GW/year of new transmission (80% shortfall vs need)
- **Siting Challenges**: Interstate transmission requires federal/state coordination, often takes 8-12 years
- **Example**: Plains & Eastern Clean Line (Oklahoma-Tennessee, 4 GW HVDC) canceled after 7 years of permitting failures

**4. Grid Reliability Mandates (ISOs Require Dispatchable Capacity)**

- **Resource Adequacy**: California requires 15% planning reserve margin, heavily favoring dispatchable resources

- **Political Economy**: Utilities lobby for capacity payments to existing gas plants, delaying retirements
